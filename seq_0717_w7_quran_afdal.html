Faṣl 3 · The Order of Excellence

Reciting the Qurʾān Is Above Dhikr

قِرَاءَةُ القُرْآنِ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الذِّكْرِ، وَالذِّكْرُ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الدُّعَاءِ، هَذَا مِنْ حَيْثُ النَّظَرُ إِلَى كُلٍّ مِنْهَا مُجَرَّدًا
“Reciting the Qurʾān is more excellent than dhikr, and dhikr is more excellent than supplication — this when each is considered on its own, in the abstract.”
Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

He adds why the Qurʾān stands highest: it is itself among the dhikr of Allah — “وَمَنْ أَعْرَضَ عَن ذِكْرِي” (Q 20:124), where “My remembrance” is His speech revealed to His Messenger. “The excellence of the Qurʾān over all other speech is as the excellence of Allah over His creation.” But — he says at once — “for every station there is a fitting word.”

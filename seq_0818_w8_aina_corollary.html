Faṣl 9 · Why This Seals the Set

“Help Me to Remember You” — the دُعَاء for the دُعَاء

Ibn al-Qayyim notes this same دُعَاء in a second hadith, from al-Ḥākim: the Prophet ﷺ asked, أَتُحِبُّونَ أَنْ تَجْتَهِدُوا فِي الدُّعَاءِ؟ — “would you like to strive your utmost in supplication?” — and when they said yes, he gave them this. So it is, by his own framing, the summit of دُعَاء. It is the request for the very thing the whole وِرْد is made of: the aid to remember, the aid to thank, the aid to worship well.

A corollary

This is the answer to the fear every honest student has on the last night of a course: “I will keep this for a month, then drift.” The مَتْن's reply is not “try harder by yourself.” It is to make the first thing you beg after every prayer the help to keep remembering — because the servant cannot sustain ذِكْر on his own strength. You do not guard the وِرْد; you ask, five times a day, for the One who turns the heart to guard it for you.

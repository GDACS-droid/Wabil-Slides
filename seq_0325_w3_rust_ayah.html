Ṣadaʾ al-Qalb

The Greatest Punishment of the Heart

When the rust thickens and the heart blackens, the covering (الرَّان) settles over it; its perception is ruined, so it no longer accepts truth nor rejects falsehood. “This,” Ibn al-Qayyim says, “is أَعْظَمُ عُقُوبَاتِ القَلْبِ — the greatest punishment of the heart.” Its root is the two from before — heedlessness and following desire; these two put out the heart's light and blind its sight.
وَلَا تُطِعْ مَنْ أَغْفَلْنَا قَلْبَهُ عَن ذِكْرِنَا وَاتَّبَعَ هَوَاهُ وَكَانَ أَمْرُهُ فُرُطًا
“And do not obey one whose heart We have made heedless of Our remembrance, who follows his own desire, and whose affair is ever in excess.”
Sūrat al-Kahf 18:28

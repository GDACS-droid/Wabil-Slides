The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 3

The Supplication That Was Never Made in Vain

لَّا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
“There is no god but You; glory be to You; truly I have been among the wrongdoers.”
Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ 21:87

The Prophet ﷺ said: “The supplication of my brother Dhū-l-Nūn, when he called upon Allah from inside the whale — no Muslim ever calls his Lord by it in anything but that Allah answers him” (al-Tirmidhī). Notice its shape: it opens not with a request but with tahlīl (lā ilāha illā anta) and tasbīḥ (subḥānak), and only then the confession. There is no explicit “save me” at all — the asking is folded so completely inside the praise that the praise is the asking.

A corollary

Take this as a template for the worst nights. When you are so crushed you do not even know what to ask for, you are not left wordless: declare His oneness, exalt Him above any wrong on His part, own your own — and the duʿāʾ of the man in the belly of the whale, in the belly of the night, is already complete on your tongue.

Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

“Its Planting Is سُبْحَانَ اللَّه…”

إِنَّ الجَنَّةَ طَيِّبَةُ التُّرْبَةِ، عَذْبَةُ المَاءِ، وَأَنَّهَا قِيعَانٌ، وَأَنَّ غِرَاسَهَا: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ
“The Garden is sweet of soil and fresh of water, and it is a level plain. Its planting is: Subḥāna llāh, wa-l-ḥamdu li-llāh, wa-lā ilāha illā llāh, wa-llāhu akbar.”
Marfūʿ, from the report of Ibrāhīm al-Khalīl — recorded by al-Tirmidhī
A corollary

Hold the two images together as Ibn al-Qayyim does: the building rises while the mason works and pauses when he rests (“when the rememberer stops, the angels stop”), and the orchard is sown word by word. Both make the Garden something you are raising now, not only awaiting. The plain reading for the week ahead: an idle hour is not neutral. It is a stretch of the wall left unbuilt and a row of ground left unplanted. The level plain stays level exactly where you fell silent.

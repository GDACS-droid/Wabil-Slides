It Is the Food of the Heart and the Soul

It is the nourishment (qūt) of the heart and the spirit

إِنَّهُ قُوتُ الْقَلْبِ وَالرُّوحِ. If Benefit 16 is water, this is bread: deprive the heart of dhikr, Ibn al-Qayyim says, and it is like a body kept from its food. It weakens, sickens, and fails. Then he gives the living proof from his own teacher. He once saw Ibn Taymiyya pray Fajr and sit remembering Allah until nearly midday; turning to him, the Shaykh said: “This is my morning meal — were I not to take this breakfast, my strength would fall.” And once: “I never leave dhikr except intending to give my soul rest, so that by that rest I may ready myself for another remembrance.” For such a man even the pause from dhikr was in service of more dhikr.

هَذِهِ غَدْوَتِي، وَلَوْ لَمْ أَتَغَدَّ هَذَا الْغَدَاءَ لَسَقَطَتْ قُوَّتِي
A corollary

We do not skip meals and call it discipline; we know the body fails. Ibn al-Qayyim is asking us to take the heart’s hunger that seriously. A heart that goes a whole day without a single deliberate remembrance has, by his measure, simply gone unfed; its listlessness by nightfall is no mystery.

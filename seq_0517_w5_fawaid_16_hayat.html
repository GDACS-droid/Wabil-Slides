It Gives Life to the Heart

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
16
100

It bequeaths the very life of the heart

This is the heart of the book. Ibn al-Qayyim writes: “I heard Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyya — may Allah sanctify his soul — say: dhikr is to the heart as water is to the fish; so what is the state of the fish when it is parted from the water?” The heart does not merely benefit from remembrance; it lives by it, as a fish lives by water, and torn from it the fish gasps and dies. This is why the ḥadīth of the living and the dead frames everything: heedlessness is not a small lapse but the heart out of its element.

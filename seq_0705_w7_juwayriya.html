The Ḥadīth of Juwayriya

Four Words That Outweigh a Morning of Dhikr

The Prophet ﷺ returns to Juwayriya

The Prophet ﷺ left Juwayriya (may Allah be pleased with her) in the morning sitting in her place of prayer, then returned near midday to find her still seated. He asked: “Have you remained in the state I left you?” She said yes. He ﷺ said: “I have said, after leaving you, four words three times — if they were weighed against all you have said since this morning, they would outweigh them: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ رِضَا نَفْسِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ زِنَةَ عَرْشِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ مِدَادَ كَلِمَاتِهِ — Glory be to Allah as much as the number of His creation, Glory be to Allah to the contentment of His Self, Glory be to Allah by the weight of His Throne, Glory be to Allah by the ink of His words.”

Recorded by Muslim. Ibn al-Qayyim's lesson: a few words that bind the tasbīḥ to the infinity of what is praised outweigh a long morning of unmeasured remembrance. The quality of dhikr is in how fully it grasps the greatness of the One remembered.

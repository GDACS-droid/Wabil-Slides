Faṣl 74 · al-Taslīm li-l-Qaḍāʾ

Neither Abandoning the Means nor Leaning on Them

Reliance, held in one hadith: the Prophet ﷺ commands the servant first to take the means he cannot do without (“be keen, do not be weak”), and only when the decree overtakes him to surrender without the corrosive “if.” Tawakkul is neither abandoning the means nor leaning on them — it is doing your full part as if everything depended on it, then resting the outcome with Allah as one who knows nothing depended on it but Him.

A corollary

Notice where the hadith says the shayṭān actually gets in: not before the loss, but after it, through the word “if.” If only I had taken the other job; if only we had seen the doctor sooner; if only I had not sent that message. The Prophet ﷺ does not forbid you to grieve a real loss — he forbids you to keep gnawing at a past that the decree has already closed, because that “if” تَفْتَحُ عَمَلَ الشَّيْطَان, opens the door to his work: resentment, despair, and quarrel with the qadar. So once you have done your part and the outcome is fixed, the tongue's medicine is to drop the “if” entirely and say قَدَرُ اللَّهِ وَمَا شَاءَ فَعَلَ — Allah decreed, and what He willed He did — and shut that door before it opens.

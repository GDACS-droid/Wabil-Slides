Fawāʾid al-Dhikr · A Cluster

More Fruits to Carry Together

  • 48 — Dhikr is the root of friendship with Allah. The servant keeps remembering until he loves Him and takes Him as a friend; the heedless keeps forgetting until enmity grows.
  • 49 — It draws down blessings and wards off afflictions. “Nothing brings Allah's favours and repels His punishments like the remembrance of Allah.”
  • 53 — Allah boasts of the rememberers to His angels — as in Muslim's report of the Prophet ﷺ coming upon a circle remembering Allah.
  • 54 — The constant rememberer enters the Garden laughing (Abū al-Dardāʾ).
  • 55 — Every act of worship was legislated to establish dhikr — وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي.
  • 56 — The best of any people in a deed are those who remember Allah most in it — the best of fasters, the best of pilgrims, “the rememberers carried off all the good.”

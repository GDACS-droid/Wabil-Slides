Faḍl al-Dhikr

“Keep Your Tongue Moist with His Remembrance”

The man who could not keep up

A man came and said: “Messenger of Allah, the gateways of good are many, and I cannot keep up with all of them — the laws of Islam have grown many upon me, and I have grown old. So tell me one thing I can hold fast to, and do not make it too much for me, lest I forget.”

He ﷺ answered: لَا يَزَالُ لِسَانُكَ رَطْبًا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى — “Let your tongue never cease to be moist with the remembrance of Allah.”

One thing to hold

From ʿAbd Allāh b. Busr; recorded by al-Tirmidhī, who graded it ḥasan gharīb, and declared ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim. When the whole dīn feels too vast to carry, this is the single rope the Prophet ﷺ hands the weak: a tongue kept wet with His name.

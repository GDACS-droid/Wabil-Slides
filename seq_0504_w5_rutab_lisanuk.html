Faḍl al-Dhikr

“Keep Your Tongue Moist with His Remembrance”

The man who could not keep up

A man came and said: “Messenger of Allah, the gateways of good are many, and I cannot keep up with all of them — the laws of Islam have grown many upon me, and I have grown old. So tell me one thing I can hold fast to, and do not make it too much for me, lest I forget.”

He ﷺ answered: لَا يَزَالُ لِسَانُكَ رَطْبًا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى — “Let your tongue never cease to be moist with the remembrance of Allah.”

From ʿAbd Allāh b. Busr; recorded by al-Tirmidhī, who graded it ḥasan gharīb, and declared ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim.
A corollary

Notice what the Prophet ﷺ did not prescribe: not a long litany, not a fixed number, nothing the old man would forget. The single rope handed to the weak is a tongue kept wet — a remembrance so light and so constant that it never fully dries between tasks. So when the whole dīn feels too vast to carry, the answer is not to do more, but to never go fully silent: a سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ at the sink, a الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ on the stairs.

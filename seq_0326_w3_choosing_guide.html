Ṣadaʾ al-Qalb

Whom Will You Take as a Guide?

From this āyah Ibn al-Qayyim draws a working test for choosing whom to follow. Allah forbade obeying the one who combines three traits: a heart made heedless of His remembrance, the following of desire, and فُرُط — an affair gone to waste (the salaf glossed it as neglect, excess, ruin, and opposition to the truth).

Apply the three to your exemplar

Look hard at the one you would take as a shaykh or guide: Is he of the people of dhikr, or of the heedless? Does desire govern him, or revelation? Is his affair disciplined, or neglected and adrift? If desire rules, he is of the heedless, and his affair is in waste — do not follow him; فَإِنَّهُ يَقُودُكَ إِلَى الهَلَاكِ, he leads only to ruin. But if Allah's remembrance and the Sunna prevail in him and he is resolute, فَلْيَتَمَسَّكْ بِغَرْزِهِ — hold fast to him.

Today

Run the same three traits over the voices a person now lets shape him — not only the teacher he sits with, but the streams of talk he absorbs online. Does this voice draw me toward the remembrance of Allah, or only feed an appetite? Is it governed by what Allah revealed, or by what performs well and pleases a crowd? Ibn al-Qayyim's filter is as exact for a feed as for a shaykh.

The Muqaddima

The Whole Matter in One Definition

التَّوْفِيقُ: أَنْ لَا يَكِلَكَ اللهُ إِلَى نَفْسِكَ، وَالخِذْلَانُ: أَنْ يَكِلَكَ اللهُ إِلَى نَفْسِكَ
“Success (tawfīq) is that Allah does not leave you to yourself; abandonment (khidhlān) is that He leaves you to yourself.”
at-tawfīqu: an lā yakilaka llāhu ilā nafsik, wa-l-khidhlānu: an yakilaka llāhu ilā nafsik
Ibn al-Qayyim, citing the agreement of the ʿārifīn

Why this reframes the two cases

The author says العَارِفُونَ كُلُّهُمْ مُجْمِعُونَ on this — the people who know Allah are agreed on it. Now both prior cases read clearly: when Allah wills a servant good, He does not leave him to his own self after the sin — He opens for him a door of regret and return. When He wills otherwise, خَلَّاهُ وَعُجْبَهُ وَكِبْرَهُ — He leaves him to his self-conceit, “and that is the very abandonment that brings his ruin.” So the real danger is being left alone with the self that slipped, not the slip itself.

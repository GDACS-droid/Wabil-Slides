Ḥadīth al-Ḥārith al-Ashʿarī

Five Words for the Children of Israel

Allah, says Ibn al-Qayyim, told the slave plainly how to guard himself from his enemy and ransom himself when taken — and gathered it into one long ḥadīth of five parables. Learn it whole.

The setting

Allah commanded Yaḥyā b. Zakariyyā عليه السلام with five words, to act on them and command the Children of Israel to act on them. Yaḥyā was slow to convey them, until ʿĪsā عليه السلام urged him: Either you command them, or I will. Yaḥyā said, I fear that if you precede me with them I will be sunk into the earth or punished. So he gathered the people in Bayt al-Maqdis until the masjid was full and they sat on the balconies, and he said: “Allah has commanded me with five words, to act on them and command you to act on them.”

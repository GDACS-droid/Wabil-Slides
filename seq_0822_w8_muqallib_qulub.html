The Heart Between Two Fingers

“O Turner of Hearts, Keep My Heart Firm”

مَا مِنْ قَلْبٍ إِلَّا بَيْنَ إِصْبَعَيْنِ مِنْ أَصَابِعِ الرَّحْمَنِ، إِنْ شَاءَ أَقَامَهُ وَإِنْ شَاءَ أَزَاغَهُ… يَا مُقَلِّبَ القُلُوبِ ثَبِّتْ قَلْبِي عَلَى دِينِكَ
“There is no heart but it is between two of the fingers of al-Raḥmān: if He wills He keeps it upright, and if He wills He turns it aside.” And the Prophet ﷺ would say: O Turner of hearts, keep my heart firm upon Your religion.
al-Nawwās b. Samʿān — ḥadīth ṣaḥīḥ, Aḥmad & al-Ḥākim

The perfect protective duʿāʾ to close eight weeks on the life of the heart. The One who gives the heart life is the same One who turns it, so its constancy is never something we own; it is a gift renewed. Against the shayṭān the book gave us the words of refuge; against our own inconstancy it gives us this. The heart that prayed sincerely this morning is not guaranteed to itself by tonight.

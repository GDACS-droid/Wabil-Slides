Why Praise-Then-Need Is Nearer to an Answer

Ibn al-Qayyim draws a likeness from how people ask one another — “and Allah's is the highest likeness” (وَلِلَّهِ المَثَلُ الأَعْلَى):

When a man comes to someone whose favour he hopes for, and first praises his generosity and bounty, and then mentions his own need and poverty — that is more moving to the heart of the one asked, and nearer to having the need met, than to open by saying “give me such and such.” When the servant joins the two — praising his Lord by His perfection, and laying bare his own need before Him — “such is the etiquette of servanthood.”

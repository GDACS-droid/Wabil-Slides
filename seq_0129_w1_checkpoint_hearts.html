Group breakout
Ibn al-Qayyim says two believers can do the same act and walk away with very different weights, and that a sin can lift a man while a virtue sinks him.
  • Name one act you do on autopilot — وُضُوء, a daily صَلَاة, a routine دُعَاء. What, concretely, is the difference between doing it as a “dead body” and doing it with خُشُوع? What would change tomorrow morning?
  • The السَّلَف said a sin can take a man to Paradise through brokenness, and a good deed to the Fire through عُجْب. Have you seen this — in a heart softened by a fall, or hardened by its own good record?
  • “No veil is thicker than الدَّعْوَى — the claim.” Where does the quiet claim “I have arrived” hide in a practicing person's life?
Choose one answer to bring back to the room.

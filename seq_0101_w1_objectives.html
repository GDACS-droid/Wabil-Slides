Orientation

Where we're going tonight

  • Meet the author: Ibn Qayyim al-Jawziyya (691–751H / 1292–1350, Damascus) — his name, his sixteen years with his shaykh, his nights of tahajjud, and his standing as a master of tazkiyah.
  • Place the book among his great works (Madārij al-Sālikīn, Zād al-Maʿād, Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn) and read its title out of the Qurʾān (Q 35:10; Q 14:24).
  • Work through the author's own opening — شُكْر · صَبْر · اسْتِغْفَار — and see exactly what each demands of a believer in ease, hardship, and sin.
  • Learn the muqaddima's hardest teachings: ʿubūdiyya in what you hate, the sin that takes a man to Paradise, and the two pillars of an upright heart.
  • Settle how we, in our tradition, read a Ḥanbalī master of the heart — faithfully, warmly, and without manufacturing quarrel.

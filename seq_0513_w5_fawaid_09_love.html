It Bequeaths the Love That Is the Soul of Islam

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
9
100

It bequeaths the love (al-maḥabba) — the spirit of the dīn

Ibn al-Qayyim calls love the soul of Islam, the axle on which the whole dīn turns, the pivot of felicity and salvation. Then he gives the means with a craftsman’s precision: وَجَعَلَ سَبَبَ الْمَحَبَّةِ دَوَامَ الذِّكْرِ — Allah has appointed a cause for everything, and the cause He appointed for love is the constancy of dhikr. So love is not summoned by wishing for it; it is grown by a settled habit. Just as al-mudhākara — steady study and review — is the door to knowledge, dhikr is the door to love: its widest road and its straightest path. “Whoever wishes to attain the love of Allah, let his tongue cleave to His remembrance.”

فَمَنْ أَرَادَ أَنْ يَنَالَ مَحَبَّةَ اللَّهِ فَلْيَلْهَجْ بِذِكْرِهِ
A corollary

This reframes the complaint “I don’t feel close to Allah.” Love is a fruit with a known cause — so the answer is never to wait for the feeling, but to plant the cause and tend it. The one who says His name through the day, on the days he feels nothing, is laying the very road on which the love will later arrive.

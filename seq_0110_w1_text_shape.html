The Text

A Letter on the Heart and Its Dhikr

  • It began as a letter to a friend — intimate and urgent, written to be acted on.
  • Its spine is the heart: when it is sound, the limbs are sound; when it is corrupt, so is everything built on it.
  • Its center of gravity is dhikr — its merits, its many forms, and the fawāʾid (benefits) it brings, which we will count one by one in later weeks.
  • It ends with the prophetic adhkār themselves — a working wird for morning and evening that this course will close on.

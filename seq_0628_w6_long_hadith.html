Benefit 73 · The Long Ḥadīth

“I Saw a Man of My أُمَّة… and His Deed Came to Him”

The vision Ibn al-Qayyim builds the last benefit on

The Prophet ﷺ came out one morning and said: “Last night I saw a wonder. I saw a man of my أُمَّة whom the angel of death came to seize — and his kindness to his parents came and turned the angel back. I saw a man over whom the punishment of the grave was spread — and his ablution came and rescued him. I saw a man whom the شَيَاطِين had surrounded — and the remembrance of Allah came and drove the شَيْطَان from him.” He went on: a man pressing toward the gathering of the prophets, turned away each time, until his غُسْل from جَنَابَة came and seated him beside the Prophet ﷺ; a man in darkness before and behind, right and left, above and below, bewildered — until his حَجّ and عُمْرَة came and drew him out into the light; a man fending the blaze of the Fire from his face with his hand — until his charity came and became a screen between him and it; a man at the gates of the Garden, the doors shut against him — “until his witness that there is no god but Allah came, and the doors were opened, and it brought him in.”

Why it crowns the list

Ibn al-Qayyim cites it for its central line — and the remembrance of Allah came and drove the شَيْطَان from him — set beside the ḥadīth of al-Ḥārith. Its chains are weak (the editor lays them out), yet Ibn Taymiyya is reported to have said شَوَاهِدُ الصِّحَّةِ عَلَيْه, “the marks of soundness are upon it,” and Ibn al-Qayyim presents it for its meaning: on the day a man stands alone, his deeds arrive as persons to plead for him. The deed that drives off the enemy at the last is his remembrance of Allah.

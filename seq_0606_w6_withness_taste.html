Benefit 42 · A Caution from the Text

Seek the Nearness — Keep the Distinction

Ibn al-Qayyim says this nearness is مَعِيَّةٌ لَا يُشْبِهُهَا شَيْء — a withness nothing resembles, known only by ذَوْق (immediate taste). Then he warns: it is مَزِلَّةُ أَقْدَام — a place where feet slip.

The exact danger he names

His caution is one we teach gladly, because it is our own ground. The taste of nearness is real and to be sought. But the moment a servant lets the سُلْطَان of dhikr so overpower him that he is absent from himself and his own remembering, and loses the distinction between the Eternal and the originated, between Lord and servant, Creator and created, at that point he writes, “the door of ḥulūl and ittiḥād is forced open, inevitably.” The safeguard is not to flee the nearness but to carry beneath it عَقِيدَةٌ صَحِيحَة: the Creator is never the creature, and tanzīh is never surrendered to ecstasy. Pursue the ذَوْق; keep the creed under it.

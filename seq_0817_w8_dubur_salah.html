Faṣl 9 · The Words After the Prayer

“Help Me to Remember You, Thank You, and Worship You Well”

يَا مُعَاذُ، وَاللَّهِ إِنِّي لَأُحِبُّكَ، لَا تَدَعَنَّ فِي دُبُرِ كُلِّ صَلَاةٍ أَنْ تَقُولَ: اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ
“O Muʿādh — by Allah, I love you. Do not fail, at the end of every prayer, to say: O Allah, help me to remember You, to thank You, and to worship You well.”
Muʿādh b. Jabal — recorded by Aḥmad, Abū Dāwūd & al-Nasāʾī, ṣaḥīḥ

This is the perfect seal for a wird, because it asks for the wird itself: the help to keep remembering. Ibn al-Qayyim places it among the appointed adhkār precisely because the servant cannot sustain dhikr by his own strength — so the first thing he begs, every prayer, is the aid to remember.

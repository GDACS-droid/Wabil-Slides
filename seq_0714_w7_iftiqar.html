The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 4

Add to His Praise Your Own Brokenness

The supplication preceded by dhikr and praise is nearer to being answered than bare asking. And there is one thing more: when the servant adds to it an honest report of his own state — his need, his poverty, his lowliness — it is more complete still.

What the servant brings

  • Praise of the One asked, by His perfection, kindness and bounty — the appeal (مُقْتَضٍ) from the Lord's side.
  • An open confession of his own need, poverty and brokenness — the appeal (مُقْتَضٍ) from the servant's side.

Three masters of the form

  • Mūsā: رَبِّ إِنِّي لِمَا أَنزَلْتَ إِلَيَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ (Q 28:24).
  • Dhū-l-Nūn: سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ (Q 21:87).
  • Ādam: رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَا أَنفُسَنَا (Q 7:23).

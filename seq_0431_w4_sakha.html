صَدَقَة & سَخَاء

Whoever Is Guarded from His Own شُحّ

وَمَن يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ
“And whoever is guarded against the greed of his own soul — it is they who are the successful.”
Sūrat al-Ḥashr 59:9 (also al-Taghābun 64:16)

Two ranks of generosity

ʿAbd al-Raḥmān b. ʿAwf would circle the Kaʿba with one prayer only: “My Lord, guard me from the greed of my soul” — for, he said, when I am guarded from it I have succeeded: I do not steal, nor commit indecency, nor betray. Ibn al-Qayyim ranks سَخَاء in two: the higher and nobler is to be indifferent to what is in others' hands (سَخَاؤُكَ عَمَّا بِيَدِ غَيْرِكَ); the second is to give freely of what is in your own. A man may be among the most generous of people though he gives nothing — because he has let go of wanting what others hold. The generous one is near to Allah, near to people, near to the Garden, far from the Fire; the miser is far from all of these.

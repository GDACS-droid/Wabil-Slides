Ḥadīth al-Ḥārith al-Ashʿarī

The Five Parables

“I command you with five…”

One — Tawḥīd. That you worship Allah and associate nothing with Him. The likeness of one who associates partners with Allah is a man who buys a slave from his own pure wealth and says, “This is my house and my work; work and render to me” — but the slave works and renders to someone other than his master. Which of you would be pleased that his slave were like that?

Two — the prayer. When you pray, do not turn away; for Allah sets His Face toward the face of His slave in his prayer so long as he does not turn aside.

Three — fasting. Its likeness is a man in a company who has with him a pouch of musk; every one of them delights in its scent — and the breath of the faster is sweeter with Allah than the scent of musk.

Four — charity. Its likeness is a man taken captive by the enemy, his hand bound to his neck, brought forward to have his neck struck; he says, “I will ransom myself from you with little and much,” and so ransoms himself from them.

Five — the remembrance of Allah. Its likeness is a man whose enemy comes swiftly on his trail, until he reaches a fortified stronghold and saves himself from them. So too the slave: he does not guard himself from shayṭān except by the dhikr of Allah.

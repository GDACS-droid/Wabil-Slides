The آدَاب of دُعَاء · 1

“This Man Has Hurried” — Praise, Then صَلَاة, Then Ask

The حَدِيث of Faḍāla ibn ʿUbayd

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ heard a man supplicate in his prayer without praising Allah or sending صَلَاة upon the Prophet. He ﷺ said: “This one has hurried.” Then he called him and said: “إِذَا صَلَّى أَحَدُكُمْ فَلْيَبْدَأْ بِتَحْمِيدِ رَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ وَالثَّنَاءِ عَلَيْهِ، ثُمَّ يُصَلِّي عَلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، ثُمَّ يَدْعُو بَعْدُ بِمَا شَاءَ — When one of you prays, let him begin by praising his Lord and extolling Him, then send صَلَاة upon the Prophet ﷺ, then ask after that for whatever he wishes.” (Abū Dāwūd, al-Tirmidhī — ḥasan ṣaḥīḥ; ṣaḥḥaḥahu Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim.)

This is the master-pattern, and its why is plain: praise, then صَلَاة on the Prophet ﷺ, then the request. A صَلَاة upon him ﷺ is never turned back, and the scholars said a دُعَاء placed between two accepted صَلَوَات is not left suspended between them. So the praise and the صَلَاة are not a preamble to get through. They are what carries the request up.

In practice

“Hurrying” today rarely looks like rudeness; it looks like a believer who, the instant the إِمَام says the سَلَام, plunges straight into “يَا رَبّ, the rent, the result, the marriage…” The أَدَب is to take ten unhurried seconds first — الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ… اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ — and only then bring the need. The need loses nothing by waiting; it gains a doorway.

Where we're going

Ibn al-Qayyim turns from the heart's life to its sicknesses: how the enemy gets in, what sin leaves behind, and how the same act can raise one servant and ruin another. The thread under all of it — sins are diseases of the heart, and dhikr is its medicine.
  • How the Shayṭān reaches the believer — never head-on, only by ambush through the open door of الغَفْلَة (heedlessness), and through appetite and anger.
  • What sin does to the heart: how brokenness over a sin can outweigh many obediences, and how self-satisfaction over a good deed can ruin it — the hinge of tawfīq and khidhlān.
  • Why two people pray one prayer and are separated by what is between heaven and earth: deeds weigh by what the heart carries — faith, sincerity, love.
  • The nullifiers of deeds — riyāʾ, المَنّ, deeds undone while the doer feels nothing — and the hard case of the repentant show-off.
  • The straight path between two pits the enemy is content to use either way: lax license and anxious, doubting strictness.
  • Where the cure begins: the heart rusts by two things and is polished by two — this opens the door to next week's dhikr.

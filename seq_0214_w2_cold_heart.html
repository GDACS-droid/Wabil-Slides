ʿAlāmāt al-Taʿẓīm

Unmoved by the Loss

Many scholars said: he has no prayer

Ibn al-Qayyim reports that many of the ʿulamāʾ said of the man whose heart is cold, empty of any sense of this loss, unalarmed by it: لَا صَلَاةَ لَهُ — and that this coldness “is from the absence of magnifying the command of Allah in his heart.”

  • Missing the first of the time, which is the pleasure of Allah.
  • Missing the first row, upon whose right Allah and His angels send blessings — “if a servant knew its merit he would compete for it, and it would come to drawing lots.”
  • Missing the larger gathering: the more the people, the more beloved to Allah; and the farther the steps, “a step that wipes out a sin and another that raises a degree.”

Counting the Loss Honestly

The merchant's grief

Ibn al-Qayyim draws the picture: a person grieves at missing the congregation, knowing that had his prayer alone been accepted from him he would still have lost twenty-seven times its reward. Then he turns it over: if a merchant — at home, with no travel and no hardship — lost twenty-seven dīnārs on a single deal, “he would bite his hands in regret and sorrow.” And how can the two compare, when every single fold by which the congregation multiplies the prayer is better than a thousand, and a thousand thousand, and whatever Allah wills? When the servant lets that profit slip, “he has lost, for certain.”

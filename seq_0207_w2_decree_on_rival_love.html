Foundation One

A Decree That Is Not Turned Back

He raises it from a way of dealing to a decree: وَقَدْ قَضَى اللَّهُ قَضَاءً لَا يُرَدُّ وَلَا يُدْفَع — “Allah has decreed a decree that is neither repelled nor pushed back,” and lists what falls on the heart that prefers another to Him:
  • Whoever loves a thing besides Him عُذِّبَ بِهِ وَلَا بُدّ — is, without fail, punished by means of that very thing.
  • Whoever fears other than Him is handed over to the thing he feared, and made to suffer it.
  • Whoever busies himself with something other than Him finds that thing turn into a misfortune upon him.
  • Whoever prefers another to Him is given no blessing لَمْ يُبَارَكْ لَهُ فِيه in what he preferred.
  • Whoever pleases another by angering Allah أَسْخَطَهُ عَلَيْهِ وَلَا بُدّ — has Allah's anger turned upon him, without fail, and the one he pleased is not enough to shield him.
The corollary

These are not five separate threats but one settled measure seen from five angles: whatever a heart sets above Allah is precisely what Allah turns against it. So the safest place for any love, fear, or hope is under Him, never ahead of Him. A thing kept in its rank beneath Allah is enjoyed and blessed; the same thing promoted above Him becomes the very means of one's punishment in it.

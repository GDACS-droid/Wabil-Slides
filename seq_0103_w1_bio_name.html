Who Was Ibn al-Qayyim?

شَمْس الدِّين مُحَمَّد بن أبي بَكْر بن أيُّوب
Shams al-Dīn Abū ʿAbdillāh Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr ibn Ayyūb
known as Ibn Qayyim al-Jawziyya — “son of the steward (qayyim) of the school of al-Jawzī”
  • Born 691H (1292) near Damascus, into a learned family; his father was the qayyim (steward) of a school named for Ibn al-Jawzī — hence the family name.
  • He lived under the Mamlūk sultanate, in a Damascus that was a great hub of ḥadīth and fiqh after the Mongol wars.
  • He died in 751H (1350), having taught, written, and worshipped in that city his whole life.
  • His own students — Ibn Kathīr, al-Dhahabī, Ibn Rajab — record him as a man of tahajjud and constant dhikr, gentle and free of malice.

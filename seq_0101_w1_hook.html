Before we open the book

Why begin with the heart?

Two believers stand in the same row, say the same words, make the same رُكُوع. One walks out forgiven; the other is told his prayer was written down as a half, or a third. What is the difference — if not the prayer itself?

The claim the whole course turns on

Ibn al-Qayyim gives the answer in this very letter: deeds are ranked before Allah بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ وَالإِخْلَاصِ وَالمَحَبَّةِ — by the faith, sincerity and love in the hearts that produce them. The same outward act, weighed by two different hearts, comes back two different weights. So we begin where he begins: with the heart that is alive, and the remembrance that keeps it so.

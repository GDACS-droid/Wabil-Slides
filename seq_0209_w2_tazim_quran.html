Foundation Two

Why Have You No Awe of Allah?

The second foundation is تَعْظِيمُ الأَمْرِ وَالنَّهْي, and Ibn al-Qayyim grounds it in the Qurʾān itself: فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ ذَمَّ مَنْ لَا يُعَظِّمُهُ وَلَا يُعَظِّمُ أَمْرَهُ وَنَهْيَه — “Allah has blamed the one who does not magnify Him, nor magnify His command and prohibition.”
مَّا لَكُمْ لَا تَرْجُونَ لِلَّهِ وَقَارًا
“What is the matter with you, that you hope not for any reverence toward Allah?”
Sūrat Nūḥ 71:13

How the mufassirūn read it

Ibn al-Qayyim reports their gloss: مَا لَكُمْ لَا تَخَافُونَ لِلَّهِ عَظَمَة — “why do you not fear the greatness of Allah?” The verse does not blame this people for outward crimes first; it indicts one inward lack — that they carry no weight of Allah in their hearts. Reverence for His command starts precisely here, in awe of His majesty; a heart that does not magnify the Lord will never, for long, magnify His order.

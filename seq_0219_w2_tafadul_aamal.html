The Principle

Two Foundations That Dissolve Many Confusions

The general rule — it is not only the prayer

Ibn al-Qayyim widens the prayer-principle to all deeds: وَيَنْبَغِي أَنْ يُعْلَمَ أَنَّ سَائِرَ الأَعْمَالِ تَجْرِي هَذَا المَجْرَى — “know that all deeds run this same course.” Therefore فَتَفَاضُلُ الأَعْمَالِ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ وَالإِخْلَاصِ وَالمَحَبَّةِ وَتَوَابِعِهَا — “deeds outrank one another before Allah by the differences in what is in the hearts: faith, sincerity, love, and their attendants.” The complete deed expiates completely; the deficient one, only to its measure.

  • Deeds excel by the realities of faith in the hearts بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنْ حَقَائِقِ الإِيمَان — two identical forms can carry utterly different weight, because the hearts behind them differ.
  • A deed wipes out sins to the degree of its completeness or its deficiency تَكْفِيرُ العَمَلِ لِلسَّيِّئَاتِ بِحَسَبِ كَمَالِهِ وَنُقْصَانِه — and with these two foundations together, he says, تَزُولُ إِشْكَالَاتٌ كَثِيرَة, “many confusions are resolved.”

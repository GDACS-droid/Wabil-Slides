A Question to Open With

Why Does Praise Come Before the Request?

We pray to ask. So why does the Prophet ﷺ stop a man who hurried straight to his need, and teach him to begin with the praise of his Lord?
مَنْ شَغَلَهُ ذِكْرِي عَنْ مَسْأَلَتِي أَعْطَيْتُهُ أَفْضَلَ مَا أُعْطِي السَّائِلِينَ
“Whoever is kept by My remembrance from asking of Me, I give him the best of what I give those who ask.”
Ḥadīth qudsī — recorded by al-Tirmidhī

Ibn al-Qayyim opens his chapters on dhikr with this puzzle: a servant so absorbed in remembering his Lord that he forgets to ask is given more than the asker. The whole week turns on the answer — that praise of Allah is itself the highest speech, and that it is what makes the asking answered.

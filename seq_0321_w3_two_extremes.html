The Straight Path

Lax License vs. Anxious Strictness

Lax license

  • التَّرَخُّصُ الجَافِي — riding a concession until it becomes neglect.
  • Delaying ظُهْر in the heat until the time nearly runs out; treating the traveller's joining of prayers as a fixed سُنَّة whether or not there is need, though the concession is only for genuine hardship.
  • Eating to the point of fullness and heaviness, so the body grows sluggish and worship becomes a chore — when the سُنَّة is a third for food, a third for drink, a third for breath.

Anxious strictness

  • التَّشْدِيدُ الغَالِي — overstrictness, and the obsessive doubt (وَسْوَسَة) that breeds it.
  • Repeating وُضُوء out of وَسْوَسَة until the time is lost; repeating the تَكْبِير until the Fātiḥa behind the إِمَام, or the رَكْعَة itself, slips away.
  • False scruple: a worshipper who would not eat the food of the Muslims out of suspicion of its provenance, and lived instead on food brought from the lands of the disbelievers.

The aim is the straight path

Quoting al-Harawī again: true honouring of the command and prohibition is that they not be met with a slack license, nor exposed to an excessive strictness, nor carried onto some pretext that weakens compliance. The goal Ibn al-Qayyim keeps returning to is الصِّرَاطُ المُسْتَقِيمُ — the one straight road that actually arrives at Allah.

The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 2

What Made Both Openings “the Greatest Name”

Ibn al-Qayyim's careful conclusion: it is the dhikr and thanāʾ that preceded the request which is the “greatest Name.” That is why two different openings both earned the same praise — “the remembrance of Allah and His praise is the most successful means by which a servant seeks his needs, and it is what makes the supplication answered.”

In practice

This frees the worshipper from hunting for a single secret word. If the “greatest Name” is the sincere praise that precedes the asking, then the way to call on it is open to anyone: before you bring the need, name the Names of Allah that actually fit it — calling Him al-Shāfī, the Healer, over a sickness; al-Razzāq, the Provider, over a debt; al-Tawwāb, the Accepter of repentance, over a sin. Let the opening praise lean toward what you are about to ask, so that by the time you reach the request the heart is already turned fully to the One who answers — and you have, in effect, called Him by His greatest Name.

The Title's Root

The Goodly Word Rises Up

إِلَيْهِ يَصْعَدُ الْكَلِمُ الطَّيِّبُ وَالْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ يَرْفَعُهُ
“To Him ascends the goodly word, and the righteous deed raises it up.”
ilayhi yaṣʿadu l-kalimu ṭ-ṭayyibu wa-l-ʿamalu ṣ-ṣāliḥu yarfaʿuh
Sūrat Fāṭir 35:10

Why this is the title

Ibn al-Qayyim draws the book's name from this āyah: الكَلِمُ الطَّيِّبُ — the goodly word that climbs to Allah — and الْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ, the righteous deed that raises it up. Tongue and limbs together; a dhikr the body confirms. The full title is al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib min al-Kalim al-Ṭayyib.

Taʿẓīm al-Amr

The Sign of an Honouring Heart

A man may perform the command and avoid the prohibition only for the eyes of people — to keep his standing, or out of fear of worldly punishment. Ibn al-Qayyim says this is not really obeying from taʿẓīm of the command at all.
  • The sign of honouring the commands: guarding their times and limits, examining their pillars and conditions, hastening to them, and grieving when any of their due is lost.
  • He gives the example of a man who grieves at missing the congregation — knowing that praying alone forfeits twenty-seven times the reward — the way a trader would bite his hands in regret over losing twenty-seven dīnārs in a single deal in his own town.
  • A man may pray with a cold heart, untouched by this loss; the scholars say of such a one that he barely has a prayer — for a servant gets from his prayer only what he was present for.

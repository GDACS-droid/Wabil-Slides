Group breakout
Ibn al-Qayyim says two believers can do the same act and walk away with very different weights — and that a sin can lift a man while a virtue sinks him.
  • Name one act you do on autopilot — wudūʾ, a daily ṣalāh, a routine duʿāʾ. What, concretely, is the difference between doing it as a “dead body” and doing it with khushūʿ? What would change tomorrow morning?
  • The salaf said a sin can take a man to Paradise through brokenness, and a good deed to the Fire through ʿujb. Have you seen this — in a heart softened by a fall, or hardened by its own good record?
  • “No veil is thicker than al-daʿwā — the claim.” Where does the quiet claim “I have arrived” hide in a practicing person's life?
Choose one answer to bring back to the room.

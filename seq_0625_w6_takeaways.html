Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry This Week

  • Dhikr is the easiest of acts and the most rewarded — do not be among those who, when the covering lifts, regret leaving the cheapest treasure unspent.
  • Make the bāqiyāt ṣāliḥāt — سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ — a daily wird: each phrase plants a tree in your Garden.
  • Seek out a gathering of dhikr — it is a meadow of the Garden now, and a gathering of the angels.
  • When the heart hardens or finds no rest, return to بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ القُلُوب — its cure and its rest are in His remembrance.
  • Remember to ripen love: “the servant keeps remembering until he loves Him” — and love, once settled, leaves no room for anything but ikhlāṣ.

Drawing the Threads · 2

Remembrance Ripens into Love, Love into Sincerity

المَحَبَّة
al-maḥabba
love of Allah — born of constant remembrance
الإِخْلَاص
al-ikhlāṣ
sincerity — the heart turned to Him alone

The chain Ibn al-Qayyim already drew

He gave us the links himself. Benefit 48: “the servant keeps remembering his Lord until he loves Him and befriends Him” — so dhikr is the root of love. Benefit 72: “if you do not settle the heart with the love of Allah, the love of creatures settles in it” — so love, once settled, leaves no room beside it, and the heart is cleared for Him alone. That clearing is ikhlāṣ. Read in order: remembrance feeds love, love crowds out every rival, and a heart with no rival is sincere by nature. This is the inward freedom al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib was written to plant: a heart so full of Him that nothing false can find a seat.

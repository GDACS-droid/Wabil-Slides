Faṣl 2 · “He Told You the Truth, Though He Is a Liar”

The Thief, the Charity, and Āyat al-Kursī

Abū Hurayra and the guard of the zakāt

Abū Hurayra was set by the Prophet ﷺ to guard the charity of Ramadan. Someone came scooping from the food; he seized him: “I will take you to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ!” The man pleaded need and was let go — twice. On the third night Abū Hurayra held him fast. The man said: “Let me go, and I will teach you words by which Allah will benefit you. When you go to your bed, recite Āyat al-Kursī — اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الحَيُّ القَيُّومُ — to its end. A guardian from Allah will stay with you, and no devil will come near you until morning.” The Prophet ﷺ said: “He told you the truth, though he is a liar. That was a devil.”

Recorded in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī. To it Ibn al-Qayyim joins Abū Masʿūd's hadith in the Ṣaḥīḥayn: “Whoever recites the two verses from the end of Sūrat al-Baqara at night, they suffice him” — meaning, the sound view, they suffice him against every harm.

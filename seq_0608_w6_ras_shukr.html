Fawāʾid al-Dhikr · 44–45

Dhikr Is the Head of Gratitude — and the Mark of the Noblest

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
44
73

No one thanked Allah who did not remember Him

Gratitude is built on dhikr: فَمَا شَكَرَ اللَّهَ مَنْ لَمْ يَذْكُرْهُ — none has truly thanked Allah who did not remember Him. Allah taught Mūsā the equation directly: “Remember Me much; when you remember Me much, you have thanked Me much, and when you forget Me you have been ungrateful to Me.” And the Prophet ﷺ joined the two in the counsel to Muʿādh — اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ — so that dhikr and shukr together are the whole of felicity.

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ رَأْسُ الشُّكْرِ

Benefit 45 — the noblest of the God-fearing

Then he raises it higher: the noblest of the pious before Allah is the one whose tongue never dries of His remembrance. When Mūsā asked “which of Your creation is dearest to You?” the answer came: الَّذِي يَذْكُرُنِي وَلَا يَنْسَانِي — “the one who remembers Me and does not forget Me.” Taqwā earns the Garden and rescue from the Fire, and that is reward; but dhikr earns nearness and rank beside Him, the higher station, the one the knowers race for.

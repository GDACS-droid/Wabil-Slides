Faṣl 1 · The Morning & the Evening

The Two Ends of the Day

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ ذِكْرًا كَثِيرًا ۝ وَسَبِّحُوهُ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا
“O you who believe, remember Allah with much remembrance, and glorify Him morning and evening.”
Sūrat al-Aḥzāb 33:41–42

Ibn al-Qayyim opens the section here, with طَرَفَيِ النَّهَار — the window between dawn and sunrise, and between ʿAṣr and sunset. The Qurʾān commands glorifying Allah بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا and بِالعَشِيِّ وَالإِبْكَار (Q 40:55), “before the rising of the sun and before its setting” (Q 50:39) — and this, he says, is what the morning-and-evening hadiths are pointing to: the place of these adhkār is after Ṣubḥ and after ʿAṣr.

Faṣl 1 · The Morning & the Evening

The Two Ends of the Day

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ ذِكْرًا كَثِيرًا ۝ وَسَبِّحُوهُ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا
“O you who believe, remember Allah with much remembrance, and glorify Him morning and evening.”
Sūrat al-Aḥzāb 33:41–42

Ibn al-Qayyim opens with طَرَفَيِ النَّهَار — the two ends: the window between dawn and sunrise, and between ʿAṣr and sunset. He lines up three āyāt — glorify Him بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا, بِالعَشِيِّ وَالإِبْكَارِ (Q 40:55), قَبْلَ طُلُوعِ الشَّمْسِ وَقَبْلَ الغُرُوبِ (Q 50:39) — then concludes: this is the tafsīr of the hadiths. “Whoever says such-and-such حِينَ يُصْبِحُ وَحِينَ يُمْسِي” means the two āyāt's window — “the place of these adhkār is after Ṣubḥ and after ʿAṣr.”

In practice

So the slot is already in your day: the few minutes after Fajr before you stand, and after ʿAṣr before the evening's errands begin. You do not have to find time — the Prophet ﷺ has pinned the wird to two prayers you already pray. Stay seated where you prayed; that is the appointed place.

Benefit 64 · The Proof

“Its Planting Is Subḥāna llāh…”

إِنَّ الجَنَّةَ طَيِّبَةُ التُّرْبَةِ، عَذْبَةُ المَاءِ، وَأَنَّهَا قِيعَانٌ، وَأَنَّ غِرَاسَهَا: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ
“The Garden is sweet of soil and fresh of water, and it is a level plain. Its planting is: Subḥāna llāh, wa-l-ḥamdu li-llāh, wa-lā ilāha illā llāh, wa-llāhu akbar.”
Marfūʿ, from the report of Ibrāhīm al-Khalīl — recorded by al-Tirmidhī

So dhikr is both the planting of the Garden and its building. Ibn al-Qayyim's point is plain: every word of these bāqiyāt ṣāliḥāt is not merely rewarded — it raises something for you there.

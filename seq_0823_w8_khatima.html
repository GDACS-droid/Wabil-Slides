Khātima · How Ibn al-Qayyim Closes

The Praise That Does Not Cease

وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ العَالَمِينَ حَمْدًا طَيِّبًا مُبَارَكًا فِيهِ كَمَا يُحِبُّ رَبُّنَا وَيَرْضَى، حَمْدًا لَا يَنْقَطِعُ وَلَا يَبِيدُ وَلَا يَفْنَى، عَدَدَ مَا حَمِدَهُ الحَامِدُونَ، وَصَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَى خَاتَمِ أَنْبِيَائِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ
“Praise is Allah's, Lord of the worlds — a praise wholesome and blessed, as our Lord loves and is pleased with; a praise that does not break off, nor pass away, nor perish, as much as all who have praised Him have praised; and may Allah send ṣalāt upon the seal of His prophets and messengers…”
Ibn al-Qayyim — the closing of al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

He ends the book by doing the very thing it taught: he remembers Allah. And he seals it with the Prophet ﷺ — “by whom Allah gave the hearts life after their death, and lit them after their darkness, and joined them after their scattering.” The whole book was about that life of the heart; the last line places it where it belongs, upon the one who brought it.

Fawāʾid al-Dhikr · 48–49

The Root of His Friendship; the Magnet of His Favours

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
48
73

Remembrance is the root of walāya; heedlessness the root of enmity

Ibn al-Qayyim sets the two roots side by side: dhikr is the root and head of befriending Allah, and ghafla is the root and head of warring with Him. He traces the mechanism: “the servant keeps remembering his Lord until he loves Him and takes Him as a friend; and the heedless keeps forgetting until he hates Him and takes Him as an enemy.” Ḥassān ibn ʿAṭiyya put the far end starkly: no servant ever showed his Lord a harsher enmity than to hate His remembrance and to hate the one who reminds him of Him.

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ أَصْلُ مُوَالَاةِ اللَّهِ، وَالغَفْلَةَ أَصْلُ مُعَادَاتِهِ

Benefit 49 — favours drawn, punishments repelled

And nothing draws down Allah's favours and wards off His punishments like His remembrance — “إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَدْفَعُ عَنِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا” (al-Ḥajj 22:38). His defence of a servant is measured by the strength of his faith, and faith's fuel is dhikr: the more complete a man's īmān and the more his remembrance, the greater Allah's warding off from him; whoever falls short in remembrance, falls short in protection by exactly that much.

The Principle

Two Foundations That Dissolve Confusions

The general rule

Ibn al-Qayyim says all deeds run this same course: فَتَفَاضُلُ الأَعْمَالِ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ وَالإِخْلَاصِ وَالمَحَبَّة — deeds outrank one another before Allah by the differences in what is in the hearts: faith, sincerity, love, and their attendants. The complete deed expiates completely; the deficient one, only to its measure.

  • Deeds excel by the realities of faith in the hearts — the same form can carry very different weight.
  • A deed wipes out sins to the degree of its completeness or deficiency — which untangles, he says, “many confusions.”

The Muqaddima

No Power Over My Servants — Yet Three Doors Remain

إِنَّ عِبَادِي لَيْسَ لَكَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُلْطَانٌ
“Indeed, over My servants you have no authority.”
inna ʿibādī laysa laka ʿalayhim sulṭān
Sūrat al-Ḥijr 15:42

What “no authority” means — and does not

When the enemy of Allah saw that Allah would not hand His servants over to him, he made his vow: فَبِعِزَّتِكَ لَأُغْوِيَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ ۝ إِلَّا عِبَادَكَ مِنْهُمُ المُخْلَصِينَ (Ṣād 38:82–83). So Allah's servants are in His ḥirz — His guarding and shelter; the enemy has no settled dominion over them. But, says Ibn al-Qayyim, he may still ambush one of them as a thief steals upon a heedless man — غِيلَةً عَلَى غِرَّةٍ وَغَفْلَةٍ — because the servant has been tested with three openings he can never fully seal: الغَفْلَة (heedlessness), الشَّهْوَة (desire), and الغَضَب (anger).

The Heart's Return

Tawba — Turning the Heart Back to Allah

The same confession that opens an answered duʿāʾ is the substance of tawba — the heart turning home. Ibn al-Qayyim places the duʿāʾ the Prophet ﷺ taught Abū Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him) at the meeting-point of asking and returning:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي ظُلْمًا كَثِيرًا، وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، فَاغْفِرْ لِي مَغْفِرَةً مِنْ عِندِكَ وَارْحَمْنِي، إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ
“O Allah, I have wronged myself with much wrong, and none forgives sins but You; so forgive me a forgiveness from Yourself, and have mercy on me — truly You are the Forgiving, the Merciful.”
al-Bukhārī & Muslim, from Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq

Ibn al-Qayyim: this supplication “gathers the confession of one's state, then the appeal to one's Lord by His bounty and generosity — that He alone forgives sins — then the request after this twofold appeal. Such is the etiquette of servanthood.” Notice it holds every adab of the week at once: praise, confession of poverty, then asking. There is no despair in tawba. It is the broken heart turning back, certain the door of mercy stands open.

Group breakout
“Its planting is سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ” And when the rememberer stops, the angels stop building.
  • Ibn al-Qayyim names a breach in the heart that only ذِكْر closes (38), and the poor Muhājirūn were given ذِكْر to overtake the rich (57). Where in your week do you reach for something other than ذِكْر to close that breach — and what would change if you reached for the planting instead?
  • If a word of ذِكْر literally lays a stone against Hell (65) and plants a tree in جَنَّة (64), what is it about the heart that still finds ذِكْر “easier to leave than to do,” as the regretful ones will say (62)?
  • Pick one phrase of the بَاقِيَات صَالِحَات to make your “planting” this week. Say exactly when and where you will plant it. Name a fixed time and a fixed place, not “more often.”
Ten minutes. Bring back one phrase your group commits to, with its time and place, and one honest reason we let it slip.

Benefits 63 & 65

“My Servant Has Told the Truth” — and the Barrier of Stones

Benefit 63 — the Lord affirms the rememberer's word

Dhikr is a cause of Allah confirming His servant, for in it the servant reports Allah's own attributes back to Him, and the Lord declares him truthful. The Prophet ﷺ said: when the servant says لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَر, Allah says: صَدَقَ عَبْدِي، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنَا وَأَنَا أَكْبَر — “My servant has spoken the truth; there is no god but Me and I am Greatest”; and so with each phrase, ending: “there is no god but Me and there is no power and no strength except by Me.” And “whoever is granted these at his death, the Fire shall not touch him.” (al-Tirmidhī and Ibn Mājah; ṣaḥīḥ per Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim.) Whom Allah names truthful is not raised with the liars.
Benefit 65 — a barrier you build a stone at a time

Dhikr is a wall between the servant and Hell: wherever a deed of his opens a road toward the Fire, his remembrance stands as a barrier across that road. And if his dhikr is constant and whole, it is “a sealed barrier with no gap.” A man of old set seven stones in his prayer-niche and after each prayer said, “O stones, I call you to witness there is no god but Allah”; when he died he saw in a vision each stone grown huge, sealing one of the gates of Hell against him, until all its gates were shut. The image is exact for us: every لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّه is one more stone laid in a wall you are building now, against a fire you have not yet seen.

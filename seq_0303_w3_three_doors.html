Madākhil al-Shayṭān

Three Doors He Comes Through

However much a servant guards himself, Ibn al-Qayyim says, he is still قَدْ بُلِيَ بِالغَفْلَةِ وَالشَّهْوَةِ وَالغَضَبِ — tested with heedlessness, appetite, and anger. The enemy enters through these three.
  • Ghafla — heedlessness: forgetting Allah, the unguarded moment. This is the master-door; the other two open because of it.
  • Shahwa — appetite: he makes a forbidden desire look sweet and presses you toward it.
  • Ghaḍab — anger: he inflames you until you wrong yourself or another.

Even Ādam was reached this way

Our father Ādam was the most knowing and most steadfast of creation, and still the enemy did not relent until he made him slip. “So what of the moth of forbearance,” Ibn al-Qayyim asks, “whose mind beside his father's is a drop in the sea?” The point is humility, not despair.

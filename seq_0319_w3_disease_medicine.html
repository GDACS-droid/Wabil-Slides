Amrāḍ al-Qulūb

The Heart Heals Like the Body

Here Ibn al-Qayyim names the image that gives this whole section its title: السَّيِّئَاتُ وَالذُّنُوبُ هِيَ أَمْرَاضٌ قَلْبِيَّةٌ — sins are diseases of the heart, as fever and aches are diseases of the body. The earlier deeds are like the body's prior strength; the sins are the illness; and true repentance is recovery — like for like.

Three kinds of recovery

He distinguishes three: the patient whose health never returns; the one restored to exactly what he was; and the one who comes back stronger and more vigorous than before, because the causes of healing overpowered the causes of sickness — “until his very illness became a cause of his health.” As the poet says: وَرُبَّمَا صَحَّتِ الأَجْسَامُ بِالعِلَلِ — sometimes bodies are made sound by their ailments. So the repentant servant stands at one of these three stations; and a sin that drives a man to a repentance like this may leave his heart sounder than it was before he fell.

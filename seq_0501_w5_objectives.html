Tonight's Path

We leave the introduction behind and enter the heart of the book: why dhikr is ranked where it is, and what it does, point by point, to the one who keeps it.
  • Hear Ibn al-Qayyim’s case for faḍl al-dhikr — the rank of dhikr above charity and above the open battlefield, from the words of the Prophet ﷺ.
  • Follow his faṣl al-khiṭāb — the decisive ruling that weighs the one who remembers against the warrior, and shows that dhikr is the soul inside the great deed, not its rival.
  • Begin the great enumeration: the roughly hundred fawāʾid al-dhikr, benefit by benefit — tonight, the first twenty-five — and learn the exact mechanism by which each fruit is produced.
  • Dwell on the heart-reviving ones: dhikr drives off Shayṭān, lifts grief and brings joy, gives the heart its very life and food, and polishes off its rust.
  • Ask, honestly, in breakout: which of these benefits does my own heart most need right now?

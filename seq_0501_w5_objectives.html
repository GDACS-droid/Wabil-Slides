Tonight's Path

We leave the introduction behind and enter the heart of the book: why dhikr is what it is, and what it does to the one who keeps it.
  • Hear Ibn al-Qayyim’s case for faḍl al-dhikr — the rank of dhikr above other deeds, from the words of the Prophet ﷺ.
  • Sit with his striking ruling — the faṣl al-khiṭāb — that the one who remembers Allah can outweigh even the warrior in the field.
  • Begin the great enumeration: the roughly hundred fawāʾid al-dhikr, benefit by benefit (tonight, the first twenty-five).
  • Dwell on the heart-reviving ones: dhikr drives off Shayṭān, lights the face and heart, and — in Ibn Taymiyya’s image — is to the heart as water is to the fish.
  • Ask, honestly, in breakout: which of these benefits does my own heart most need right now?

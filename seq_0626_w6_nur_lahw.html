Benefits 70 & 72 · Toward the Close

Light on the Face, and a Tongue That Will Not Be Still

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
70
73

Freshness in this world, light in the next

ذِكْر clothes the face in نَضْرَة in this life and نُور in the next: the rememberers are the most radiant of people in face here and the most luminous hereafter. This closes a thread Ibn al-Qayyim opened pages earlier — that ذِكْر gives light to the heart, the face and the limbs, and is “the servant's light in his world, in his بَرْزَخ, and on the Day of Rising.” The face that turns toward Him in private is the face He brightens in public, here and there.

أَنَّهُ يَكْسُو الوَجْهَ نَضْرَةً فِي الدُّنْيَا وَنُورًا فِي الآخِرَةِ

Benefit 72 — and there is no neutral tongue

Among the last fruits: busying the tongue with ذِكْر turns it away from backbiting, talebearing, idle talk, and the praise and blame of people. Then the sharp law beneath it: “The tongue is never silent — it is either a remembering tongue or an idle one; there is no escaping one of the two. So too the heart: if you do not settle it with the love of Allah, the love of creatures settles in it, unavoidably. فَاخْتَرْ لِنَفْسِكَ إِحْدَى الخُطَّتَيْن — choose for yourself one of the two states, and lodge in one of the two stations.” There is no third tongue and no empty heart; the only question is which tenant moves in.

Reading the Text Soundly

On Joining the Different Worded Reports

A question of method comes up with the duʿāʾ the Prophet ﷺ taught Abū Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him), narrated both with كَثِيرًا (“much”) and كَبِيرًا (“great”): when one wording is reported two ways, may a worshipper recite both at once?

Combine the two

  • al-Nawawī (may Allah have mercy on him) held it good to say both — ظُلْمًا كَثِيرًا كَبِيرًا — to be sure of catching the Prophet's ﷺ exact word.
  • Our own tradition has found room in this: joining two reliably-reported synonyms in seeking the certain wording does no harm.

Say one at a time

  • Ibn al-Qayyim, with his school's masters, prefers saying one wording this time and the other another — combining, he argues, is “a way no known imām preceded.”
  • Where the Prophet ﷺ is reliably reported to have said a word, saying that word is itself sunna.

We engage the principle, not the persons. The disagreement is one of awlā — what is preferable — not of ḥalāl and ḥarām; there is breadth here either way. Keep the full adab of the supplication, and let the heart's presence, not the count of words, be the real measure of the duʿāʾ.

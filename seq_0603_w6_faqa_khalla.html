Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

A Poverty in the Heart Only ذِكْر Can Close

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
38
73

In the heart is a خَلَّة — a breach — and a فَاقَة — a want

Ibn al-Qayyim is precise about the condition: in the heart there is a خَلَّة (a breach in the wall) and a فَاقَة (a craving need), and nothing at all closes it but the remembrance of Allah. He then names the point at which it closes: when ذِكْر becomes the heart's own شِعَار — its standard — so that the heart is the rememberer by origin and the tongue merely follows it. Reach that, and its owner becomes غَنِيًّا بِلَا مَال، عَزِيزًا بِلَا عَشِيرَة، مَهِيبًا بِلَا سُلْطَان — rich without wealth, mighty without a clan, awe-commanding without office. Stay heedless of it, and a man is the reverse: poor in the thick of his riches, lowly atop his authority, contemptible amid his many kin.

أَنَّ فِي القَلْبِ خَلَّةً وَفَاقَةً لَا يَسُدُّهَا شَيْءٌ إِلَّا ذِكْرُ اللَّهِ
Today

We feel that breach as a low restlessness, and we try to fill it with whatever is nearest: the screen unlocked the moment a queue forms, the second helping no hunger asked for, the conversation kept going so the silence never lands. Each closes the gap for a minute and reopens it wider. Ibn al-Qayyim is naming a real ache and a real treatment — try, for one waiting line this week, reaching for سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ instead of the phone, and watch which one the heart was actually asking for.

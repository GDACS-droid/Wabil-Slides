Tawḥīd

Two Slaves, Two Masters

The muwaḥḥid

  • Like the slave who worked for his master in his master's house and rendered to him exactly what he was set to do.
  • Everything he is — his being, sustenance, mercy, care, the warding off of every harm — is from Allah alone, with no partner; so he loves, fears, and hopes in Allah alone.

The mushrik

  • Like the slave whom the master set to work in the master's own house — yet who renders his labour and his tribute to someone other than his master.
  • He works for other than Allah in Allah's own house, and draws near to Allah's enemy by means of Allah's own favours upon him.

If a human master had such a slave, he would be the most hated of slaves to him, the one he would drive off and banish — though both master and slave are creatures living on another's bounty. How then with the Lord of the worlds, from whom alone every favour comes, who alone created, sustains, and shelters His slave? Yet the states of the mushrikīn — their very words and deeds — declare it: they love, fear, and hope in their andād, among the living and the dead, and chase their pleasure and flee their anger, more than they do toward Allah.

Orientation

Where we're going tonight

  • Finish Ibn al-Qayyim's list of the fruits of ذِكْر, from benefit 37 to the seventy-third, and slow down on the weightiest, where he himself slows down.
  • Learn the exact arguments, not the headlines: why ذِكْر substitutes for the voluntary acts the poor could not afford; why “لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ” gives the body strength; why the hypocrite is known by the scarcity of his remembrance.
  • Sit with the great proofs in full: the roving angels who hunt for circles of ذِكْر, Allah's صَلَاة upon those who remember much, and the long حَدِيث of the deeds that come to a man's rescue at death, in the grave, and on the صِرَاط.
  • See where every fruit is carrying the heart: into سَكِينَة (a settled stillness), then مَحَبَّة (love of Allah), then إِخْلَاص (sincerity to Him alone) — the whole point of the book.
  • Walk out with a concrete وِرْد: the بَاقِيَات صَالِحَات as a planting, and one phrase made a habit by name, time and place.

Orientation

Where we're going

  • Continue Ibn al-Qayyim's list of the fruits of dhikr — from benefit 37 toward seventy-three — slowing down on the weightiest.
  • See dhikr as the easiest of acts and among the noblest: it builds the homes of the Garden and plants its trees.
  • Take in the great proofs: gatherings of dhikr are the gatherings of angels, the cure of the heart, and a cause of Allah's صَلَاة upon the one who remembers.
  • Trace where all of this is pointing the heart: toward sakīna (tranquility), maḥabba (love of Allah), and ikhlāṣ (sincerity).

The Muqaddima

Servanthood Has Two Faces

In what you love (al-sarrāʾ)

  • Allah has over the servant a ʿubūdiyya in ease — gratitude for blessing, worship in comfort.
  • “Most people give servanthood in what they love,” Ibn al-Qayyim notes — this is the easier face.

In what you hate (al-ḍarrāʾ)

  • Allah has over the servant a ʿubūdiyya in hardship too — patience under what he dislikes.
  • “The real matter is giving servanthood in the things one hates” — and by this the ranks of servants are told apart.

The teaching

He gives homely examples: cold water for wuḍūʾ in the heat, and cold water for wuḍūʾ in the cold, are both ʿubūdiyya — “but there is a vast difference between the two.” إِنَّمَا ابْتَلَاهُ لِيَمْتَحِنَ صَبْرَهُ وَعُبُودِيَّتَهُ — He tried him only to test his patience and his servanthood.

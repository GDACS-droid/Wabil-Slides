The Third Kind · His Favours

Remembering His Blessings and Favours

The third kind is the remembrance of His آلَاء — His blessings, His gifts, His kindness, and the places His bounty has fallen on His servants. To remember a gift is to remember the Giver; this kind is the very seed of gratitude.

The heart that recalls how it was clothed, fed, guided and forgiven cannot stay heedless of the One who did it. So gratitude is not a feeling that arrives on its own. It is built by remembering. And because Allah says لَئِن شَكَرْتُمْ لَأَزِيدَنَّكُمْ (Q 14:7), this remembrance is also the door through which more blessing comes. The one who counts his Lord's favours is the one whose favours increase.

Today

Lie awake at night naming, slowly, the mercies of a single ordinary day: that you woke, that your eyes opened, that there was water in the tap and food in the kitchen, that no one you love was taken, that the sin you committed was still concealed. By the tenth, the heart that lay down complaining is sitting up grateful — and a grateful heart, the matn teaches, is a heart already in dhikr.

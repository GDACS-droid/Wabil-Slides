The Muqaddima

Patience Is Three Restraints

وَالصَّبْرُ: حَبْسُ النَّفْسِ عَنِ التَّسَخُّطِ بِالمَقْدُورِ، وَحَبْسُ اللِّسَانِ عَنِ الشَّكْوَى، وَحَبْسُ الجَوَارِحِ عَنِ المَعْصِيَةِ
“Patience is: restraining the soul from raging at the decree, restraining the tongue from complaint, and restraining the limbs from disobedience.”
wa-ṣ-ṣabru: ḥabsu n-nafsi ʿani t-tasakhkhuṭi bi-l-maqdūr, wa-ḥabsu l-lisāni ʿani sh-shakwā, wa-ḥabsu l-jawāriḥi ʿani l-maʿṣiya
Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

What this rules out

He names what breaks it: the wailing slap, the tearing of clothes, the pulling of hair. Ṣabr is a discipline of soul, tongue, and limb held together. When the servant keeps it, Ibn al-Qayyim says, انْقَلَبَتِ المِحْنَةُ فِي حَقِّهِ مِنْحَةً — the affliction is turned, for him, into a gift.

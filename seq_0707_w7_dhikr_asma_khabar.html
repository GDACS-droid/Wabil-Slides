The First Kind · Reporting His Perfections

Reporting Him as He Described Himself

The second face of the first kind is to report Allah's perfections — to remember Him by speaking truly of what He is:
  • “Allah hears the voices of His servants and sees their movements; nothing of their deeds is hidden from Him.”
  • “He is more merciful to them than their own fathers and mothers, and He is over all things powerful.”
  • “He is more joyful at His servant's repentance than a man who, having lost his mount in a barren land, suddenly finds it again.”
In the text

Ibn al-Qayyim says the highest of this kind is to praise Him “by what He praised Himself with, and by what His Messenger ﷺ praised Him with — مِنْ غَيْرِ تَحْرِيفٍ وَلَا تَعْطِيلٍ، وَمِنْ غَيْرِ تَشْبِيهٍ وَلَا تَمْثِيلٍ.” This affirming formula is the language of his Ḥanbalī school.

How we read it

We share that aim, and gladly: speak of Allah only as He and His Messenger ﷺ spoke, never likening Him to creation (لَيْسَ كَمِثْلِهِ شَيْءٌ) and never emptying His names of meaning. Our tradition affirms every name and attribute reported in the revelation, consigns the how to Him, and where a wording suggests bodily likeness, understands it in a way that befits His majesty. The reverence is one; only the manner of explaining is discussed. Engage the idea, not the person.

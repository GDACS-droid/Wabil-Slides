Benefit 72 · Toward the Close

The Tongue Will Not Be Still

Among the last fruits Ibn al-Qayyim names: in busying the tongue with dhikr there is a turning of it away from backbiting, talebearing, idle talk, and the praise and blame of people.

“The tongue is never silent — it is either a remembering tongue or an idle one, and there is no escaping one of the two. So too the heart: if you do not settle it with the love of Allah, the love of created things will settle in it, unavoidably. So choose for yourself one of the two states, and one of the two stations.”

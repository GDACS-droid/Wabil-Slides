The Prayer

“Rest Us With It, Not From It”

The lovers say: we pray to rest

The slave whose heart is present finds, when he leaves the prayer, a lightness in himself, as if weights had been set down — vigour, ease, and rest — until he wishes he had not left it; for it is the coolness of his eye, the bliss of his soul, the garden of his heart, and his rest in this world. He is as one in a prison until he enters it, that he may find rest in it, not from it. So the lovers say: we pray to find rest by our prayer — as their Imām and Prophet ﷺ said: “Refresh us with the prayer, O Bilāl” — and he did not say, “Refresh us from it.”

يَا بِلَالُ أَرِحْنَا بِالصَّلَاةِ
“O Bilāl, refresh us with the prayer.” — and he ﷺ also said: “The coolness of my eye has been placed in the prayer.”
Recorded by Abū Dāwūd and Aḥmad; the second by al-Nasāʾī and Aḥmad

Takeaways

What to carry out the door

  • Guard the door of ghafla. The enemy reaches you only off-guard. Claim the idle minute — a word of dhikr before the hand reaches for the phone is the lock on the door.
  • Travel on both wings. Hold I confess Your favour and I confess my sin together — gratitude without conceit, brokenness without despair — and enter by the door of bankruptcy, with no claim to show.
  • Watch the deed after you do it. The danger is rarely the falling; it is standing tall afterward. Guard your acts from riyāʾ, from the silent المَنّ, and from speaking them into the register of the public.
  • Hold the straight road. Refuse both the slack license and the anxious, doubting strictness; the enemy is glad to take you by either, and will work both ends of the same Ramaḍān.
  • Polish daily. Sins are diseases of the heart; istighfār and dhikr are its medicine — and the bridge into next week's hundred fruits of dhikr.

This week's wird

Anchor each day in Sayyid al-istighfār — morning and evening — letting أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي set both wings before the day begins and as it closes.

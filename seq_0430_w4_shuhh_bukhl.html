Ṣadaqa & Sakhāʾ

Two Men in Coats of Iron

The miser and the giver

The Prophet ﷺ likened the miser and the giver to two men in coats of iron reaching from breast to collarbone. Whenever the giver gives, the coat expands until it covers his fingertips and wipes out his tracks; whenever the miser means to give, every ring clamps back into its place — “he tries to widen it and it will not widen.”

So the giver's chest opens with each ṣadaqa — his heart unbinds, his ease and gladness swell. The miser's heart stays shut in its cell, narrow-chested, small-souled, heavy with worry, scarcely able to get a need met.
al-Bukhārī (#1443) and Muslim (#1021).
الشُّحّ
al-shuḥḥ
intense greed for a thing, grasping pursuit of it and craving for it — latent in the soul, before the thing is even gained; it summons bukhl
البُخْل
al-bukhl
withholding what one already has after attaining it — the fruit of shuḥḥ; whoever withholds has obeyed his shuḥḥ, whoever does not has disobeyed it

Group breakout
“The Garden is a level plain; its planting is سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ.”
  • If a word of dhikr literally plants a tree in your Janna, what is it about our hearts that we still find dhikr “easier to leave than to do” — as the regretful ones will say?
  • Ibn al-Qayyim says dhikr closes a gap in the heart nothing else fills. Where in your week do you reach for something other than dhikr to close that gap — and what is it?
  • Pick one bāqiyāt ṣāliḥāt phrase to make your “planting” this week. When and where, concretely, will you say it?
Ten minutes. Bring back one phrase your group will commit to, and one honest reason we let it slip.

Faṣl 1 · Anwāʿ al-Dhikr

Remembrance Is of Three Kinds

Ibn al-Qayyim divides the remembrance of Allah into three kinds (الذِّكْرُ نَوْعَان, then a third), each one a way the heart and tongue turn to their Lord:
  • 1 — Dhikr of His names and attributes: praising Him by them, declaring Him One by them, and exalting Him above all that does not befit Him.
  • 2 — Dhikr of His command and prohibition: remembering what He has ordered and forbidden, the lawful and the forbidden — the dhikr of the people of knowledge.
  • 3 — Dhikr of His blessings and favours: remembering His gifts, His kindness, and the reach of His bounty over His servants.

He adds that each kind can be of the heart and tongue together — “that is the highest” — or of the heart alone, or of the tongue alone. The fullest dhikr is the one upon which the heart and the tongue agree.

The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 3

When Praise Becomes the Greatest Name

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ بِأَنِّي أَشْهَدُ أَنَّكَ أَنْتَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ الأَحَدُ الصَّمَدُ الَّذِي لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ
“O Allah, I ask You — for I bear witness that You are Allah, there is no god but You, the One, the Eternal Refuge, who did not beget nor was begotten, and there is none comparable to Him.”
Ḥadīth of Buraydah — recorded in the Sunan and ṣaḥḥaḥahu Ibn Ḥibbān

The Prophet ﷺ said of the man who prayed thus: “He has asked Allah by His greatest Name (الاسْمُ الأَعْظَم), by which when He is called He answers, and when He is asked He gives.” And of another who began “O Allah, to You belongs all praise, there is no god but You, the Bestower (al-Mannān)…” he said the same. Ibn al-Qayyim: it is the dhikr and thanāʾ preceding the request that is the greatest Name — “the remembrance of Allah and His praise is the most successful means by which a servant seeks his needs.”

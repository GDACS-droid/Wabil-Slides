The Roving Angels

Gatherings of ذِكْر Are Gatherings of the Angels

Benefit 52 — “Come — here is what you sought!”

“Allah has angels, surplus to the recording angels, who rove the roads seeking out the people of ذِكْر. When they find a folk remembering Allah, they call to one another: Come — here is what you sought! — and they enfold them with their wings up to the lowest heaven. Their Lord asks them, though He knows best: What do My servants say? They say: They glorify You, magnify You, praise You, exalt You. He asks: Have they seen Me? They say: No, by Allah, they have not seen You. He says: And how if they had seen Me? They say: Had they seen You, they would worship You harder, exalt and praise and glorify You more.” He asks what they seek (the Garden) and what they flee (the Fire), though they have seen neither, and they answer that the seekers would be the more eager and the fleers the more afraid. Then: “I call you to witness that I have forgiven them.” An angel objects: among them is so-and-so, not one of them — he only came for a need. He says: هُمُ الجُلَسَاءُ لَا يَشْقَى بِهِمْ جَلِيسُهُمْ — “They are the sitters; the one who sits with them is not made wretched.”

Recorded in al-Ṣaḥīḥayn

Al-Bukhārī (no. 6408) and Muslim (no. 2689) from Abū Hurayra. Ibn al-Qayyim's point lands on the last line: of every gathering on earth, this is the only one whose mere bystander is forgiven for the company he kept. The blessing of the circle reaches the one who only wandered in.

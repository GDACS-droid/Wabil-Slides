The Title's Root

The Word That Rises, the Tree That Stands

إِلَيْهِ يَصْعَدُ الْكَلِمُ الطَّيِّبُ وَالْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ يَرْفَعُهُ
“To Him ascends the goodly word, and the righteous deed raises it up.”
Sūrat Fāṭir 35:10
أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ ضَرَبَ اللَّهُ مَثَلًا كَلِمَةً طَيِّبَةً كَشَجَرَةٍ طَيِّبَةٍ أَصْلُهَا ثَابِتٌ وَفَرْعُهَا فِي السَّمَاءِ
“Do you not see how Allah strikes a parable — a goodly word is like a goodly tree, its root firm and its branches in the sky?”
Sūrat Ibrāhīm 14:24

Two آيَات, one title

From the first آيَة he takes the name: the word ascends, and the deed beneath it does the lifting. Tongue and limbs together, a ذِكْر the body confirms. The second gives him the shape: the goodly word is a tree, its root set firm in the heart — faith and remembrance — and its branches the good a believer says and does out in the open. The whole letter answers one question: how do you keep that tree watered?

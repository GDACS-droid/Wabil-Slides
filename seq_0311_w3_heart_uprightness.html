Istiqāmat al-Qalb

Two Pillars of an Upright Heart

  • First: that the love of Allah outranks every other love. When love of Allah and love of anything else collide, the love of Allah wins, and you act on what it requires. “How easy this is in word,” he says, “and how hard in deed; at the test a man is honoured or disgraced.”
  • Ibn al-Qayyim adds a warning law: whoever prefers his own desire — or the wish of someone he reveres — over what Allah loves will be tried through the very thing he preferred; مَنْ أَحَبَّ شَيْئًا سِوَاهُ عُذِّبَ بِهِ, and what he sought he reaches only with hardship and bitterness.
  • Second: that you magnify His command and prohibition. This grows out of magnifying the Commander Himself — for the believer knows his Lord through the Message He sent, and honouring the Sender shows in honouring His command.
مَّا لَكُمْ لَا تَرْجُونَ لِلَّهِ وَقَارًا
“What is the matter with you, that you do not attribute to Allah due grandeur?” — explained by the salaf as: that you do not fear the greatness of Allah.
Sūrat Nūḥ 71:13

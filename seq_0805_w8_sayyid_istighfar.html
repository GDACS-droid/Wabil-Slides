Faṣl 1 · The Master of Seeking Forgiveness

Sayyid al-Istighfār

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ، وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي، فَاغْفِرْ لِي، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
“O Allah, You are my Lord; there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant; I keep Your covenant and Your promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil I have done; I acknowledge Your favour upon me, and I acknowledge my sin — so forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.”
Shaddād b. Aws — Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 6323

“Whoever says it in the evening with certainty and dies that night enters the Garden; and whoever says it in the morning with certainty and dies that day enters the Garden.” This is the weightiest single phrase of the morning-and-evening set — keep it if you keep nothing else.

Foundation Two

Doing the Deed, Missing the Point

Ibn al-Qayyim forestalls a confusion: a man may do the command and leave the prohibition and still magnify neither — the outward act identical, the heart behind it aimed elsewhere فَإِنَّ الرَّجُلَ قَدْ يَتَعَاطَى فِعْلَ الأَمْر.

Why he obeys

  • لِنَظَرِ الخَلْق — for the eyes of people, that they may see him doing it.
  • To win rank and standing among them طَلَبُ المَنْزِلَةِ وَالجَاه — the deed is a bid for status.

Why he abstains

  • خَشْيَةَ سُقُوطِهِ مِنْ أَعْيُنِهِم — out of fear of falling in their estimation.
  • Out of dread of worldly penalties — the ḥudūd the Lawgiver attached to the prohibitions, not dread of the Lawgiver.

The verdict

فَهَذَا لَيْسَ فِعْلُهُ وَتَرْكُهُ صَادِرًا عَنْ تَعْظِيمِ الأَمْرِ وَالنَّهْي، وَلَا عَنْ تَعْظِيمِ الآمِرِ النَّاهِي — such a man's doing and leaving “issues neither from magnifying the command and prohibition, nor from magnifying the One who commands and forbids.” He is obeying creation through the form of obeying the Creator: the limbs upright, the foundation missing.

The Master Supplication

It Holds Both Wings

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي، فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ.
“O Allah, You are my Lord, there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I keep Your covenant and Your promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil I have done. I acknowledge before You Your favour upon me, and I acknowledge my sin — so forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.”
al-Bukhārī (6323), from Shaddād b. Aws

Why Ibn al-Qayyim quotes it here

In أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي the Prophet ﷺ joined the two wings in one breath: I confess Your favour (witnessing the gift) and I confess my sin (inspecting the fault). This is the whole posture of the slave before his Lord.

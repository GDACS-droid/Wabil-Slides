Picking Up the Thread

Dhikr Is the Head of the Whole Affair

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
37
73

It opens the door of entry upon Allah

Ibn al-Qayyim calls dhikr رَأْسُ الأَمْرِ — the head of the whole affair, the road that the people of Allah travel as a body, and the warrant of His friendship. His argument is a clean conditional: whoever is opened to dhikr is opened to the door of entry upon Allah; whoever enters upon Allah finds with Him everything he wants. “So let him purify himself and enter upon his Lord — he will find with Him all he wants. For if he finds his Lord, he has found everything; and if he loses his Lord, he has lost everything.”

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ رَأْسُ الأُمُورِ، وَطَرِيقُ عَامَّةِ الطَّائِفَةِ، وَمَنْشُورُ الوِلَايَةِ

What this frames

If dhikr is the door and not one room among many, then the question of the night is not “is remembrance good?” — that is settled — but “why do we leave open a door that opens onto everything we want?” Every benefit that follows is Ibn al-Qayyim answering that question from a different side.

Ṣadaqa & Sakhāʾ

Charity Quenches Sin as Water Quenches Fire

والصَّدَقَةُ تُطْفِئُ الخَطِيئَةَ كَمَا يُطْفِئُ المَاءُ النَّارَ
“…and charity extinguishes sin as water extinguishes fire.”
From the ḥadīth of Muʿādh; recorded by al-Tirmidhī (#2616): ḥasan ṣaḥīḥ
فَاتَّقُوا النَّارَ ولَوْ بِشِقِّ تَمْرَةٍ
“So guard yourselves against the Fire, even with half a date.”
From the ḥadīth of ʿAdī b. Ḥātim; al-Bukhārī (#7512) and Muslim (#1016)
In practice

The two ḥadīths set the bar deliberately low — “even half a date,” “even from your jewellery” — so that no one is excused. The lesson is not the size of the gift but the unbroken habit of giving: a fixed daily ṣadaqa, however small, that you never let a day pass without — a coin in the box on the way to work, a few dollars sent each morning. That steady drip is the water kept ready against the fire.

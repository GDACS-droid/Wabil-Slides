Fawāʾid al-Dhikr · A Cluster

Six Quieter Fruits to Carry Together

  • 39 — It gathers what is scattered. Dhikr draws together the heart, will and resolve that the world had pulled apart; “all of life and all of bliss lie in the heart's gathering.”
  • 40 — It wakes the sleeping heart. The heedless heart is asleep and its trade is loss; nothing rouses it but dhikr, for “heedlessness is a heavy sleep.”
  • 41 — It is a tree that fruits the stations. From the tree of dhikr grow the spiritual states the travellers seek; the larger the tree and the deeper its root, the greater its fruit.
  • 42 — It brings the special maʿiyya. The rememberer is with his Remembered, and his Remembered is with him — a nearness of love, support and aid, beyond the general “withness” of knowledge.
  • 43 — It equals freeing slaves and spending in jihād. “Were I to glorify Allah a few times,” said Ibn Masʿūd, “it is dearer to me than to spend their number in dīnārs in the path of Allah.”
  • 44 — It is the head of gratitude. No one has thanked Allah who did not remember Him; Allah taught Mūsā: “remember Me much — when you do, you have thanked Me much.”

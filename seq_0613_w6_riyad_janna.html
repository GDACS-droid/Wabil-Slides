Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

The Meadows of the Garden, Settled in This World

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
51
73

Whoever would dwell in جَنَّة's meadows now, let him settle in ذِكْر's gatherings

Jābir reports the Prophet ﷺ coming out and saying, “O people, graze in the meadows of the Garden!” They asked what they were; he said: مَجَالِسُ الذِّكْر — the gatherings of remembrance. Ibn al-Qayyim joins this to benefit 69, the one delight no other act resembles: were the rememberer's only reward the joy that settles on his heart, “it would be enough” — and it is for this that these gatherings earn the name of the Garden's own meadows. They are not like the meadows; they are a portion of them, tasted early.

مَنْ شَاءَ أَنْ يَسْكُنَ رِيَاضَ الجَنَّةِ فِي الدُّنْيَا فَلْيَسْتَوْطِنْ مَجَالِسَ الذِّكْرِ

Mālik ibn Dīnār

“مَا تَلَذَّذَ المُتَلَذِّذُونَ بِمِثْلِ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ — the seekers of pleasure have tasted nothing like the remembrance of Allah.” The claim is comparative and total: of every pleasure ever chased, none matched this one.

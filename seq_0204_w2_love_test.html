Foundation One

Easy to Claim, Hard to Live

وَمَا أَسْهَلَ هَذَا بِالدَّعْوَى، وَمَا أَصْعَبَهُ بِالفِعْل! — “how easy this is to claim, and how hard to do.”

At the test, a man is honored or disgraced

Ibn al-Qayyim follows the claim with the verdict on it: فَعِنْدَ الامْتِحَانِ يُكْرَمُ المَرْءُ أَوْ يُهَان — “at the moment of testing, a man is either honored or disgraced.” The point is sharp: love of Allah is never measured while the two loves agree. It is measured only at the seam where they tear apart, where obedience costs you the very thing you wanted. Anyone can say Allah comes first when nothing is on the table. The claim becomes true, or is exposed as empty, the instant something has to be given up.

In practice

The alarm sounds for fajr in the cold dark, and at that exact second two loves are on the scale: love of the One who called you, and love of the warm bed. Nothing here is dramatic, and that is the point. The seam is small, ordinary, and recurring, and the heart's real ranking shows in which way the hand moves. This is the imtiḥān Ibn al-Qayyim means: not a grand trial once in a lifetime, but the quiet collisions that come several times a day.

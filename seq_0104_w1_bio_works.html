The Author

A Master of Many Sciences

  • Madārij al-Sālikīn — his masterwork of تَزْكِيَة, a vast commentary on the stations of the wayfarer to Allah. We will cross-check our letter against it more than once: it is where he expands شُكْر from three pillars to five.
  • Zād al-Maʿād — on the هَدْي (guidance) of the Prophet ﷺ in worship, dealings, medicine, and the ordering of a day.
  • Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn — a major work of أُصُول الْفِقْه and the discipline of issuing فَتْوَى “on behalf of” Allah.
  • And the works of the heart that are al-Wābil's near kin: al-Dāʾ wa-l-Dawāʾ (the disease and its cure), Ṭarīq al-Hijratayn, Miftāḥ Dār al-Saʿāda, and Kitāb al-Rūḥ.

Among these, al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib is the short, intimate one. It is not a system but a letter, the same teacher of the heart speaking to a single friend in need. What Madārij maps over volumes, this letter presses into your hand in an evening.

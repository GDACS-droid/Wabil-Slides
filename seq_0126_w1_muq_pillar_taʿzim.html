The Muqaddima

Magnifying the Command and the Prohibition

مَّا لَكُمْ لَا تَرْجُونَ لِلَّهِ وَقَارًا
“What is the matter with you, that you do not ascribe to Allah a fitting majesty?”
mā lakum lā tarjūna li-llāhi waqārā
Sūrat Nūḥ 71:13

Where it comes from, and a warning

The second thing that makes the heart stand straight is تَعْظِيمُ الأَمْرِ وَالنَّهْي — magnifying Allah's command and prohibition — and this, the author insists, flows from magnifying the One who commands and forbids: you honor the order because you honor the King. But honoring is not extremism. He gives the precise definition: it is that the command and prohibition لَا يُعَارَضَا بِتَرْخِيصٍ جَافٍ، وَلَا يُعَرَّضَا لِتَشْدِيدٍ غَالٍ — “neither be set against by a slack license, nor exposed to an extreme severity, nor borne on a pretext that weakens one's compliance.” Reverence keeps to the middle: neither watering the command down, nor piling on what Allah never asked.

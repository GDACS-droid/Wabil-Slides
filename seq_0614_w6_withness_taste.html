Benefit 42 · A Caution from the Text

Seek the Nearness — Keep the Distinction

Ibn al-Qayyim pauses on the experiential taste (ذَوْق) of this nearness and calls it مَزِلَّة أَقْدَام — a place where feet can slip.

His caution is one we teach gladly, for it is our own ground: the maʿiyya of love and aid is real and to be sought, but the servant must never lose the distinction between the Eternal and the originated — between the Lord and the servant, the Creator and the created. Whoever loses it falls into the error of ḥulūl (indwelling) or the claim that the Lord's being is the very being of created things (waḥdat al-wujūd in its corrupt sense). So pursue the nearness, and keep beneath the taste a sound creed: the Creator is never the creature, and tanzīh is never surrendered to ecstasy.

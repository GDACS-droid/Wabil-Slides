Benefit 42 · The Special مَعِيَّة

“I Am with My Servant as He Remembers Me”

أَنَا مَعَ عَبْدِي مَا ذَكَرَنِي وَتَحَرَّكَتْ بِي شَفَتَاهُ
“I am with My servant as long as he remembers Me and his two lips move with Me.”
Ḥadīth qudsī — suspended (muʿallaq) by al-Bukhārī and connected in Khalq Afʿāl al-ʿIbād; graded ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim (al-Dhahabī concurring), from Abū Hurayra

Two withnesses, kept distinct

Ibn al-Qayyim draws a line the student must not blur. There is a general مَعِيَّة of knowledge and encompassment that takes in all creatures, believer and rebel alike. And there is a special withness — of nearness, friendship, love, support, and granting of success — that belongs only to the rememberer, as in إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا (al-Naḥl 16:128) and لَا تَحْزَنْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا (al-Tawba 9:40). The rule that follows is exact: the servant who keeps Allah's company in his ease earns Allah's company in his hardship.

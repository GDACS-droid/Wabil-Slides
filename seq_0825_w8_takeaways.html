Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry — For Good

  • Keep the wird you built tonight — small and daily beats large and abandoned. Begin tomorrow with the morning set after Ṣubḥ.
  • Anchor the two ends of the day: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ ×100, and sayyid al-istighfār — “whoever says it with certainty and dies that day enters the Garden.”
  • Tie remembrance to the things you already do — the doorway, the masjid, the adhān, the end of each prayer: اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ.
  • Keep one duʿāʾ for the hard nights — اللَّهُمَّ فَارِجَ الهَمِّ، كَاشِفَ الغَمِّ — and one for the heart itself: يَا مُقَلِّبَ القُلُوبِ ثَبِّتْ قَلْبِي عَلَى دِينِكَ.
  • Remember what the whole book was for: not adhkār collected, but a heart given life. Guard the wird, and let it guard your heart.

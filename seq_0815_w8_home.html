Faṣl 6 & 7 · The Doorway

Leaving the Home, Entering the Home

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ
“In the name of Allah; I rely upon Allah; there is no power and no strength except by Allah.” — said on leaving the house, after which he is told: you are sufficed, guided and protected, and the devil withdraws, one saying to another: how can you touch a man already sufficed, guided and protected?
Anas — Abū Dāwūd, al-Tirmidhī & al-Nasāʾī, ḥasan

On entering, Ibn al-Qayyim cites Jābir (Muslim): when a man remembers Allah at his coming in and at his food, the devil says to his band — لَا مَبِيتَ لَكُمْ وَلَا عَشَاءَ, “no lodging for you here and no supper.” But if he enters heedless of Allah, the devil says: أَدْرَكْتُمُ المَبِيتَ — “you have your lodging.” So enter with His name, and greet your household with سَلَام — “a blessing upon you and upon your house.”

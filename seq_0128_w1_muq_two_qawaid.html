The Muqaddima

Two Rules — and the Puzzle They Solve

Rule 1 — Deeds ranked by hearts

  • تَفَاضُلُ الأَعْمَالِ بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنْ حَقَائِقِ الإِيمَانِ.
  • Acts outrank one another by the realities of faith, sincerity and love in the hearts behind them. So two identical prayers are not two equal prayers.

Rule 2 — Forgiveness by completeness

  • An act effaces sins in proportion to its own completeness: the perfect deed wipes out perfectly, the deficient one to its measure.
  • So a deed's power to efface sins is not a fixed amount; it follows how whole the deed — and the heart within it — actually was.

The puzzle these solve

With these two rules, the author says, تَزُولُ إِشْكَالَاتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ — many difficulties dissolve. Take the famous one: the حَدِيث that the fast of ʿArafa expiates two years and ʿĀshūrāʾ one year (Muslim no. 1162, from Abū Qatāda). People ask: how can one day's fast erase years of sin? The answer: the promise is for the complete fast of a complete heart, and to the exact degree a servant falls short of that completeness, the expiation falls short with him. The reward is real; you simply receive it in the measure of the heart you brought.

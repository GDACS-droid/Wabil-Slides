Tamthīl al-Qulūb bi-l-Buyūt

Which House Will the Thief Rob?

Three houses, one thief

A house belonging to the king, holding his treasures and jewels. A house belonging to the slave, holding the slave's own goods, with none of the king's. And an empty house with nothing in it at all.

A thief comes to rob one of the three — which will he choose? Not the empty house: there is nothing in it to take. Hence Ibn ʿAbbās رضي الله عنهما, told that the Jews claim they do not get whisperings in their prayer, replied: “What would shayṭān do with a ruined heart?” Not the king's house: it has guards and soldiers about it the thief cannot approach — and its guard is the King Himself. There remains only the third house — and that is the one he raids.

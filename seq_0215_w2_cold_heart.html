ʿAlāmāt al-Taʿẓīm

Unmoved by the Loss

Many scholars said: he has no prayer

Ibn al-Qayyim reports that many of the ʿulamāʾ said of the man whose heart is cold toward this loss, empty of any sense of the calamity, not alarmed by it at all: لَا صَلَاةَ لَهُ, “he has no prayer.” And he names the cause: فَهَذَا مِنْ عَدَمِ تَعْظِيمِ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ فِي قَلْبِه, “this coldness is from the absence of magnifying the command of Allah in his heart.” The feeling, or its absence, is the readout of the foundation.

  • Missing the first of the time أَوَّلُ الوَقْتِ الَّذِي هُوَ رِضْوَانُ اللَّه — which is the very pleasure of Allah.
  • Missing the first row, upon whose right “Allah and His angels send blessings” — “if a servant knew its merit he would compete for it, even down to drawing lots.”
  • Missing the larger gathering: the more the worshippers, the more beloved to Allah; and the farther the walk, “a step that erases a sin, another that raises a degree” خُطْوَةٌ تَحُطُّ خَطِيئَة، وَأُخْرَى تَرْفَعُ دَرَجَة.
The conscience gone quiet

The frightening case is not the man who feels guilt and ignores it. It is the one who feels nothing, whose conscience has gone silent and lets one loss after another pass without a flinch. The small inward ache when a right of the prayer is dropped is itself a mercy: the day it stops ringing is the day the foundation has quietly given way.

From the Qurʾān

The Warning Against the Rusted Heart

وَلَا تُطِعْ مَنْ أَغْفَلْنَا قَلْبَهُ عَن ذِكْرِنَا وَاتَّبَعَ هَوَاهُ وَكَانَ أَمْرُهُ فُرُطًا
“And do not obey one whose heart We have made heedless of Our remembrance, who follows his own desire, and whose affair has become excess.”
al-Kahf 18:28

How to choose whom to follow

Ibn al-Qayyim draws a practical rule from this āyah: before you take a person as a guide, look — is he of the people of dhikr, or of the heedless? Is he ruled by revelation, or by his own desire (hawā)? Where heedlessness, desire, and furuṭ (a squandered, unguarded affair) meet in a man, he leads only to ruin.

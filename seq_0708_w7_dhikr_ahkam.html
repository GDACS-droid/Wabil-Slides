The Second Kind · His Command

Remembering His Command and Prohibition

Reporting His ruling

  • Remembering that He commanded this, forbade that, loves this, is angered by that.
  • This is the knowledge of the scholars — studying and teaching what Allah loves and hates: مُدَارَسَةُ العِلْم, learning and conveying it.

Acting upon it

  • Remembering Him at His command — hastening to obey; and at His prohibition — fleeing from it.
  • This is standing at His limits: الوُقُوفُ عِنْدَ حُدُودِ اللَّه when the moment of action arrives.

When these two meet in one servant — knowing the ruling, and answering it in the moment — “his dhikr is the noblest and greatest in benefit. This is the fiqh al-akbar.” To remember His command is to live answerable to Him, not merely recall it.

In practice

A man at his desk recalls, exactly, the ruling that backbiting an absent colleague is forbidden — that is the first half, the scholar's dhikr. Then the conversation turns, the joke about that colleague is offered to him, and he closes his mouth and changes the subject — that is the second half, the dhikr that is the greater fiqh. Knowledge that does not move the limbs at the moment of test is only the half of it.

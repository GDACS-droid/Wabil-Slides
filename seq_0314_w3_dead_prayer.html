Taʿẓīm al-Amr

A Gift With No Life In It

خُشُوع and the heart's presence, Ibn al-Qayyim says, are the very soul of the prayer; فَصَلَاةٌ بِلَا خُشُوعٍ كَبَدَنٍ مَيِّتٍ لَا رُوحَ فِيهِ — a prayer with no presence is like a dead body with no soul in it.

He puts a question to the conscience: would you feel no shame to gift another human being — a king, an أَمِير — a dead slave? How then would such an offering be received? So too is the prayer emptied of presence: a corpse presented to the King of kings. Allah does not accept it from him nor reward him for it, even if it lifts the legal obligation in the rulings of this world. As the hadith says: إِنَّ العَبْدَ لَيُصَلِّي الصَّلَاةَ وَمَا كُتِبَ لَهُ إِلَّا نِصْفُهَا، إِلَّا ثُلُثُهَا… حَتَّى بَلَغَ عُشْرُهَا — a servant prays, and only half of it is written for him, only a third, until a tenth. (Recorded by Abū Dāwūd, al-Nasāʾī in al-Kubrā, and Aḥmad, from ʿAmmār b. Yāsir; graded ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān.)

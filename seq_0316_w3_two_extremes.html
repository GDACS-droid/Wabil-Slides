The Straight Path

Lax License vs. Excess Strictness

Lax license

  • التَّرَخُّصُ الجَافِي — riding a concession until it becomes neglect.
  • Delaying Ẓuhr in the heat until the time nearly runs out; treating travel-joining of prayers as a fixed sunna with or without need.
  • Eating to the point of overfilling and heaviness, so worship becomes a burden.

Excess strictness

  • التَّشْدِيدُ الغَالِي — overstrictness, and the obsessive doubt (waswasa) that breeds it.
  • Repeating the takbīr or wuḍūʾ out of waswasa until the prayer or its time is lost.
  • False scruple: a worshipper who would not eat the food of the Muslims out of suspicion, and lived on food brought from the lands of the disbelievers.

The aim is the straight path

Quoting al-Harawī again: true honouring of the command and prohibition is that they not be met with a slack license, nor exposed to an excessive strictness, nor carried onto some pretext that weakens compliance. The goal is the straight road that arrives at Allah.

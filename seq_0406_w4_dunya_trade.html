Ḥiqārat al-Dunyā

The Trade That Loses Both Worlds

ابْنَ آدَمَ، بِعِ الدُّنْيَا بِالآخِرَةِ تَرْبَحْهُمَا جَمِيعًا، ولا تَبِعِ الآخِرَةَ بِالدُّنْيَا فَتَخْسَرَهُمَا جَمِيعًا
“Son of Ādam, sell the dunyā for the ākhira and you win both; do not sell the ākhira for the dunyā, or you lose both.”
An athar; cited in the matn (related from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī and from Luqmān al-Ḥakīm)

The order matters

Some of the salaf said: you need your share of the dunyā, but you need your share of the ākhira more. Begin with your share of the dunyā and you squander your share of the ākhira and remain in danger even of the dunyā; begin with your share of the ākhira and you win your share of the dunyā too, neatly arranged.

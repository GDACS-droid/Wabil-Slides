The Gift He Will Not Accept

The image Ibn al-Qayyim presses

“Is the servant not ashamed,” he asks, “to give to a creature like himself a dead slave, or a dead servant-girl?” What does he imagine the one he meant it for — a king, a prince, anyone — would make of such a gift? Just so, he says, is the prayer empty of khushūʿ, presence, and a heart gathered upon Allah: it is like that dead gift offered to a king. “For this reason Allah does not accept it from him” — even if it discharges the obligation in the rulings of this world — “and does not reward him for it, for the servant has of his prayer only what he was conscious of in it.”

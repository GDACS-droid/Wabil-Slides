Group breakout
Where does love of other-than-Allah actually compete in me — and what does it cost me when it wins?
  • Name one concrete seam from this week where two loves tore apart — your desire, or pleasing someone whose approval you need, against what Allah loves. Which way did your hand move, and what did the collision feel like?
  • Ibn al-Qayyim says the preferred beloved is “made bitter” on the one who chose it over Allah. Can you name a time a thing you grabbed at the cost of obedience arrived spoiled — never quite the pleasure you expected?
  • Is it possible to love family, a teacher, or your own good name and keep love of Allah first? Where exactly is the line between an ordered love and a rival love?
Ten to twelve minutes in your group. Bring back one honest, specific example — not a theory.

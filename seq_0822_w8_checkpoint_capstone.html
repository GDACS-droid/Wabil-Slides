Capstone practicum
Now build it. From only what the book has given us tonight, each person writes a personal daily وِرْد on one page — small enough to actually keep.
  • A morning set and an evening set — choose 3–4 phrases each from Set 1 that you will say after Ṣubḥ and after ʿAṣr. Be honest about how much you'll truly keep.
  • A ṣalāh-linked set — pick at least one from Set 3 that ties to a prayer you already pray (answering the adhān, or اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ after the salām).
  • One situational duʿāʾ — choose a single comprehensive supplication for when it is hard: distress, grief, rizq, or the firmness of the heart. Name when you will reach for it.
Twelve minutes. Write it down; do not leave it in your head. Read your wird aloud to one partner and have them check it is small enough to last.

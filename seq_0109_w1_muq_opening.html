The Muqaddima

Three States — the Banner of a Happy Servant

He does not open with a definition. He opens with a دُعَاء for his friend, and the thing he asks for holds the whole moral world of the book.
أَنْ يَجْعَلَكُمْ مِمَّنْ إِذَا أَنْعَمَ اللهُ عَلَيْهِ شَكَرَ، وَإِذَا ابْتُلِيَ صَبَرَ، وَإِذَا أَذْنَبَ اسْتَغْفَرَ
“…that He make you among those who, when Allah blesses them, give thanks; when tried, are patient; and when they sin, seek forgiveness.”
Ibn al-Qayyim, opening of al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

Why these three, and only these

He calls them عُنْوَانَ سَعَادَةِ العَبْدِ — the very banner of a servant's happiness — and says لَا يَنْفَكُّ عَبْدٌ عَنْهَا أَبَدًا: no servant is ever free of them. His logic is total: at any moment you are in a blessing, or a trial, or a sin. There is no fourth box. So if these three responses are sound, your whole life is sound; the religion is simply how you meet whichever of the three you are standing in right now.

A Sin That Becomes a Door

When Allah wills good for a servant after he falls, He opens to him a door of التَّوْبَةِ وَالنَّدَمِ وَالاِنْكِسَارِ وَالذُّلِّ وَالاِفْتِقَارِ — repentance, regret, brokenness, lowliness, and need — until the very sin becomes a cause of mercy, and the enemy says: “Would that I had left him and not made him fall.”

How a sin can outweigh many obediences

A servant sins and the sin stays before his eyes — afraid of it, grieving, weeping, ashamed before his Lord, head bowed, heart broken. That brokenness can be more beneficial to him than many acts of worship. The danger is not the falling alone; it is falling and then standing tall in self-satisfaction.

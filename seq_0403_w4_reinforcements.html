The Struggle

He Did Not Leave Him Undefended

Then the mercy of his Lord required that He aid the slave with another army to fight off the army bent on his ruin.
  • He sent him His Messenger and sent down to him His Book.
  • He supported him with a noble angel who faces the enemy: when shayṭān commands by his command, the angel commands by the Lord's command and shows him the ruin in obeying the foe.
  • Against the nafs al-ammāra He set a nafs muṭmaʾinna — when one orders evil, the other forbids it; when one forbids good, the other commands it.
  • Against hawā He gave light, insight, and intellect (نُورٌ وبَصِيرَةٌ وعَقْلٌ) that call out: Beware! the ruins and the highwaymen lie ahead if you follow that guide.

“The one given victory is whom Allah aids; the one kept safe is whom Allah preserves.” The war is back and forth — but the slave is not abandoned to it.

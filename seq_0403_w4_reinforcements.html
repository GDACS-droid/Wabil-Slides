The Struggle

He Did Not Leave Him Undefended

Then the mercy of his Lord required that He aid the slave with another army to fight off the army bent on his ruin — matching every besieger with its defender.
  • He sent him His Messenger and sent down to him His Book — the outward guidance.
  • Against شَيْطَان He set a noble angel: when the enemy commands by his command, the angel commands by the Lord's command and shows the slave the ruin that lies in obeying the foe.
  • Against the النَّفْس الأَمَّارَة He set a نَفْس مُطْمَئِنَّة — when one orders evil, the other forbids it; when one forbids good, the other commands it. The slave obeys this one once and that one once, and sometimes one of the two is overpowered for good.
  • Against هَوَى He gave light, insight, and intellect (نُورٌ وبَصِيرَةٌ وعَقْلٌ) that call out: Beware! ruin and the highwaymen lie ahead if you walk behind that guide.

“The one given victory is whom Allah aids; the one kept safe is whom Allah preserves” (المَنْصُورُ مَنْ نَصَرَهُ اللَّهُ، والمَحْفُوظُ مَنْ حَفِظَهُ اللَّهُ). The war is back and forth — but the slave is never abandoned to it, and both armies were given to him by the same Lord.

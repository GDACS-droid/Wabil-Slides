The Three Degrees

Heart and Tongue Together, Then the Heart Alone

  • Highest: dhikr upon which the heart and the tongue agree (مَا تَوَاطَأَ عَلَيْهِ القَلْبُ وَاللِّسَان).
  • Second: dhikr of the heart alone.
  • Third: dhikr of the tongue alone.

Why does the heart's dhikr outweigh the tongue's? Because, he says, “the dhikr of the heart yields knowledge, stirs love, sows shame, awakens fear, calls to vigilance, and restrains from falling short in obedience and from making light of sins.” The tongue's dhikr alone yields none of this — “and if it bears any of it, its fruit is weak.”

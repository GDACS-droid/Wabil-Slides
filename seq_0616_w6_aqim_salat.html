Benefits 55–56

Every Act Was Legislated to Establish Remembrance

وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي
“And establish the prayer for My remembrance.”
Sūrat Ṭā Hā 20:14

The لَام is causal — and the rites confirm it

Ibn al-Qayyim weighs the readings of the لَام and settles on لَامُ التَّعْلِيل: the prayer is established for the sake of remembering Him. Salmān, asked which deed is best, answered: “Do you not recite the Qurʾān? — وَلَذِكْرُ اللَّهِ أَكْبَر (al-ʿAnkabūt 29:45).” And ʿĀʾisha reports the Prophet ﷺ saying the طَوَاف, the سَعْي between Ṣafā and Marwa, and the casting of pebbles “were appointed only لِإِقَامَةِ ذِكْرِ اللَّه — to establish the remembrance of Allah” (Abū Dāwūd and al-Tirmidhī, who graded it حَسَن صَحِيح). So 56 follows: the best of any people in a deed are those who remember Allah most in it — the best of fasters, the best of givers, the best of pilgrims — “ذَهَبَ الذَّاكِرُونَ بِالخَيْرِ كُلِّهِ: the rememberers carried off all the good.”

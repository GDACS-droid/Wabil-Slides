Where the Door Stands Open

If ghafla is the master-door, the question for a serious ṭālib is concrete: when, in my day, does the door swing open? The enemy does not need a dramatic crisis. He needs an idle minute the heart has stopped guarding.
Today

Notice the reflex: a quiet moment arrives — waiting in line, a lull at work, the seconds after the prayer ends — and the hand reaches for the phone before the heart has formed a single thought of Allah. That reflex is the unguarded door. Nothing forbidden has happened yet; that is the point. Ghafla is not a sin you commit, it is a watch you stop keeping, and the appetite and the anger walk in behind it. The cure is not to despise the ordinary minute but to claim it: a word of istighfār, a سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ, a glance of the heart upward — a lock set on the door before the thief arrives.

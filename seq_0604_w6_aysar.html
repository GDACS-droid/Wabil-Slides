Dhikr lowers the difficult and eases the hard

Ibn al-Qayyim says no hardship was ever met with the remembrance of Allah but it was eased, no difficulty but it was made light, no distress but it lifted, no calamity but it was settled, no anguish but it was relieved — “the remembrance of Allah is relief after hardship, ease after difficulty, and relief after grief and worry.”

أَنَّ ذِكْرَ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يُسَهِّلُ الصَّعْبَ، وَيُيَسِّرُ العَسِيرَ، وَيُخَفِّفُ المَشَاقَّ

Why this matters for us

This is the mercy running through the whole list: the cure of the heart asks the least of the body. A tongue kept moist with His remembrance is within reach of the busy, the poor, and the tired — the burden Ibn al-Qayyim later calls the lightest of any act, with no act richer in delight for the heart.

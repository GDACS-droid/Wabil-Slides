Anwāʿ al-Qulūb

Which One Beats in You

Today

The dark heart is the one in which the conscience has gone quiet: the alarm for fajr is silenced without a flicker of unease, the ḥarām is reached for and nothing inside objects. There are no whispers because there is no battle — the territory was conceded long ago.

Today

The contested heart is most of us: you pray, then scroll; you intend tahajjud, then sleep; you feel the pull of the ḥalaqa and the pull of the feed in the same hour. Some days the day is yours; some days it is the enemy's. The tide goes back and forth — and that very war is a sign the lamp is still lit.

Today

The radiant heart you have met in a few people: someone near whom backbiting dies on the tongue, whose certainty seems to scorch the whisper before it lands. Ibn al-Qayyim's point is not that you are fixed in one heart — it is that the heart is moved between them by what you feed it: dhikr and obedience kindle the lamp; appetite and ghafla bank it down.

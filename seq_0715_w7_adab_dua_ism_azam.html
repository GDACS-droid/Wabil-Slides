The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 2

The Praise Before the Request Is the Greatest Name

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ بِأَنِّي أَشْهَدُ أَنَّكَ أَنْتَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ الأَحَدُ الصَّمَدُ الَّذِي لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ
“O Allah, I ask You — for I bear witness that You are Allah, there is no god but You, the One, the Eternal Refuge, who did not beget nor was begotten, and there is none comparable to Him.”
Ḥadīth of Buraydah — Sunan; ṣaḥḥaḥahu Ibn Ḥibbān & al-Ḥākim

Of the man who prayed thus the Prophet ﷺ said: “He has asked Allah by His greatest Name, by which when He is called He answers, and when He is asked He gives.” And of another who opened, “O Allah, to You belongs all praise, there is no god but You, al-Mannān, Originator of the heavens and earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honour…” he ﷺ said exactly the same (Abū Dāwūd, al-Tirmidhī — ḥasan; ṣaḥḥaḥahu Ibn Ḥibbān & al-Ḥākim).

Two Foundational Rules

It Is the Heart That Weighs

Rule one

  • Deeds outrank one another with Allah by what the hearts carry of faith, sincerity, and love.
  • Two people pray the same prayer; between them in reward is what is between the heavens and the earth.

Rule two

  • A deed wipes away sins in proportion to its completeness — the fuller the deed, the fuller the expiation; the deficient, accordingly.
  • An act drowned in ghafla, void of the sincerity that is its soul, wipes away little or nothing — for what is there in it to expiate?

With these two rules, Ibn al-Qayyim says, many confusions dissolve — such as how ʿArafa fasts “a year before and after” or ʿĀshūrāʾ “a year” for one who repeats them every year. The expiation is conditional: it falls only when the deed met its conditions and no nullifier touched it. The outward form is one; the hearts behind it are not.

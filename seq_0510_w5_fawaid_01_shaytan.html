It Drives Off Shayṭān — the Fortress

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
1
100

It drives Shayṭān off, subdues and breaks him

The Prophet ﷺ likened the one commanded to remember Allah to a man whose enemy comes swiftly on his trail, until he reaches a fortified stronghold and shields himself — “so too, the servant does not shield himself from Shayṭān except by the remembrance of Allah.” Ibn al-Qayyim presses the image: your enemy enters only through the gate of heedlessness. He lies in wait; when you grow heedless he pounces, and when you remember Allah he shrinks and cowers and is subdued — until he becomes as small as a sparrow, even a fly. This is why he is named al-waswās al-khannās: he whispers, and when Allah is remembered he khanasa — he withdraws and recoils. Ibn ʿAbbās ﷺ said the same: “Shayṭān is crouched upon the heart of the son of Adam; when he is heedless, he whispers, and when he remembers Allah, he withdraws.”

كَذَلِكَ الْعَبْدُ لَا يُحْرِزُ نَفْسَهُ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ إِلَّا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ
Today

The gate is still heedlessness. Notice the empty moment — in line, at a red light, the instant after one task ends — and the reflex to reach for the phone to fill it. That reflex is the enemy on the trail. The fortress is to fill the gap with His name instead, so the door he comes through is simply not left open.

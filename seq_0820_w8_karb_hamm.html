When Distress and Grief Press In

اللَّهُمَّ فَارِجَ الهَمِّ، كَاشِفَ الغَمِّ، مُجِيبَ دَعْوَةِ المُضْطَرِّينَ، رَحْمَنَ الدُّنْيَا وَالآخِرَةِ وَرَحِيمَهُمَا، أَنْتَ تَرْحَمُنِي، فَارْحَمْنِي رَحْمَةً تُغْنِينِي بِهَا عَنْ رَحْمَةِ مَنْ سِوَاكَ
“O Allah, Reliever of worry, Remover of grief, Answerer of the call of the distressed, Most Merciful of this world and the next and Compassionate in both — You are the One who shows me mercy, so have mercy on me with a mercy that frees me from the mercy of any besides You.”
Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq — recorded by al-Ḥākim

Abū Bakr asked the Prophet ﷺ for a duʿāʾ to say in hardship; he answered that ʿĪsā عليه السلام used to teach it to his companions, “if one of you had a debt like a mountain of gold and called Allah by it, He would settle it for him.” Note the last line: not merely “relieve me,” but a mercy that frees me from needing the mercy of anyone but You. That is the very independence the heart loses in distress.

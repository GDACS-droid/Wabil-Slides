The Hinge

Enabling and Forsaking

Why does brokenness save one man and conceit destroy another? Ibn al-Qayyim names the hinge. When Allah wills good for this poor servant, He tries him with something that breaks his pride and humbles his neck and makes him small in his own eyes. When He wills otherwise, He leaves him to his conceit — and that abandonment is the cause of his ruin.
التَّوْفِيق
al-tawfīq
that Allah does not leave you to yourself
الخِذْلان
al-khidhlān
that Allah leaves you to yourself

The knowers are unanimous on this

Ibn al-Qayyim reports that the people of maʿrifa are agreed without exception: التَّوْفِيقُ أَنْ لَا يَكِلَكَ اللَّهُ إِلَى نَفْسِكَ، وَالخِذْلَانُ أَنْ يَكِلَكَ اللَّهُ إِلَى نَفْسِكَ. Read this way, the worst thing that can befall a servant is to be handed back to his own management — and the truest mercy is to be kept from it.

Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

Six More Fruits

  • 19 — It effaces sins. Dhikr is among the greatest good deeds, وَالْحَسَنَاتُ يُذْهِبْنَ السَّيِّئَاتِ — the same act that lifts the heart’s grief also lightens its record.
  • 20 — It ends the estrangement. Between the heedless servant and his Lord sits a cold waḥsha that nothing thaws except remembrance.
  • 21 — Your dhikr pleads for you. Your tahlīl, takbīr, and taḥmīd rise around the Throne humming like bees, making mention of the one who said them.
  • 22 — Known in ease, known in hardship. When the remembering servant cries out in distress, the angels say: “A familiar voice from a familiar servant”; of the heedless they say: “A strange voice from a stranger.”
  • 23 — It rescues from the punishment — “no son of Adam has done a deed more saving from the punishment of Allah than His remembrance.”

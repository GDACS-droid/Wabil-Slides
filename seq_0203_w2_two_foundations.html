The Architecture

Love Out Front, Reverence Throughout

First foundation — love

  • أَنْ تَكُونَ مَحَبَّةُ اللَّهِ تَتَقَدَّمُ عِنْدَهُ عَلَى جَمِيعِ المَحَابّ
  • That love of Allah comes ahead of every beloved he has.
  • So that when love of Allah and love of something else collide, سَبَقَ حُبُّ اللَّهِ — love of Allah outruns it, “and he acts on what that love requires of him.”

Second foundation — reverence

  • تَعْظِيمُ الأَمْرِ وَالنَّهْي
  • Magnifying His command and His prohibition.
  • Which, Ibn al-Qayyim says, “grows out of magnifying the One who commands and forbids” — you weigh the order heavily because you weigh the Sender heavily.

Why exactly two — and why this order

Love supplies the motion: it is what makes a heart lean toward Allah at all. Reverence supplies the shape, keeping that love obedient instead of self-styled, bound to the command as it was actually given. Love without reverence drifts into a religion of feelings; reverence without love hardens into cold compliance. Ibn al-Qayyim names love first because it is the root, and reverence second because it proves the root is alive.

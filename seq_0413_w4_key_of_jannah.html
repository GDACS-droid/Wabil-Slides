Tawḥīd

A Key — and Its Teeth

Because shirk is the gravest of the three ledgers, the Garden is forbidden to its people: no soul of shirk enters it. Tawḥīd is the key to its door — and no door opens to a key without teeth.

The teeth of the key

The teeth are: the prayer, fasting, zakāt, ḥajj, jihād, enjoining good and forbidding evil, truthful speech, returning trusts, keeping ties of kin, and dutifulness to parents. Whoever takes in this world a sound key of tawḥīd and sets in it teeth of obedience comes on the Day of Resurrection to the door of the Garden, and nothing bars him from opening it — unless sins remain whose trace tawba and istighfār did not remove here.

It Polishes the Rust from the Heart

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
18
100

It clears the heart of its rust (جَلَاء الْقَلْب)

Abū al-Dardāʾ ﷺ said: “Everything has a polish, and the polish of the hearts is the remembrance of Allah.” Ibn al-Qayyim lays it out as a clean diagnosis: the heart rusts as copper and silver rust. Its rust has two causes — heedlessness and sin; its polish has two means — اِسْتِغْفَار and ذِكْر. And he tells you exactly what the rust does: a rusted heart no longer takes the true shape of things — فَيَرَى الْبَاطِلَ فِي صُورَةِ الْحَقِّ، وَالْحَقَّ فِي صُورَةِ الْبَاطِلِ, it sees falsehood as truth and truth as falsehood. Let the rust pile up unchecked until it hardens into الرَّان, and the heart no longer accepts a truth nor rejects a lie — and that, he says, is the gravest of the heart’s punishments.

لِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ جِلَاءٌ، وَإِنَّ جِلَاءَ الْقُلُوبِ ذِكْرُ اللَّهِ
Today

Rust is gradual and quiet — this is its danger. A sin that once stung is committed now without a flinch; a truth once obvious now feels “debatable.” That dulling is the rust thickening. The two causes are paired by two cures: where sin laid the rust, اِسْتِغْفَار lifts it; where heedlessness laid it, ذِكْر polishes it back to a clear mirror.

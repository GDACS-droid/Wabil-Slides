Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

It Stands Equal to Freeing Slaves and Striking with the Sword

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
43
73

It weighs against the costliest deeds of body and wealth

Dhikr stands in for the freeing of necks, the spending of wealth, and the charge on horseback in Allah's path — and even “the striking with the sword in the path of Allah.” This is not loose praise: Abū al-Dardāʾ carries the very ḥadīth of the Prophet ﷺ — “Shall I tell you of the best of your deeds, the purest before your King, the highest in your ranks, better than spending gold and silver, and better than to meet your enemy and strike his neck and have him strike yours?” They said: yes. He said: ذِكْرُ اللَّهِ.

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ يَعْدِلُ عِتْقَ الرِّقَابِ وَنَفَقَةَ الأَمْوَالِ وَالحَمْلَ عَلَى الخَيْلِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ
لَأَنْ أُسَبِّحَ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى تَسْبِيحَاتٍ أَحَبُّ إِلَيَّ مِنْ أَنْ أُنْفِقَ عَدَدَهُنَّ دَنَانِيرَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ
“That I glorify Allah a number of times is dearer to me than to spend their number in dīnārs in the path of Allah.”
Ibn Masʿūd — recorded by Ibn Abī Shayba in al-Muṣannaf and al-Bayhaqī in al-Shuʿab

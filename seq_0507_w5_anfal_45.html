From the Qurʾān

Dhikr at the Point of the Sword

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا لَقِيتُمْ فِئَةً فَاثْبُتُوا وَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ
“O you who believe — when you meet a host, stand firm and remember Allah much, that you may prosper.”
al-Anfāl 8:45

Why ‘much’ is no accident

Ibn al-Qayyim reads the qualifier closely: Allah does not merely say “remember,” but remember much (كَثِيرًا), tying their falāḥ to two things at once: firmness in the line and abundant dhikr. He pairs it with يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ ذِكْرًا كَثِيرًا (al-Aḥzāb 33:41). The lesson: the heart needs His name most where life is hardest. The harder the moment, the more the tongue must move with it.

From the Qurʾān

Dhikr at the Point of the Sword

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا لَقِيتُمْ فِئَةً فَاثْبُتُوا وَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا لَّعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ
“O you who believe — when you meet a host, stand firm and remember Allah much, that you may prosper.”
al-Anfāl 8:45

The hinge of falāḥ

Allah ties their hope of victory (falāḥ) to two things at once — firmness in the line and abundant dhikr. As Ibn al-Qayyim reads it, the command to remember much (كَثِيرًا) is no accident: the heart most needs His name exactly where life is hardest.

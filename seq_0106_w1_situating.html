How We Read Him

Faithful, and Contextualized

In the text

al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib is a Ḥanbalī work, written by a student of Ibn Taymiyya. On a few questions — certain creedal asides, an occasional remark on “the people of kalām” — its author writes from his own school, which on those points differs from the Ashʿarī reading.

How we read it

The heart of this book — the living heart, the fruits of dhikr, the prophetic adhkār — is the shared inheritance of the whole umma, and we receive it warmly. Where a point genuinely diverges, we will name the view briefly and move on. We engage the idea, never the man, and we do not look for conflict where there is none.

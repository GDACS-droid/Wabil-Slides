The Decree of ʿAwf ibn Mālik

When “Allah Suffices Me” Is Praised — and When It Is Blamed

The حَدِيث of ʿAwf ibn Mālik

The Prophet ﷺ judged between two men. As the one ruled against turned away he said, “Allah is enough for me, and the best Disposer of affairs (حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الوَكِيلُ).” The Prophet ﷺ said: “إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَلُومُ عَلَى العَجْزِ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَيْكَ بِالكَيْسِ، فَإِذَا غَلَبَكَ أَمْرٌ فَقُلْ: حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الوَكِيلُ — Allah blames incapacity; take to shrewdness and effort, but when a matter overcomes you, then say: Allah is enough for me, and the best Disposer.” (Abū Dāwūd and al-Nasāʾī, with a حَسَن chain.)

Ibn al-Qayyim draws the fine line: say حَسْبِيَ اللَّه after taking the means you were commanded, and you are praised and profit by both the deed and the word. But if you are weak, abandon the means, and then say it, you say it while blamed for abandoning the means Allah's wisdom required — “and the word does not benefit you as it benefits the one who acted.” The very same words are worship in one mouth and an excuse in another; what divides them is whether the hands moved first.

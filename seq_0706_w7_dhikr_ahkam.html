The Second Kind · His Command

Remembering His Command and Prohibition

Reporting His ruling

  • Remembering that He commanded this, forbade that, loves this, is angered by that.
  • This is the knowledge of the scholars — studying and teaching what Allah loves and hates: مُدَارَسَةُ العِلْم.

Acting upon it

  • Remembering Him at His command — hastening to obey; and at His prohibition — fleeing from it.
  • This is standing at His limits: الوُقُوفُ عِنْدَ حُدُودِ اللَّه when the moment of action comes.

Ibn al-Qayyim says: when these two come together in one servant — knowing the ruling, and answering it in the moment — “his dhikr is the noblest dhikr, the greatest in benefit. This is the fiqh al-akbar.” To remember His command is not abstract recall; it is to live answerable to Him.

Benefit 61 · A Gap We Skipped

Remembrance That Strengthens the Body Itself

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
61
73

It gives the rememberer a strength he does not have without it

Ibn al-Qayyim says dhikr gives the rememberer power, “so that he does with it what he could not do without it.” His proof is the ḥadīth of the mill: when Fāṭima asked the Prophet ﷺ for a servant, complaining of the grinding and labour of her hands, he taught her and ʿAlī to say, on going to bed, سُبْحَانَ اللَّه thirty-three, الحَمْدُ لِلَّه thirty-three, and اللَّهُ أَكْبَر thirty-four, and said: هُوَ خَيْرٌ لَكُمَا مِنْ خَادِم — “it is better for the two of you than a servant” (al-Bukhārī and Muslim). It is said: whoever keeps to it finds in his body a strength that dispenses with a servant.

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ يُعْطِي الذَّاكِرَ قُوَّةً حَتَّى إِنَّهُ لَيَفْعَلُ مَعَهُ مَا لَا يُطِيقُ فِعْلَهُ بِدُونِهِ
The word for the impossible task

He pairs this with لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّه, “a phrase of wondrous effect” in bearing hard labour, entering upon rulers and those one fears, and facing terrors. He reports the āthār of Ḥabīb ibn Maslama, who would say it when he met an enemy or assaulted a fortress, until at one fortress the Muslims said it and cried اللَّهُ أَكْبَر and its wall split open. So when a task looms larger than you — the diagnosis you must deliver, the deadline you cannot meet, the door you dread to knock on — this is the phrase to put your hand on it with: there is no power and no strength except by Allah.

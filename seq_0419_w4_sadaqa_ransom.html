Ṣadaqa & Sakhāʾ

Its Proof Is That It Happens

Returning to the fourth parable: charity is “speech whose proof is its existence” — ṣadaqa has a wondrous effect in turning away kinds of affliction, even from a sinner or a disbeliever, as people high and low have tried and found.
إِنَّ الصَّدَقَةَ تُطْفِئُ غَضَبَ الرَّبِّ، وتَدْفَعُ مِيتَةَ السُّوءِ
“Charity extinguishes the anger of the Lord, and wards off an evil death.”
Recorded by al-Tirmidhī (#664): ḥasan gharīb; ṣaḥḥaḥahu Ibn Ḥibbān

As the captive ransomed himself with little and much, so ṣadaqa ransoms the slave from Allah's punishment: his sins call for his ruin, and his charity comes and frees him from it. Hence the Prophet ﷺ urged the women on the day of ʿĪd: “Give charity, even from your jewelry” — as if spurring them to ransom themselves from the Fire.

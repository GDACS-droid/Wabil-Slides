Opening

He Comes Only by Ambush

Ibn al-Qayyim opens with a striking image: the enemy of Allah cannot break into the believer head-on. He gets at him the way a thief gets at a sleeping man — غِيلَةً عَلَى غِرَّةٍ وَغَفْلَةٍ, by stealth, in a moment of carelessness and heedlessness.

The believer is in Allah's keeping

The Qurʾān denies Iblīs any real sulṭān (authority) over the servants of Allah; the believer is in His protection and under His shade. So when the enemy does land a blow, it is never by overpowering the believer — only by catching him asleep at the door.

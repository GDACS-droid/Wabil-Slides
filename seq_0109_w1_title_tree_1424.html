The Title's Root

A Good Word Like a Good Tree

أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ ضَرَبَ اللَّهُ مَثَلًا كَلِمَةً طَيِّبَةً كَشَجَرَةٍ طَيِّبَةٍ أَصْلُهَا ثَابِتٌ وَفَرْعُهَا فِي السَّمَاءِ
“Do you not see how Allah sets forth a parable: a goodly word is like a goodly tree, its root firm and its branches in the sky.”
a-lam tara kayfa ḍaraba llāhu mathalan kalimatan ṭayyibatan ka-shajaratin ṭayyibatin aṣluhā thābitun wa-farʿuhā fī s-samāʾ
Sūrat Ibrāhīm 14:24

Root and fruit

The goodly word is a tree with its root firm in the heart and its branches in the sky. That root is faith and the remembrance of Allah; its fruit is the good a believer says and does. The whole book is a meditation on how to keep that tree watered.

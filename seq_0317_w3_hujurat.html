Muḥbiṭāt al-Aʿmāl

Cancelled While Unaware

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَرْفَعُوا أَصْوَاتَكُمْ فَوْقَ صَوْتِ النَّبِيِّ وَلَا تَجْهَرُوا لَهُ بِالْقَوْلِ كَجَهْرِ بَعْضِكُمْ لِبَعْضٍ أَن تَحْبَطَ أَعْمَالُكُمْ وَأَنتُمْ لَا تَشْعُرُونَ
“O you who believe, do not raise your voices above the voice of the Prophet, nor be loud with him in speech as you are loud with one another, lest your deeds be nullified while you do not perceive it.”
Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt 49:2

Loud raising of the voice was no apostasy

Allah warned the believers that a deed can be undone وَأَنْتُمْ لَا تَشْعُرُونَ — and this was not apostasy, only a disobedience that voids the deed while the doer feels nothing. Ibn al-Qayyim presses the lesson: what then of one who places another's word, way, and guidance above the word and way of the Messenger ﷺ? He cites مَنْ تَرَكَ صَلَاةَ العَصْرِ فَقَدْ حَبِطَ عَمَلُهُ (al-Bukhārī, from Buraydah), and ʿĀʾisha's word to Zayd b. Arqam over a sale of ʿīna — that he had voided his jihād with the Messenger ﷺ unless he repented — neither of which is apostasy, only a sin that wipes a deed away.

Faṣl 4 & 5 · al-Fazaʿ, al-Qalaq, al-Ruʾyā

When You Cannot Sleep, and When the Dream Frightens You

Khālid b. al-Walīd complains of insomnia

Khālid — the unbeaten general — came to the Prophet ﷺ and said: “O Messenger of Allah, I cannot sleep at night from الأَرَق” — restlessness. The Prophet ﷺ taught him to say at the bed: اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ السَّمَاوَاتِ السَّبْعِ وَمَا أَظَلَّتْ، وَرَبَّ الأَرَضِينَ وَمَا أَقَلَّتْ، وَرَبَّ الشَّيَاطِينِ وَمَا أَضَلَّتْ، كُنْ لِي جَارًا مِنْ شَرِّ خَلْقِكَ كُلِّهِمْ جَمِيعًا (al-Tirmidhī).
  • For night-terror and the frightened child. أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ غَضَبِهِ وَعِقَابِهِ، وَشَرِّ عِبَادِهِ، وَمِنْ هَمَزَاتِ الشَّيَاطِينِ وَأَنْ يَحْضُرُونِ — Ibn ʿAmr taught it to those of his children who could memorize, and wrote it and hung it on the younger ones (Abū Dāwūd & al-Tirmidhī, ḥasan).
  • For the dream that disturbs you. “The good dream is from Allah, the bad from Shayṭān.” So: turn to your left, spit dryly three times, seek refuge in Allah from its evil — “and it will not harm you” — tell no one of it, and do not lie on the side you were on (al-Bukhārī & Muslim, from Abū Qatāda).

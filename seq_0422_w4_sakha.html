Ṣadaqa & Sakhāʾ

Whoever Is Saved from His Own Shuḥḥ

وَمَن يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ
“And whoever is guarded against the greed of his own soul — it is they who are the successful.”
Sūrat al-Ḥashr 59:9 (also al-Taghābun 64:16)

Two kinds of generosity

ʿAbd al-Raḥmān b. ʿAwf circled the Kaʿba with one prayer: “My Lord, guard me from the greed of my soul” — for, he said, when I am guarded from it I have succeeded. Ibn al-Qayyim ranks sakhāʾ in two: the higher is to be indifferent to what is in others' hands; the second is to give freely of what is in your own. The generous one is near to Allah, near to people, near to the Garden; the miser is far from all of these.

The Prayer

Two Kinds of Turning Away

Returning to the second word — “when you pray, do not turn away” — Ibn al-Qayyim says the forbidden turning (الالْتِفَات) is of two kinds, and both are forbidden.

Turning of the heart

  • The heart turns from Allah to other than Allah — drifting to the matter, the worry, the plan.
  • Allah keeps facing His slave as long as the slave faces his prayer; when he turns away with his heart, Allah turns from him.

Turning of the gaze

  • The eyes wander right and left from the place of prostration.
  • Asked about a man turning in his prayer, the Prophet ﷺ said: “It is a snatching by which shayṭān steals from the slave's prayer.”

“Allah keeps facing His slave so long as the slave faces his prayer; when he turns aside with heart or gaze, Allah turns from him.” And in an athar: when the slave turns away, it is as if Allah says — “To something better than Me? To something better than Me?”
The “snatching” ḥadīth: recorded by al-Bukhārī (#751) from ʿĀʾisha رضي الله عنها.

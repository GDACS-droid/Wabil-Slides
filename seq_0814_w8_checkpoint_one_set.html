Group breakout
Ibn al-Qayyim calls these مُوَظَّفَة — appointed, fixed to a time. The whole point is that they are kept, not admired.
  • Of the morning–evening and sleep–waking adhkār, which two have you actually kept before — and what made them stick, when others slipped?
  • Where in a real day does the morning set keep dying on you (the commute? the phone? the rush)? Name the exact moment, and one concrete fix that ties the dhikr to something already in that moment.
  • If a person could keep only three phrases for the rest of their life, which three from tonight would your group choose, and why those?
Ten minutes. Bring back your group's three, and one honest reason these adhkār slip.

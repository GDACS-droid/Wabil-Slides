The Muqaddima

Three Things, the Mark of a Happy Servant

Ibn al-Qayyim opens by praying that his friend be made among a certain kind of person — and the description holds the whole moral world of the book.
أَنْ يَجْعَلَكُمْ مِمَّنْ إِذَا أَنْعَمَ اللهُ عَلَيْهِ شَكَرَ، وَإِذَا ابْتُلِيَ صَبَرَ، وَإِذَا أَذْنَبَ اسْتَغْفَرَ
“…that He make you among those who, when Allah blesses them, give thanks; when tried, are patient; and when they sin, seek forgiveness.”
an yajʿalakum mimman idhā anʿama llāhu ʿalayhi shakara, wa-idhā btuliya ṣabara, wa-idhā adhnaba istaghfara
Ibn al-Qayyim, opening of al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

The three states

He calls these three عُنْوَانَ سَعَادَةِ العَبْدِ — the very banner of a servant's happiness. The servant, he says, never leaves these three states; he is always turning between blessing, trial, and slip, and his religion is how he meets each one.

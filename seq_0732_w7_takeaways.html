Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry This Week

  • Praise before you ask, every time. Begin every duʿāʾ with the praise of Allah by His Names, then ṣalāt on the Prophet ﷺ, then the request — “this one has hurried” is the warning against rushing to the need.
  • Fold the asking inside the praise, and add your poverty. As Mūsā, Dhū-l-Nūn and Ādam did — it is the greatest Name and nearer to being answered.
  • Weigh dhikr by the One remembered, and bring the heart. Prefer the gathering phrases — سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ — and seek the dhikr the heart agrees to, not the tongue alone.
  • Put each act in its place. The lesser act in its moment can be the better act; give every right its right, and let the prayer gather them all.
  • Tie the camel, then trust. “Be keen, seek Allah's help, do not be weak” — then, when overtaken, “قَدَرُ اللَّهِ وَمَا شَاءَ فَعَلَ.” Keep the heart turning back in tawba, and ask above all for al-ʿafw wa-l-ʿāfiya.

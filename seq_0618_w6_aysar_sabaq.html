Benefit 59

It Makes the Hard Easy and Lightens Every Difficulty

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
59
73

It lowers the difficult and lightens the hard

No hardship was ever met with the remembrance of Allah but it was eased; no difficulty but it was made light, no distress but it lifted, no calamity but it was settled, no anguish but it was relieved — “dhikr is relief after hardship, ease after difficulty, and relief after grief and worry.”

أَنَّ ذِكْرَ اللَّهِ يُسَهِّلُ الصَّعْبَ وَيُيَسِّرُ العَسِيرَ وَيُخَفِّفُ المَشَاقَّ

Easiest in this world, greatest in the next

Notice the paradox the benefit holds: the very act that lightens every weight is itself the lightest of acts to perform — a word on the tongue, costing neither wealth nor toil. So dhikr is at once the easiest road and the one that smooths every other road; the servant who keeps it finds his hardships met halfway. And precisely because it is so easy, it is the most regretted when neglected — which is where Ibn al-Qayyim turns next, to the Day the covering is lifted.

Sakīna Is the Sum of Every Fruit

The arithmetic of the list

Read the fruits back as a single line and they all terminate here. The breach is closed (38), the scattered is gathered (39), the hard is eased (59), the heart is cured (47), the face is lit (70), the enemy is driven off (73) — and a heart with no breach, nothing scattered, nothing hard, no sickness, no siege, is, by definition, a heart at rest: تَطْمَئِنُّ القُلُوب. This is not a new topic after the fruits; it is their sum. Sakīna is what is left when remembrance has done its work.

Today

You can tell whether the work has been done by the shape of an ordinary anxious day. Two people receive the same hard news — a job lost, a worrying result. The heart still scattered grabs for the phone, paces, replays the worry, sleeps badly, snaps at the people nearest. The heart that has kept its remembrance does not stop feeling the weight, but it does not shatter under it: it returns to لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّه, the worry loosens its grip, and there is a floor under the fear. That floor is the sakīna Allah lets down on a remembering heart. Trouble does not vanish, but underneath it sits a settledness the heedless heart does not have.

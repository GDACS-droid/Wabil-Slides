Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

It Brings Him Near — It Opens a Vast Door

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
12
100

It bequeaths nearness (al-qurb) to Him

عَلَى قَدْرِ ذِكْرِهِ لِلَّهِ يَكُونُ قُرْبُهُ مِنْهُ، وَعَلَى قَدْرِ غَفْلَتِهِ يَكُونُ بُعْدُهُ مِنْهُ. The measure is exact: as much as a servant remembers Allah, that much is his nearness; as much as he is heedless, that much is his distance. (Benefit 11, before it: dhikr bequeaths ināba — the heart’s returning to Allah as the refuge it flees to in every trial.)

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
13
100

It opens a vast door of maʿrifa

يَفْتَحُ لَهُ بَابًا عَظِيمًا مِنْ أَبْوَابِ الْمَعْرِفَةِ. Ibn al-Qayyim is emphatic: the more a servant remembers, the more his knowledge of Allah increases. Dhikr is not the opposite of ʿilm — it is one of its springs. (And Benefits 14–15: it bequeaths awe of the Lord, and earns His remembrance of you.)

The Heart's Return

Tawba — Turning the Heart Back to Allah

The same confession that opens an answered duʿāʾ is the substance of tawba: the heart turning back to its Lord. Ibn al-Qayyim places the duʿāʾ the Prophet ﷺ taught Abū Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him) at the meeting-point of asking and returning:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي ظُلْمًا كَثِيرًا، وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، فَاغْفِرْ لِي مَغْفِرَةً مِنْ عِندِكَ وَارْحَمْنِي، إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الغَفُورُ الرَّحِيمُ
“O Allah, I have wronged myself with much wrong, and none forgives sins but You; so forgive me a forgiveness from Yourself, and have mercy on me — truly You are the Forgiving, the Merciful.”
al-Bukhārī & Muslim, from Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq

Ibn al-Qayyim: this noble supplication “gathers the confession of one's state and the seeking of one's Lord by His bounty and generosity, that He alone forgives sins — then the request, after this twofold appeal. Such is the etiquette of servanthood.” Tawba is not despair; it is the broken heart turning home, certain that the door of mercy is open.

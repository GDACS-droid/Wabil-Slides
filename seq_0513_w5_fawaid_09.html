It bequeaths the love (al-maḥabba) — the spirit of the dīn

Ibn al-Qayyim calls love the soul of Islam, the axle on which the whole dīn turns, the pivot of felicity and salvation. Then he gives the means: Allah has appointed a cause for everything, and the cause of love is the constancy of dhikr. “Whoever wishes to attain the love of Allah, let his tongue cleave to His remembrance.” Just as study and review (al-mudhākara) are the gateway of knowledge, dhikr is the gateway of love — its widest road and its straightest path.

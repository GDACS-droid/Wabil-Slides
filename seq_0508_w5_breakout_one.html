Group breakout
The Prophet ﷺ ranked dhikr above charity, above battle, above “meeting your enemy and striking their necks.” Sit with that, honestly.
  • Why do we instinctively rate the dramatic, costly deed (the sword, the great donation) above the quiet tongue? What does that instinct reveal about how we measure worth?
  • Ibn al-Qayyim’s ruling is that dhikr is the soul inside the great deed, not its competitor. Where in your own ʿibāda is the body present but the remembrance thin?
  • If a struggling old man is told to hold to just one rope — “keep your tongue moist” — what is the one realistic rope you could hold to this week?

The Muqaddima

A Prayer Counted as Half, or a Tenth

The clearest proof that a deed is weighed by the heart within it is one Ibn al-Qayyim takes from the prayer itself — where two men may stand the same length and walk away with very unequal shares written for them:
إِنَّ العَبْدَ لَيُصَلِّي الصَّلَاةَ وَمَا كُتِبَ لَهُ إِلَّا نِصْفُهَا، إِلَّا ثُلُثُهَا، إِلَّا رُبُعُهَا، إِلَّا خُمُسُهَا، حَتَّى بَلَغَ عُشْرَهَا
“A servant may pray the prayer and have written for him only half of it, only a third, only a quarter, only a fifth — until he reaches a tenth.”
inna l-ʿabda la-yuṣallī ṣ-ṣalāta wa-mā kutiba lahu illā niṣfuhā, illā thuluthuhā, illā rubuʿuhā, illā khumusuhā, ḥattā balagha ʿushrahā
Abū Dāwūd no. 796, al-Nasāʾī, and Aḥmad, from ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir; graded ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān

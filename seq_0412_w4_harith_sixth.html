Ḥadīth al-Ḥārith al-Ashʿarī

The Prophet's Own Five

“…and I command you with five Allah commanded me”

The Prophet ﷺ said: “And I command you with five that Allah has commanded me: hearing and obeying (السَّمْعُ والطَّاعَةُ), jihād, hijra, and the jamāʿa; for whoever leaves the jamāʿa by a handspan has slipped the yoke of Islam from his neck unless he returns, and whoever calls with the call of jāhiliyya is of the kindling of Hell.”

A man asked, “Even if he prays and fasts?” He said, “Even if he prays and fasts. So call with the call of Allah, who named you the Muslims, the believers, the slaves of Allah.”

Ibn al-Qayyim calls this “the ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth of mighty import, which every Muslim ought to memorize and master”, for it names exactly what rescues a slave from shayṭān and wins him success in this life and the next. The first five are the worship that joins the slave to his Lord; these five are what hold the believing community itself together.
Recorded by Aḥmad and al-Tirmidhī (#2863); ṣaḥḥaḥahu al-Tirmidhī, Ibn Khuzayma, Ibn Ḥibbān, and al-Ḥākim.

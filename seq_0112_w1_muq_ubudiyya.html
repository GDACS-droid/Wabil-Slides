The Muqaddima

Servanthood Has Two Faces — and a Test

In what you love (al-sarrāʾ)

  • Allah has over the servant a عُبُودِيَّة in ease — gratitude for blessing, worship in comfort, spending on a family you love.
  • أَكْثَرُ الخَلْقِ يُعْطُونَ العُبُودِيَّةَ فِيمَا يُحِبُّونَ — “most people give servanthood in what they love.” This is the easy face, and almost everyone passes it.

In what you hate (al-ḍarrāʾ)

  • Allah has over the servant a عُبُودِيَّة in hardship too — patience under what he dislikes, restraint when the soul burns for a sin “without fear of people.”
  • وَالشَّأْنُ فِي إِعْطَاءِ العُبُودِيَّةِ فِي المَكَارِهِ — “the real matter is giving servanthood in the things one hates.” Here the ranks of servants are told apart.

His own example, and the verdict

He gives a homely case: وُضُوء with cold water in the heat is عُبُودِيَّة; وُضُوء with cold water in the cold is also عُبُودِيَّة — وَلَكِنْ فَرْقٌ عَظِيمٌ بَيْنَ العُبُودِيَّتَيْنِ. Same act, two weights. The trial was never sent to break you: لَمْ يَبْتَلِهِ لِيُهْلِكَهُ، وَإِنَّمَا ابْتَلَاهُ لِيَمْتَحِنَ صَبْرَهُ وَعُبُودِيَّتَهُ — “He did not try him to destroy him, but to test his patience and his servanthood.”

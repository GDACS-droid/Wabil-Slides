Faṣl 1 · “No Calamity Will Strike Him”

Abū al-Dardāʾ and the House That Did Not Burn

“It has not burned”

A man came running to Abū al-Dardāʾ: “O Abū al-Dardāʾ — your house has caught fire!” He answered without alarm: “It has not burned. Allah would not do that — because of words I heard from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: whoever says them at the start of the day, no calamity will strike him until evening; and whoever says them at the day's end, no calamity will strike him until morning.” The words were: اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، عَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ، وَأَنْتَ رَبُّ العَرْشِ العَظِيمِ، مَا شَاءَ اللَّهُ كَانَ، وَمَا لَمْ يَشَأْ لَمْ يَكُنْ، لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ… — and the fire, the narration tells, did not reach his house.

Ibn al-Qayyim brings this from Ibn al-Sunnī, and the muḥaqqiq notes its chain is weak — so we hold the reward lightly and the tawakkul firmly. What is sound and instructive is the man's certainty: he had handed the day to Allah at its start, so when the report came he did not flinch. The lesson points to a heart that has already said عَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ and means it, not to a charm against fire.

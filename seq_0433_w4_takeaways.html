Takeaways

What to Carry Out the Door

  • You are besieged from within by shayṭān, hawā, and the nafs al-ammāra — but armed with Messenger, Book, angel, the nafs muṭmaʾinna, and light. The captive is the one who hands over his own hand; so fight at the first call. The watch is short.
  • Only a sliver of the day remains, and on the Day it will feel like part of a day. Do not sell the ākhira for the dunyā; begin with the ākhira and win both.
  • Memorize the ḥadīth of al-Ḥārith al-Ashʿarī — tawḥīd, prayer, fasting, charity, dhikr — and the Prophet's five: hearing & obeying, jihād, hijra, jamāʿa.
  • Tawḥīd is the key to the Garden; obedience is its teeth — you need the word and the works. In prayer, fight the turning of heart and gaze; aim for rank three and above. The true fast guards the tongue, not just the belly.
  • Guard the “contested house” — shayṭān fights you only with weapons you keep inside it. As you treat people, so Allah treats you.
  • This week's wird: give one ṣadaqa daily — “even half a date” — and watch the chest open; and once a day pray, رَبِّ قِنِي شُحَّ نَفْسِي — “My Lord, guard me from the greed of my soul.”

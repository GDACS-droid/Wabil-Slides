The Decree of ʿAwf ibn Mālik

When “Allah Suffices Me” Is Praised — and When It Is Blamed

The ḥadīth of ʿAwf ibn Mālik

The Prophet ﷺ judged between two men. As the one ruled against turned away he said: “Allah is enough for me, and the best Disposer of affairs (ḥasbiya llāhu wa-niʿma l-wakīl).” The Prophet ﷺ said: “إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَلُومُ عَلَى العَجْزِ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَيْكَ بِالكَيْسِ، فَإِذَا غَلَبَكَ أَمْرٌ فَقُلْ: حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الوَكِيلُ — Allah blames incapacity; take to shrewdness and effort, but when a matter overcomes you, then say: Allah is enough for me, and the best Disposer.”

Recorded by Abū Dāwūd and al-Nasāʾī. Ibn al-Qayyim draws the fine line: “If he says ḥasbiya llāh after taking the means he was commanded, he says it and is praised, and profits by both the deed and the word. But if he is weak, abandons the means, and says it, he says it while blamed for abandoning the means that Allah's wisdom required — and the word does not benefit him as it benefits the one who acted.”

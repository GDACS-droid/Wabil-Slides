Ḥiqārat al-Dunyā

The Trade That Wins or Loses Both

ابْنَ آدَمَ، بِعِ الدُّنْيَا بِالآخِرَةِ تَرْبَحْهُمَا جَمِيعًا، ولا تَبِعِ الآخِرَةَ بِالدُّنْيَا تَخْسَرْهُمَا جَمِيعًا
“Son of Ādam, sell the dunyā for the ākhira and you win both; do not sell the ākhira for the dunyā, or you lose both.”
An athar; in the matn, from al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī and from Luqmān al-Ḥakīm

Why the order decides everything

Some of the سَلَف said: you need your share of the دُنْيَا, but you need your share of the آخِرَة more. Begin with your share of the دُنْيَا and you squander your share of the آخِرَة and remain in danger even of the دُنْيَا; begin with your share of the آخِرَة and you win your share of the دُنْيَا too, neatly arranged (فَانْتَظَمْتَهُ انْتِظَامًا). The دُنْيَا is not forbidden; the point is that whatever you put first eats whatever you put second.

In practice

The same fifty hours of work, begun from “I will earn lawfully and pray on time and give my family their right”, wins both the wage and the آخِرَة; begun from “the دِين can wait until I am established”, it quietly costs the prayers, the family, and finally even the peace the money was meant to buy. Same act, reordered, two destinations.

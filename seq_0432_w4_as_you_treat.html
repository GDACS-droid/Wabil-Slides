Allah Loves the Generous

Generosity is a divine attribute reflected in the slave: Allah gives and is not given to, feeds and is not fed; the most beloved of creation to Him are those who take on the trace of His names. He is Generous and loves the generous, Merciful and shows mercy to the merciful, Forgiving and loves those who forgive His servants.
مَنْ سَتَرَ مُسْلِمًا سَتَرَهُ اللَّهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا والآخِرَةِ، ومَنْ نَفَّسَ عَنْ مُؤْمِنٍ كُرْبَةً مِنْ كُرَبِ الدُّنْيَا نَفَّسَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ كُرْبَةً مِنْ كُرَبِ يَوْمِ القِيَامَةِ
“Whoever conceals a Muslim, Allah conceals him in this world and the next; whoever relieves a believer of a distress of this world, Allah relieves him of a distress of the Day of Resurrection.”
Recorded by Muslim (#2699) from Abū Hurayra رضي الله عنه
A corollary

Ibn al-Qayyim's rule: as you treat, so you are treated (كَمَا تَدِينُ تُدَانُ). Allah is to His slave according to how the slave is to His creation. Forgive people, He forgives you; ease a debtor, He shades you on the Day; cover a fault, He covers yours; chase people's faults, He exposes yours. So choose, in every dealing, the treatment you will need from Allah, since you are, in effect, writing your own receipt.

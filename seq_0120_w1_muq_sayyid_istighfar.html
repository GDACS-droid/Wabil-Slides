The Muqaddima

Both Wings in a Single Breath

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي، فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
“O Allah, You are my Lord, there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant; I keep Your covenant and Your promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil I have done. I acknowledge to You Your favor upon me, and I acknowledge my sin — so forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.”
al-Bukhārī no. 6323, from Shaddād ibn Aws

Read it with the two wings now visible

Ibn al-Qayyim brings this ḥadīth — the master of all words of forgiveness — as living proof of the two wings, and once you see them you cannot un-see them: أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ, “I acknowledge Your favor upon me,” is the witnessing of the minna; وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي, “and I acknowledge my sin,” is the inspecting of one's own fault. Gratitude and brokenness laid side by side in a single breath. This is the wird we will leave with tonight.

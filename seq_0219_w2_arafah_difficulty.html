The Principle Applied

Resolving an Old Difficulty

صَوْمُ يَوْمِ عَرَفَةَ يُكَفِّرُ سَنَتَيْن، وَيَوْمُ عَاشُورَاءَ يُكَفِّرُ سَنَة
“Fasting the Day of ʿArafah expiates two years, and the Day of ʿĀshūrāʾ expiates a year.”
Recorded by Muslim 1162, from Abū Qatāda

The puzzle and the answer

Some asked: if a man habitually fasts ʿArafah and ʿĀshūrāʾ every year, how is three years' worth wiped out each year? Ibn al-Qayyim's reply is the two foundations: expiation is conditioned — bound to the presence of the heart and the absence of nullifiers. A deed swallowed by heedlessness, void of the sincerity that is its soul, “what does it expiate?” The reckoning is not in the bare act, but in guarding the act from what spoils it.

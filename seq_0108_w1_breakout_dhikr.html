Group breakout
Before Ibn al-Qayyim defines it for us — what do we mean by ذِكْر, and by a living heart?
  • Is dhikr only words on the tongue, or something of the heart too? Can you have one without the other — a tongue that moves while the heart sleeps?
  • What does a heart that is alive feel like, against one that is heedless? Name the difference from your own week, honestly.
  • If dhikr is “rain on the heart,” what is the “good earth” it needs to take root? What, in a week, packs that earth hard so the rain runs off?
Ten minutes in your group. Choose one answer to bring back — we will test it against the text as we go.

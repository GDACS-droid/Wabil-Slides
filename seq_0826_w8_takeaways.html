Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry — For Good

  • Keep the وِرْد you built tonight — small and daily beats large and abandoned. Begin tomorrow with the morning set after صُبْح, where you prayed, before you stand.
  • Anchor the two ends of the day: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ ×100, and سَيِّدُ الاِسْتِغْفَار — “whoever says it with certainty and dies that day enters the Garden.”
  • Tie remembrance to what you already do — the doorway, the مَسْجِد, the أَذَان, and the end of every prayer: اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ — the دُعَاء that begs the help to keep the rest.
  • Keep one دُعَاء for the hard nights — اللَّهُمَّ فَارِجَ الهَمِّ، كَاشِفَ الغَمِّ — and one for the heart itself: يَا مُقَلِّبَ القُلُوبِ ثَبِّتْ قَلْبِي عَلَى دِينِكَ. Memorize them now, before the night you need them.
  • Remember what the whole book was for: not أَذْكَار collected, but a heart given life. Guard the وِرْد, and let it guard your heart.

Fawāʾid al-Dhikr · 53–54

He Boasts of Them to the Angels; They Enter جَنَّة Laughing

Benefit 53 — Muʿāwiya and the circle in the mosque

Muʿāwiya came upon a circle in the mosque and asked what had seated them. “We sat to remember Allah.” By Allah, nothing seated you but that? “By Allah, nothing but that.” He said: I did not make you swear out of suspicion; but no one was closer to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in rank, nor narrated less from him, than I — and the Messenger ﷺ came upon a circle of his Companions and asked the same, and they answered the same, and he said: “I did not make you swear out of suspicion of you, but Jibrīl came to me and told me that اللَّهَ تَبَارَكَ وَتَعَالَى يُبَاهِي بِكُمُ المَلَائِكَة — Allah is boasting of you to the angels.” (Recorded by Muslim.)

Benefit 54 — the constant rememberer enters laughing

Abū al-Dardāʾ: الَّذِينَ لَا تَزَالُ أَلْسِنَتُهُمْ رَطْبَةً مِنْ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ يَدْخُلُ أَحَدُهُمُ الجَنَّةَ وَهُوَ يَضْحَك — “those whose tongues never dry of Allah's remembrance — one of them enters the Garden laughing.” That Allah's boasting of a thing is proof of its rank with Him and His love of it; and that the reward of a moist tongue is to arrive at the gate already smiling.

Benefit 62

“Nothing Was Easier” — and the Rememberers Win the Race

إِذَا انْكَشَفَ الغِطَاءُ لِلنَّاسِ يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ عَنْ ثَوَابِ أَعْمَالِهِمْ لَمْ يَرَوْا عَمَلًا أَفْضَلَ ثَوَابًا مِنَ الذِّكْرِ، فَيَتَحَسَّرُ عِنْدَ ذَلِكَ أَقْوَامٌ فَيَقُولُونَ: مَا كَانَ شَيْءٌ أَيْسَرَ عَلَيْنَا مِنَ الذِّكْرِ
“When the covering is lifted from people on the Day of Rising over the reward of their deeds, they will see no deed greater in reward than dhikr. At that, a people will fill with regret and say: nothing was easier for us than remembrance.”
ʿUmar mawlā Ghufra (benefit 62, the image of the racers) — cited by Ibn al-Qayyim; the editor notes the narrator is weak
The corollary he draws

Their regret is not over a hard thing they failed at, but over the easiest thing of all left undone. Ibn al-Qayyim frames benefit 62 with the racers: in the run, dust and grit hide who is ahead — “but when the dust settles, people see that the rememberers carried off the prize.” The lesson for now: do not judge your standing by the dust of this life, where the rememberer often looks like he is doing the least. The settling is coming.

Faṣl al-Khiṭāb

When Dhikr Outweighs the Sword

لَوْ ضَرَبَ بِسَيْفِهِ فِي الْكُفَّارِ وَالْمُشْرِكِينَ حَتَّى يَنْكَسِرَ وَيَخْتَضِبَ دَمًا، كَانَ الذَّاكِرُ لِلَّهِ تَعَالَى أَفْضَلَ مِنْهُ دَرَجَةً
It was asked which servant is highest in rank with Allah on the Day of Rising. He ﷺ said: “Those who remember Allah much.” It was said: “And the warrior in the path of Allah?” He said: “Even were he to strike with his sword among the disbelievers until it broke and he was dyed in blood, the one who remembers Allah would be higher than him in rank.”
From Abū Saʿīd; recorded by al-Tirmidhī — the taḥqīq notes a weak chain (the route of Darrāj)

Why the comparison stuns

The warrior here is at the absolute ceiling of sacrifice — sword shattered, body bloodied, life given. And the one who remembers Allah is placed above even him. Ibn al-Qayyim does not let this stand as a slogan; on the next slide he resolves it precisely, so that no one imagines the seated dhākir is simply better than the martyr.

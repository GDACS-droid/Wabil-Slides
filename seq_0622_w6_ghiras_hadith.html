Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

ذِكْر Builds the Homes of the Garden and Plants Its Trees

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
64
73

Its houses are built by ذِكْر; its orchards planted by it

“The homes of the Garden are built by ذِكْر; when the rememberer stops, the angels stop building, and when he resumes, they resume.” And as its houses are built by remembrance, so its orchards are planted by it — “فَالذِّكْرُ غِرَاسُهَا وَبِنَاؤُهَا: ذِكْر is both its planting and its building.”

أَنَّ دُورَ الجَنَّةِ تُبْنَى بِالذِّكْرِ، فَإِذَا أَمْسَكَ الذَّاكِرُ أَمْسَكَتِ المَلَائِكَةُ عَنِ البِنَاءِ

The mason who builds only while you speak

Sit with the first of the two images on its own. The building of your house in the Garden is not done all at once; it rises stone by stone as you remember, and the moment you fall silent the angels set down their tools — “when the rememberer stops, the angels stop building, and when he resumes, they resume.” So the size of the house is never settled in advance; the length of the remembrance measures it out, course upon course, exactly as far as the tongue carries it.

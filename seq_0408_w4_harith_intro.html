Ḥadīth al-Ḥārith al-Ashʿarī

Five Words for the Children of Israel

Ibn al-Qayyim now brings the long ḥadīth that gathers, in five parables, all that guards the slave from his enemy. Learn it whole.

The setting

Allah commanded Yaḥyā b. Zakariyyā with five words, to act on them and command the Children of Israel to act on them. Yaḥyā was slow to convey them, until ʿĪsā عليه السلام urged him: Either you command them, or I will. Yaḥyā said, I fear that if you precede me I will be punished. So he gathered the people in Bayt al-Maqdis until the masjid was full and they sat on the balconies, and he said: “Allah has commanded me with five words, to act on them and command you to act on them.”

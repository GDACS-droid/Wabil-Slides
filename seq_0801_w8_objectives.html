Orientation · The Capstone

Where we're going

  • Open the final chapter of the book — الأَذْكَارُ المُوَظَّفَة, the appointed adhkār, which Ibn al-Qayyim says a servant ought never to neglect, لِشِدَّةِ الحَاجَةِ إِلَيْهَا وَعِظَمِ الانْتِفَاعِ فِي الآجِلِ وَالعَاجِلِ بِهَا — “for the severity of his need of them and the immensity of the benefit in them, in the next life and this.”
  • Walk the faṣls in order: the two ends of the day; sleep, the bed, and waking; the terror of the night and the bad dream; leaving and entering the home; the door of the masjid; the adhān and the prayer.
  • Gather the jawāmiʿ duʿāʾ al-Nabī ﷺ — the comprehensive supplications that draw rizq, beg refuge from every evil, and keep the heart firm — the very ones he says to treasure when people are treasuring gold.
  • Capstone practicum: each of you assembles a personal daily wird on one page — a morning set, an evening set, a ṣalāh-linked set, and one situational duʿāʾ — drawn entirely from this book.
  • Close, after eight weeks, with one honest look at the heart that the wird is meant to keep alive.

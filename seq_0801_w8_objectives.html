Orientation · The Capstone

Where we're going

  • Open the final section of the book — الأَذْكَارُ المُوَظَّفَة, the appointed adhkār Ibn al-Qayyim says “the servant should never neglect, for his great need of them and the immense benefit in them now and hereafter.”
  • Walk faṣl by faṣl through the highest-value, most practical: the two ends of the day (morning & evening), sleep & waking, leaving & entering the home, entering & leaving the masjid, the adhān, and the things said in distress.
  • Gather the jawāmiʿ duʿāʾ al-Nabī ﷺ — the comprehensive supplications — that draw rizq, ward off harm, and steady the heart.
  • Capstone practicum: each of you assembles a personal daily wird — a morning set, an evening set, a ṣalāh-linked set, and one situational — entirely from what the book gives.
  • Close, after eight weeks, with one honest look at the heart.

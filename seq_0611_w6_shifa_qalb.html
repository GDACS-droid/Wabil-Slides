Dhikr Is the Cure of the Heart; Heedlessness Its Sickness

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
47
73

The heart is sick; its medicine is dhikr

“Dhikr is the heart's cure and its remedy, and heedlessness is its sickness.” Just before this (benefit 46), Ibn al-Qayyim says there is in the heart a hardness that nothing melts but the remembrance of Allah — and when the heart remembers, that hardness melts “as lead melts in fire.” Here he names the deeper truth: hearts are diseased, and their cure and their medicine lie in remembering Allah.

أَنَّ الذِّكْرَ شِفَاءُ القَلْبِ وَدَوَاؤُهُ، وَالغَفْلَةَ مَرَضُهُ

The poet's complaint

“إِذَا مَرِضْنَا تَدَاوَيْنَا بِذِكْرِكُمُ / فَنَتْرُكُ الذِّكْرَ أَحْيَانًا فَنَنْتَكِسُ — When we sickened we were healed by remembering you; then we leave the remembrance awhile, and relapse.”

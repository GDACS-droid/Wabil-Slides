Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

It Pleases Him, Lifts the Grief, Brings the Joy

  • Benefit 2 — It pleases al-Raḥmān. Before dhikr is a balm for you, it is a thing beloved to Him; the tongue moving with His name is itself what earns His good pleasure.
  • Benefit 3 — It removes anxiety and grief from the heart. يُزِيلُ الْهَمَّ وَالْغَمَّ عَنِ الْقَلْبِ. The clouded heart, heavy with worry over what was and dread of what comes, finds that weight lift under His remembrance — the heart was made to rest in Him and rests nowhere else.
  • Benefit 4 — It brings the heart joy, gladness, and expansion. الْفَرَحَ وَالسُّرُورَ وَالْبَسْطَ. Beyond lifting the grief of Benefit 3, dhikr gladdens and expands the heart. The heedless heart is constricted (maqbūḍ); the heart in dhikr is expanded (mabsūṭ) with the gladness of a heart that has found its Lord.

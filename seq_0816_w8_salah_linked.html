Faṣl 9 · Tied to the Ṣalāh

The Adhān, the Waṣīla, and ṣalāh ʿalā al-Nabī ﷺ

إِذَا سَمِعْتُمُ المُؤَذِّنَ فَقُولُوا مِثْلَ مَا يَقُولُ، ثُمَّ صَلُّوا عَلَيَّ؛ فَإِنَّ مَنْ صَلَّى عَلَيَّ صَلَاةً صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ بِهَا عَشْرًا، ثُمَّ سَلُوا اللَّهَ لِيَ الوَسِيلَةَ
“When you hear the muezzin, say the like of what he says, then send ṣalāt upon me — for whoever sends one ṣalāt upon me, Allah sends ten upon him — then ask Allah for me the waṣīla.”
ʿAbdullāh b. ʿAmr — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 384

So the prayer is wrapped in dhikr at both ends: answer the adhān word for word, send ṣalāt on the Prophet ﷺ, then the duʿāʾ of the waṣīla — اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ هَذِهِ الدَّعْوَةِ التَّامَّةِ وَالصَّلَاةِ القَائِمَةِ… (al-Bukhārī). The book's other prayer-adhkār — the opening duʿāʾ, the words of rukūʿ and sujūd, the istikhāra — belong to this same appointed set; the Prophet ﷺ even gave Muʿādh words for after the salām.

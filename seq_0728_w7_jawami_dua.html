Faṣl 75 · Jawāmiʿ al-Duʿāʾ

Ask With the Words That Gather Every Good

ʿĀʾisha reports that the Prophet ﷺ “loved the comprehensive supplications and left what was between them.” When Saʿd's son asked at length for Paradise and its chambers and refuge from the Fire and its chains, Saʿd warned him of iʿtidāʾ — overstepping in duʿāʾ — and taught the gathering form instead:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مِنَ الخَيْرِ كُلِّهِ مَا عَلِمْتُ مِنْهُ وَمَا لَمْ أَعْلَمْ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الشَّرِّ كُلِّهِ مَا عَلِمْتُ مِنْهُ وَمَا لَمْ أَعْلَمْ
“O Allah, I ask You for all good — what I know of it and what I do not — and I seek refuge in You from all evil — what I know of it and what I do not.”
From the ḥadīth of Saʿd — recorded by Abū Dāwūd & Aḥmad

And among the Prophet's ﷺ own gathering duʿās: “اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الهَمِّ وَالحَزَنِ، وَالعَجْزِ وَالكَسَلِ، وَالبُخْلِ وَالجُبْنِ، وَضَلَعِ الدَّيْنِ وَغَلَبَةِ الرِّجَالِ” (al-Bukhārī & Muslim) — eight ills, each pair a door the heart can slip through. He asked refuge from grief and inability, from miserliness and cowardice, from crushing debt and the domination of men.

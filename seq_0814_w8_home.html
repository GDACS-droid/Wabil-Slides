Faṣl 6 & 7 · The Doorway

Leaving the Home, Entering the Home

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ
“In the name of Allah; I rely upon Allah; there is no power and no strength except by Allah.” — said on leaving the house, “he is told: you are sufficed, guided and protected, and the devil withdraws from him.”
Anas — recorded by Abū Dāwūd & al-Tirmidhī, ḥasan

On entering, Ibn al-Qayyim cites Jābir: when a man enters and remembers Allah at his coming in and at his food, the devil says: no lodging for you and no supper here. But if he forgets Allah at his entry, the devil says: you have reached your lodging. So enter with His name, and greet your household with salām — “a blessing upon you and upon your house.”

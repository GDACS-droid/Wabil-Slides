Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

Five More Fruits of Remembrance

  • 19 — It effaces sins. ذِكْر is among the greatest of good deeds, and إِنَّ الْحَسَنَاتِ يُذْهِبْنَ السَّيِّئَاتِ — “indeed, good deeds drive away evil ones” (Hūd 11:114): the same act that lifts the heart’s grief also lightens its record.
  • 20 — It ends the estrangement (الْوَحْشَة). Between the heedless servant and his Lord sits a cold distance, and nothing thaws it but remembrance. Neither activity nor company removes the chill; only turning back to His name does.
  • 21 — Your ذِكْر pleads for you. Your تَهْلِيل, تَكْبِير, and تَحْمِيد rise and circle about the Throne with a hum like the humming of bees, making mention of the one who said them. “Would one of you not love to have something there making mention of him?”
  • 22 — Known in ease, known in hardship. Who keeps Allah’s acquaintance in his ease is recognised by Him in his distress: when the remembering servant cries out, the angels say, “A familiar voice from a familiar servant”; of the heedless, “A strange voice from a stranger.”
  • 23 — It rescues from the punishment. As Muʿādh ﷺ reported: مَا عَمِلَ آدَمِيٌّ عَمَلًا أَنْجَى لَهُ مِنْ عَذَابِ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى مِنْ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ — no son of Adam has done a deed more saving from Allah’s punishment than His remembrance.

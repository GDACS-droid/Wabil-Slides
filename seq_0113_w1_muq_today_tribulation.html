The Muqaddima

Where the Ranks Are Told Apart

Today

A young father is laid off the same month the rent rises. Anyone can be a grateful servant while the salary lands on time. That is the easy face, the worship of al-sarrāʾ. The thing being weighed now is the harder face: does he keep his tongue off complaint about his Lord while still asking Him plainly for relief? Does he hold his hands back from a haram income “without fear of people,” when no one would know? Does he keep his Fajr through the worry instead of letting the worry replace it? None of this lifts the hardship by itself — but it is exactly the ʿubūdiyya fī-l-makārih the author says sets servants apart. The same months that crush one man refine another.

The corollary

So a trial is not a pause in a servant's worship; it is a different chapter of it, and often the chapter in which his rank is truly settled. The same hardship the author calls a test of ṣabr and ʿubūdiyya is, for the one who meets it well, the means by which his standing with Allah is raised.

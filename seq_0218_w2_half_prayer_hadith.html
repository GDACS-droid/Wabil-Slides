The Proof

Half, a Third, Down to a Tenth

He grounds “the servant has of his prayer only what he was conscious of” in a marfūʿ hadith reported, he notes, فِي السُّنَنِ وَمُسْنَدِ الإِمَامِ أَحْمَد — in the Sunan and the Musnad of Aḥmad:
إِنَّ العَبْدَ لَيُصَلِّي الصَّلَاةَ وَمَا كُتِبَ لَهُ إِلَّا نِصْفُهَا، إِلَّا ثُلُثُهَا، إِلَّا رُبُعُهَا، إِلَّا خُمُسُهَا، حَتَّى بَلَغَ عُشْرَهَا
“A servant prays the prayer, and there is written for him only half of it — only a third of it, only a fourth, only a fifth” — until he reached “only a tenth of it.”
Recorded by Abū Dāwūd, al-Nasāʾī (al-Kubrā), and Aḥmad, from ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir; graded ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān

The principle drawn from it

Two people stand in the same row, pray the same outward prayer, and walk away with unequal portions before Allah — one with all of it, the next with a tenth. The act is one; its weight is set by how much of the heart was present: لَيْسَ لَهُ مِنْ صَلَاتِهِ إِلَّا مَا عَقَلَ مِنْهَا. The heedless portion does not invalidate the prayer in fiqh; it is simply not written, quietly subtracted from the reward.

Faṣl 3 · Waking

The First Words on Waking

الحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَحْيَانَا بَعْدَمَا أَمَاتَنَا وَإِلَيْهِ النُّشُورُ
“Praise is Allah's, who gave us life after He had caused us to die, and to Him is the rising.”
Ḥudhayfa — Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 6324

Ibn al-Qayyim sets a whole faṣl on the words of الانْتِبَاه — waking. For one who stirs in the night: لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ… الحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ وَسُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ، وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ, then “O Allah forgive me” or any duʿāʾ — “it is answered” (ʿUbāda b. al-Ṣāmit; al-Bukhārī). Sleep and waking, he shows, are a small death and a small resurrection — and the believer marks both with His Name.

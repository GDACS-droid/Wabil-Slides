Faṣl 1 · Entering the Evening

“We Have Entered the Evening, and the Kingdom Is Allah's”

أَمْسَيْنَا وَأَمْسَى المُلْكُ لِلَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ المُلْكُ وَلَهُ الحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ، رَبِّ أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَ مَا فِي هَذِهِ اللَّيْلَةِ وَخَيْرَ مَا بَعْدَهَا، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا فِي هَذِهِ اللَّيْلَةِ وَشَرِّ مَا بَعْدَهَا
“We have entered the evening, and the kingdom belongs to Allah. Praise be to Allah; there is no god but Allah alone, no partner to Him — His is the kingdom and His the praise, and He is over all things powerful. My Lord, I ask You for the good of this night and what follows it, and take refuge in You from its evil and what follows it.”
Ibn Masʿūd — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 2723

In the morning he would say it the same way — أَصْبَحْنَا وَأَصْبَحَ المُلْكُ لِلَّهِ — one phrase turned from evening to morning, setting the whole stretch of time ahead under Allah's kingship before the day's worries reach you.

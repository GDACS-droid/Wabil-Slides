Entering Paradise by a Sin

إِنَّ العَبْدَ لَيَعْمَلُ الذَّنْبَ يَدْخُلُ بِهِ الجَنَّةَ، وَيَعْمَلُ الحَسَنَةَ يَدْخُلُ بِهَا النَّارَ. قَالُوا: كَيْفَ؟ قَالَ: يَعْمَلُ الذَّنْبَ فَلَا يَزَالُ نُصْبَ عَيْنَيْهِ، خَائِفًا مِنْهُ، نَادِمًا، مُنْكَسِرَ القَلْبِ لَهُ.
“A servant may commit a sin by which he enters Paradise, and do a good deed by which he enters the Fire. They asked: How? He said: He commits the sin, and it stays before his eyes — fearful of it, regretful, his heart broken over it; so that sin becomes the cause of his happiness. And he does the good deed and never stops boasting of it before his Lord, growing proud and vain — so it becomes the cause of his ruin.”
Saying of the salaf; recorded as a mursal of al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī by Aḥmad in al-Zuhd and Ibn al-Mubārak in al-Zuhd

The same act can carry opposite fruit. It is not the deed on paper that decides, but what it leaves in the heart: a sin that leaves brokenness, or a virtue that leaves conceit.

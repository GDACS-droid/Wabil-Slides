Faṣl 1 · Anwāʿ al-Dhikr

Remembrance Is of Five Kinds

Ibn al-Qayyim gathers the remembrance of Allah, across this book and Jalāʾ al-Afhām, into five kinds — every one of them “the servants' remembrance of their Lord” (هِيَ ذِكْرُهُمْ لِرَبِّهِمْ):
  • 1 — His names and attributes: praising Him by them, declaring Him One by them, and exalting Him above all that does not befit Him.
  • 2 — His command and prohibition: remembering the lawful and the forbidden, what He loves and what He is angered by — the ذِكْر of the people of knowledge.
  • 3 — His blessings and favours: remembering His gifts, His kindness, and where His bounty has fallen on His servants.
  • 4 — His own speech: the Qurʾān is itself His remembrance — وَمَنْ أَعْرَضَ عَن ذِكْرِي (Q 20:124), where “My remembrance” is His speech revealed to His Messenger ﷺ.
  • 5 — Calling upon Him: supplication, seeking forgiveness, and pleading with Him (الدُّعَاءُ وَالاسْتِغْفَارُ وَالتَّضَرُّع) — itself a remembrance of the One called upon.

And each of these can be done in three degrees: with the heart and tongue together — “that is the highest” — or with the heart alone, or with the tongue alone. The fullest ذِكْر is the one upon which the heart and the tongue agree.

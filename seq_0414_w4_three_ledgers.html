The Three Ledgers of Wrong

إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَغْفِرُ أَن يُشْرَكَ بِهِ وَيَغْفِرُ مَا دُونَ ذَٰلِكَ لِمَن يَشَاءُ
“Indeed, Allah does not forgive that a partner be associated with Him, but He forgives what is less than that for whom He wills.”
Sūrat al-Nisāʾ 4:48
  • A ledger Allah does not forgive — شِرْك; He does not forgive that a partner be set up with Him.
  • A ledger Allah leaves nothing of — the wrongs of people against one another; He exacts every right in full, and it is wiped out only by leaving the wrong and seeking release from those wronged.
  • A ledger Allah may overlook — the slave's wrong against himself, between him and his Lord; the lightest and swiftest to erase, wiped out by تَوْبَة, اِسْتِغْفَار, the good deeds that efface, and trials that expiate.
A corollary

So the wrong people most fear — a private sin between them and Allah — is the most forgivable of the three; and the wrong people most easily shrug off — money withheld, a reputation torn, a sibling cut off — is the one Allah leaves nothing of until it is returned. Settle the debts you owe people now; for that ledger does not close at اِسْتِغْفَار.

Ṣadaʾ al-Qalb

Two Things Rust It, Two Polish It

No doubt the heart rusts as copper and silver rust, says Ibn al-Qayyim, and its polish is dhikr — it polishes it until it leaves it كَالمِرْآةِ البَيْضَاءِ, like a bright mirror. Leave the dhikr and it rusts again; resume it and it shines. The whole cure of this week's diseases turns out to be a single discipline.

It rusts by two

  • الغَفْلَة — heedlessness.
  • الذَّنْب — sin.
  • When heedlessness rules a person's hours, the rust piles up by the measure of his ghafla — until the heart can no longer take the true shapes of things: it sees falsehood as truth and truth as falsehood.

It is polished by two

  • الاسْتِغْفَار — seeking forgiveness.
  • الذِّكْر — remembrance.
  • These two return the heart to its clarity, so the forms of realities are impressed in it as they truly are. The two openings the enemy used, ghafla and sin, are the very ones these close.

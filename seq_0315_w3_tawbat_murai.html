Tawbat al-Murāʾī

Does the Reward Return?

Two cases, two rulings

Ibn al-Qayyim takes up a hard question: if a man's deed was spoiled by riyāʾ and then he repents, does its reward come back?

If he did the deed for other than Allah from the start, repentance does not turn it good again; the most repentance does is wipe away its punishment, so it stands neither for him nor against him.

But if he did it sincerely for Allah, and then conceit or showing-off crept in afterward, and then he repented and regretted — its reward may return to him and not be lost. He notes the scholars also held that one may simply need to do the deed anew.

The principle beneath it: good deeds and bad deeds push against one another, and the ruling goes to whichever overcomes. Sincere repentance, risen from the depth of the heart, can burn away what sin passed over it — for the one who repents from a sin is like one who has no sin.

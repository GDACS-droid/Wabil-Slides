Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry This Week

  • Praise before you ask. Begin every duʿāʾ with the praise of Allah by His Names, ṣalāt on the Prophet ﷺ, then the request — “this one has hurried” is the warning against rushing to the need.
  • Let the asking ride inside the praise. Add an honest confession of your poverty, as Mūsā, Dhū-l-Nūn and Ādam did — it is nearer to being answered.
  • Weigh your dhikr by the One remembered. Prefer the gathering phrases — سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ — and seek the dhikr of the heart, not the tongue alone.
  • Put each act in its place. The lesser act in its moment can be the better act; let the prayer gather them all.
  • Take the means, then surrender. “Be keen, seek Allah's help, do not be weak” — then, when overtaken, “قَدَرُ اللَّهِ وَمَا شَاءَ فَعَلَ.” And keep the heart turning back to Him in tawba, asking for al-ʿafw wa-l-ʿāfiya.

Takeaways

What to Carry Out the Door

  • This is a letter on the heart and its dhikr by a Damascene master of tazkiyah, student of Ibn Taymiyya, and we receive its tazkiyah as the umma's shared inheritance.
  • The title pictures the goodly word rising up (Q 35:10) and a downpour of mercy coming down on the heart that says it.
  • The servant's whole life is three states — shukr, ṣabr, istighfār — and his rank is set by his ʿubūdiyya in what he dislikes, not only in what he loves.
  • An upright heart rests on two pillars: loving Allah above all, and magnifying His command and prohibition.

This week's wird

Take on Sayyid al-Istighfār once each morning and once each evening — slowly, with its meaning. Let it be the first thread of the daily wird we build over the coming weeks.

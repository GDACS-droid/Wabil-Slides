Abundant Remembrance Is a Safety from Hypocrisy

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
68
73

The hypocrites are described by the scarcity of their dhikr

Ibn al-Qayyim's argument runs from the Qurʾān's own description. Allah marked the hypocrites by how little they remember Him: “وَلَا يَذْكُرُونَ اللَّهَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا” (al-Nisāʾ 4:142). Kaʿb said: مَنْ أَكْثَرَ ذِكْرَ اللَّهِ بَرِئَ مِنَ النِّفَاق — “whoever remembers Allah much is acquitted of hypocrisy.” And he closes it with a beautiful reasoning: Allah is too generous to afflict a remembering heart with nifāq — that lot is reserved for hearts heedless of His remembrance.

أَنَّ كَثْرَةَ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ أَمَانٌ مِنَ النِّفَاقِ

Why al-Munāfiqūn is sealed with this

He notes that Allah sealed the chapter of the hypocrites precisely here: “يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُلْهِكُمْ أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَلَا أَوْلَادُكُمْ عَن ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَفْعَلْ ذَٰلِكَ فَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْخَاسِرُونَ” (al-Munāfiqūn 63:9) — a warning against the very fitna that made the hypocrites hypocrites: heedlessness of remembrance. Asked whether the Khawārij were hypocrites, a Companion answered: “No — the hypocrites remember Allah but little.”

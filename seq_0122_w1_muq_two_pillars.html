The Muqaddima

Servanthood Rests on Love and Lowliness

حُبٌّ كَامِلٌ
complete love — born of witnessing Allah's favor (the مِنَّة)
ذُلٌّ تَامٌّ
total lowliness — born of inspecting the soul's faults

How the wings become the foundations

Servanthood, he says, turns on two roots that are its essence: حُبٌّ كَامِلٌ وَذُلٌّ تَامٌّ — complete love and total lowliness. And these two are not new; they grow straight out of the two wings: the مِنَّة a servant witnesses gives birth to the love, and the faults he inspects give birth to the lowliness. Love without lowliness drifts into presumption; lowliness without love curdles into despair. عُبُودِيَّة is the two held together. From this the author turns to ask: by what does the heart actually stand straight so it can carry this load?

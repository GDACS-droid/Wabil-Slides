Opening the Section

Adhkār a Servant Should Never Let Slip

Ibn al-Qayyim titles this closing section فِي الأَذْكَارِ المُوَظَّفَةِ الَّتِي لَا يَنْبَغِي لِلْعَبْدِ أَنْ يُخِلَّ بِهَا — the adhkār fixed to their times that a servant ought never to neglect.

Why a wird at all

The whole book has been about giving life to the heart by remembrance. Here it becomes a daily shape: not random remembering, but adhkār appointed to moments — the dawn, the dusk, the doorway, the bed. The Prophet ﷺ tied each to its time so the believer's day is held, end to end, by the Name of Allah. Our task tonight is to take a manageable set of these and make them genuinely ours.

Tawḥīd

The Pure and the Foul

وَسِيقَ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰ إِذَا جَاءُوهَا وَفُتِحَتْ أَبْوَابُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا سَلَامٌ عَلَيْكُمْ طِبْتُمْ فَادْخُلُوهَا خَالِدِينَ
“And those who feared their Lord will be driven to the Garden in throngs, until, when they reach it and its gates are opened, its keepers say to them: Peace be upon you; you have been good, so enter it to abide forever.”
Sūrat al-Zumar 39:73

Ibn al-Qayyim notes the fāʾ of cause in طِبْتُمْ فَادْخُلُوهَا: because of your purity you are told, enter. The Garden is the abode of the pure (دَارُ الطَّيِّبِينَ); only the pure enter it. The Fire, by contrast, is the abode of the foul — foul in speech, deeds, food, and drink; Allah sifts the khabīth from the ṭayyib, heaps the foul one upon another, and casts it all into Hell. Three sorts of people, therefore three abodes: the purely good, the purely foul — and a third house, for those in whom good and foulness are mixed; that house alone passes away, for no sinner of the muwaḥḥidūn remains in the Fire forever.

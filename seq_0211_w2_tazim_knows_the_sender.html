Foundation Two

How You Treat the Command Reveals Whom You Know

The chain Ibn al-Qayyim draws

He unfolds his teacher's words into a chain. The first rank of magnifying Allah is magnifying His command and prohibition — أَوَّلُ مَرَاتِبِهِ. Why? Because the believer comes to know his Lord through the message He sent with His Messenger ﷺ to all people, and what that message demands is submission: obey the command, keep clear of the prohibition. So a believer's magnifying of the order is not a separate piety added on top. It is the visible proof دَالًّا of his magnifying the One whose order it is. You cannot see a man's awe of Allah directly; you read it off how he handles Allah's command.

And by that measure he is ranked

By the size of that reverence, Ibn al-Qayyim says, a man stands among الأَبْرَار — “the righteous, witnessed to have faith and truthfulness, soundness of creed, and freedom from the greater hypocrisy.” The تَعْظِيم is not just a sign of إِيمَان; it is the gauge that ranks one believer above another.

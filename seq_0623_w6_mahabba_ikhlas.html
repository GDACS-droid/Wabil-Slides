From Remembrance to Love to Sincerity

المَحَبَّة
al-maḥabba
love of Allah — born of constant remembrance
الإِخْلَاص
al-ikhlāṣ
sincerity — the heart turned to Him alone

Ibn al-Qayyim has already shown the chain (benefit 48): “the servant keeps remembering his Lord until he loves Him and befriends Him.” Dhikr is the root of maḥabba. And when the tongue is moist with His name and the heart is settled on Him, the love of created things finds no room to settle in beside it — the heart is purified for Him alone. So remembrance ripens into love, and love into ikhlāṣ: the inward freedom that the whole of al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib was written to plant.

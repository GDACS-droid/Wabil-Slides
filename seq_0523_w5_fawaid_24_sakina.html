Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

It Brings Down Tranquillity — and Guards the Tongue

لَا يَقْعُدُ قَوْمٌ يَذْكُرُونَ اللَّهَ إِلَّا حَفَّتْهُمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ، وَغَشِيَتْهُمُ الرَّحْمَةُ، وَنَزَلَتْ عَلَيْهِمُ السَّكِينَةُ، وَذَكَرَهُمُ اللَّهُ فِيمَنْ عِنْدَهُ
“No people sit remembering Allah but that the angels encircle them, mercy covers them, tranquillity descends upon them, and Allah mentions them among those who are with Him.”
From Abū Hurayrah and Abū Saʿīd; recorded by Muslim (#2700)
Benefits 24 & 25

24 — the descent of سَكِينَة. Four gifts fall on a circle of ذِكْر at once: the angels encircle, mercy covers, tranquillity descends, and Allah mentions them above. 25 — a tongue spared. The tongue will move. That is its nature. If it is not kept with His remembrance, it does not fall silent; it turns to backbiting, slander, and idle falsehood. So ذِكْر is also what spares the tongue — the one safe place for a tongue that cannot stay still.

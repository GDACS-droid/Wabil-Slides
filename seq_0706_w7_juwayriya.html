Juwayriya · Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ

Four Words That Outweigh a Morning of Dhikr

The Prophet ﷺ returns to Juwayriya

He ﷺ left Juwayriya (may Allah be pleased with her) at dawn seated in her place of prayer, and returned near midday to find her still seated. He asked, “Have you remained as I left you?” She said yes. He ﷺ said: “I have said, after leaving you, four words three times — if weighed against all you have said since this morning, they would outweigh them: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ رِضَا نَفْسِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ زِنَةَ عَرْشِهِ، سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ مِدَادَ كَلِمَاتِهِ — Glory be to Allah as much as the number of His creation, to the contentment of His Self, by the weight of His Throne, and by the ink of His words.” (Recorded by Muslim.)

And he ﷺ taught Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ the same lesson, finding a woman counting tasbīḥ on pebbles: “Shall I tell you what is easier for you than this, or better? سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ عَدَدَ مَا خَلَقَ فِي السَّمَاء…” (al-Tirmidhī, ḥasan; ṣaḥḥaḥahu al-Ḍiyāʾ). Two reports, one point: a few words that bind the tasbīḥ to the infinity of what is praised outweigh a long morning of counted, unmeasured remembrance.

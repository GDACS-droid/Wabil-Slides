Nazaghāt al-Shayṭān

He Does Not Care Which Way You Fall

Allah commands nothing, says Ibn al-Qayyim, without the Shayṭān having two pulls toward it: إِمَّا تَقْصِيرٌ وَتَفْرِيطٌ، وَإِمَّا إِفْرَاطٌ وَغُلُوٌّ — either falling short, or going to excess. He does not care which of the two he wins from you.
  • If he finds you slack, he holds you back with laziness, easy excuses, and false hopes — until you abandon the command entirely.
  • If he finds you earnest and cannot slow you, he urges you past the mark: this is not enough for you, your aspiration is higher — until you go to excess and overstep the straight path.
  • His one aim with both is to push them off the road: one by never reaching it, the other by overshooting it.

Nothing saves a person from this but firm knowledge, faith, the strength to fight him, and holding to the middle. وَاللَّهُ المُسْتَعَان.

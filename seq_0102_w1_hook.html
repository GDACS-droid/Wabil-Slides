Before we open the book

Why begin with the heart?

We are about to spend our weeks on a single short letter that Ibn al-Qayyim wrote to a friend — on dhikr and the life of the heart.

The wager of the whole course

Ibn al-Qayyim will say it plainly near the end of this letter: deeds are ranked before Allah بِتَفَاضُلِ مَا فِي القُلُوبِ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ وَالإِخْلَاصِ وَالمَحَبَّةِ — by the faith, sincerity, and love in the hearts that produce them. So we begin where he begins: with the heart that is alive, and the remembrance that keeps it so.

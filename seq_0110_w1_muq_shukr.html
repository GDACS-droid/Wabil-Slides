The Muqaddima

Gratitude Is Built on Three Pillars

  • Inwardly — acknowledge it (الاعْتِرَافُ بِهَا بَاطِنًا): the heart confesses that this blessing is from Allah alone — its giver (وَلِيّ), the One who handed it over — and not from one's own cleverness or deserving.
  • Outwardly — speak of it (التَّحَدُّثُ بِهَا ظَاهِرًا): the tongue names the favor and praises its Giver, as the Qurʾān commands: وَأَمَّا بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ فَحَدِّثْ (al-Ḍuḥā 93:11).
  • In action — spend it where He loves (تَصْرِيفُهَا فِي مَرْضَاةِ وَلِيِّهَا): the limbs employ the blessing in the obedience of the One who gave it — eyes, wealth, health, and time turned back toward Him.
In practice

A man recovers from a serious illness. Inward شُكْر: he knows in his chest it was Allah's mercy, not merely a good doctor. Outward شُكْر: he says الْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰهِ and tells of it as a favor, not a triumph. شُكْر of the limbs: with his returned strength he stands for تَهَجُّد and walks to the مَسْجِد — the very health that was given, spent back where its Giver loves. Drop the third pillar and you have a thanks the body never confirmed. (In Madārij, the author adds two more — the heart's humility before the One thanked, and loving Him for it — making five; here he gives the working three.)

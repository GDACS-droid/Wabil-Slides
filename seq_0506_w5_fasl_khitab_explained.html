Faṣl al-Khiṭāb

The Decisive Word, Unpacked

Ibn al-Qayyim calls one ḥadīth — إِنَّ عَبْدِي الَّذِي يَذْكُرُنِي وَهُوَ مُلَاقٍ قِرْنَهُ, “My servant is the one who remembers Me even as he meets his foe in combat” — the decisive word in weighing the dhākir against the mujāhid.
  • The warrior who remembers Allah is above the warrior who fights heedless.
  • The one in dhikr without battle is above the warrior heedless of Allah.
  • And so, in his words: the best of those who remember are the ones who also strive, and the best of those who strive are the ones who remember.
  • What dhikr adds to the fighting is the presence of the heart with Allah; the warrior heedless of his Lord, for all his courage, is below the one whose tongue and heart stay with His remembrance.

Anchored in the Book

Ibn al-Qayyim grounds this in وَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ — the command to remember Allah much is given in the very verse about standing firm against the enemy (al-Anfāl 8:45).

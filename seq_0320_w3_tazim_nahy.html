Taʿẓīm al-Nahy

Honouring What He Forbade

  • Fleeing the approaches of a sin, not just the sin itself: keeping far from its haunts and occasions, shunning every means that draws near it — like one who flees a place of tempting images for fear of being tried by them.
  • Leaving the harmless for fear of the harmful: أَنْ يَدَعَ مَا لَا بَأْسَ بِهِ حَذَرًا مِمَّا بِهِ البَأْسُ — letting go of an excess of the permitted, lest it carry him into the disliked or the forbidden.
  • Avoiding the one who flaunts sin, makes it look good, and calls others to it — for keeping such company invites Allah's displeasure, and no one keeps it whose heart still holds the magnitude of Allah and His sacred limits.
  • Anger for Allah's sake: that he is angered when Allah's sanctities are violated, and feels grief and brokenness in his heart when Allah is disobeyed in His own earth and he himself cannot change it.
Today

The practical shape of this is unglamorous: closing the tab before the second click, not after; declining the gathering whose whole texture is mockery of the dīn rather than trying to ride it out; feeling something turn over in the chest when a sacred limit is treated as a joke, instead of laughing along. Ibn al-Qayyim's warning is exact: the man who can sit easily where Allah is openly disobeyed has already lost some of Allah's grandeur from his heart.

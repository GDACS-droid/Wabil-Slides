It Leads to Watchfulness — and to Iḥsān

10

It bequeaths murāqaba, until it admits him into iḥsān

يُورِثُهُ الْمُرَاقَبَةَ حَتَّى يُدْخِلَهُ فِي بَابِ الْإِحْسَانِ. The fruit of constant remembrance is that the servant comes to feel himself watched — and so he worships Allah as though he sees Him. Ibn al-Qayyim states it plainly: there is no path for the one heedless of dhikr to reach the station of iḥsān — “just as the one sitting still has no way of reaching the house.” You cannot arrive at a place you are not walking toward; dhikr is the walking.

فَيَعْبُدُ اللَّهَ كَأَنَّهُ يَرَاهُ

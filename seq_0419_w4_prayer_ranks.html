The Prayer

Five Ranks of the Praying

  • The wrongdoer to himself — he cuts short its وُضُوء, its times, its limits and pillars. He is punished.
  • The one who keeps its times, limits, pillars and وُضُوء — but loses the struggle against وَسْوَسَة, and is carried off by stray thoughts. He is held to account.
  • The one who guards its limits and pillars and fights off the whisperings — so he is in prayer and جِهَاد at once. His sins are expiated.
  • The one who completes its rights and pillars and his heart is wholly absorbed in guarding them, his whole concern poured into establishing it as it should be. He is rewarded.
  • The one who does all that, and places his heart before his Lord — watching Him as though he sees Him, filled with His love and majesty, the whispers gone and the veils lifted. He is brought near.
Today

Most of us live between the second and third ranks: present at the تَكْبِير, then ambushed by the grocery list and the unanswered message. The مَتْن does not shame this. It maps it, and shows the door out is rank three: to fight the wandering rather than drift with it. The prayer that expiates is not the one with no whispers, but the one in which you keep turning back.

The Roving Angels

Gatherings of Dhikr Are Gatherings of the Angels

Benefit 52 — “Have you found your need?”

“Allah has angels who rove the roads seeking out the people of dhikr. When they find a folk remembering Allah, they call one another: Come — here is what you sought! — and they enfold them with their wings up to the lowest heaven. Their Lord asks them, though He knows best: What do My servants say? They say: They glorify You, magnify You, praise You, exalt You. He asks: Have they seen Me? They say: No, by Allah, they have not seen You. He says: And how if they had seen Me? They say: Had they seen You, they would have worshipped You harder, praised and exalted You more, glorified You more.” The Lord asks what they seek — Paradise — and what they flee — the Fire — though they have seen neither. Then: “I call you to witness that I have forgiven them.” An angel says: among them is so-and-so, not of them — he only came for a need. He says: They are the sitters; the one who sits with them is not made wretched.

Recorded in al-Ṣaḥīḥayn (al-Bukhārī & Muslim) from Abū Hurayra. Ibn al-Qayyim's point: of all the world's gatherings, the only one the angels claim as their own is a gathering where Allah is remembered.

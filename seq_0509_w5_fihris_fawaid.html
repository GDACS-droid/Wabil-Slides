Fawāʾid al-Dhikr

“In ذِكْر There Are About a Hundred Benefits”

وَفِي الذِّكْرِ نَحْوٌ مِنْ مِائَةِ فَائِدَةٍ
“And in the remembrance of Allah there are nearly a hundred benefits.”
Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib (opening of the enumeration)

How to read the list

These are not a pile of unrelated rewards. Read in order, they trace one ascent: ذِكْر first clears the ground (it drives off Shayṭān, lifts grief, polishes rust), then plants the inner life (love, watchfulness, nearness, knowledge), and finally gives the heart its very life and food. Tonight we cover the first twenty-five, pausing fully on the heart-reviving ones and clustering the rest. Read each not as a fact to file, but as the exact cause of a state your heart either has or is missing.

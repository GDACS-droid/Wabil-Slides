The Meadows of the Garden in This World

Fawāʾid al-dhikr
51
73

Whoever would dwell in the meadows of Janna now

“Whoever wishes to dwell in the meadows of the Garden in this world, let him settle in the gatherings of dhikr — for they are the meadows of the Garden.” Ibn al-Qayyim adds that dhikr has a delight no other act resembles: were the only reward of the rememberer the joy that comes to his heart, it would be enough — and for this the gatherings of dhikr are named the Garden's meadows.

مَنْ شَاءَ أَنْ يَسْكُنَ رِيَاضَ الجَنَّةِ فِي الدُّنْيَا فَلْيَسْتَوْطِنْ مَجَالِسَ الذِّكْرِ

Mālik ibn Dīnār

“مَا تَلَذَّذَ المُتَلَذِّذُونَ بِمِثْلِ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ — those who seek pleasure have found nothing like the remembrance of Allah.”

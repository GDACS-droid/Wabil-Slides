Benefit 57 · A Gap We Skipped

When the Poor Could Not Afford to Compete

The poor Muhājirūn come to the Prophet ﷺ

The poor among the Emigrants came and said: “Messenger of Allah, the people of wealth have gone off with the highest ranks and the lasting bliss — they pray as we pray and fast as we fast, but they have a surplus of wealth, so they make Ḥajj and ʿUmra, and give charity, and go out to fight, and we cannot.” He said: “Shall I teach you something by which you overtake those ahead of you and outstrip those behind you, and no one will be better than you except one who does the like? — You glorify, praise and magnify Allah after every prayer.” So He made الذِّكْر their substitute for the Ḥajj, ʿUmra and jihād they could not reach. When the wealthy heard, they did the same — and the poor came back saying, “our brothers have heard and done likewise!” He said: ذَلِكَ فَضْلُ اللَّهِ يُؤْتِيهِ مَنْ يَشَاء. (al-Bukhārī and Muslim, from Abū Hurayra.)
A corollary

This is the answer for everyone whose worship is fenced in by what they cannot do — the one who cannot afford Ḥajj this year, the new mother who cannot fast, the worker who cannot make the night-prayer he longs for. Ibn al-Qayyim's benefit 58 adds the reason it works: ذِكْر is the greatest aid to obedience — it makes the act beloved, light and sweet, “and experience bears witness to this.” The door the poor were given is open to every constrained heart: عَلَيْكَ بِذِكْرِ اللَّه.

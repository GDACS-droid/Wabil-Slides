The Ādāb of Duʿāʾ · 1

“This Man Has Hurried” — Begin With Praise

The ḥadīth of Faḍāla ibn ʿUbayd

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ heard a man supplicating in his prayer who had not praised Allah nor sent ṣalāt upon the Prophet. He ﷺ said: “This one has hurried.” Then he called him and said to him — or to another: “إِذَا صَلَّى أَحَدُكُمْ فَلْيَبْدَأْ بِتَحْمِيدِ رَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ وَالثَّنَاءِ عَلَيْهِ، ثُمَّ يُصَلِّي عَلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، ثُمَّ يَدْعُو بَعْدُ بِمَا شَاءَ — When one of you prays, let him begin by praising his Lord and extolling Him, then send ṣalāt upon the Prophet ﷺ, then ask after that for whatever he wishes.”

Recorded by Abū Dāwūd and al-Tirmidhī, who graded it ḥasan ṣaḥīḥ; also graded ṣaḥīḥ by Ibn Ḥibbān and al-Ḥākim. This is the master-pattern of the etiquette of asking: praise, then ṣalāt on the Prophet ﷺ, then the request.

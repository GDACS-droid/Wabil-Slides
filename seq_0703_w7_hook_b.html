A Question to Open With

Praise Is the Higher Speech

Ibn al-Qayyim opens these chapters with this puzzle and spends the week answering it: a servant so taken up with remembering his Lord that he forgets his own need is given more than the asker. The whole answer is that praise of Allah is itself the highest speech, and that it is the praise placed before the request that makes the request answered.

Today

Two believers raise their hands after Fajr. The first runs a list — the job, the diagnosis, the visa, the debt — and leaves. The second begins, “Yā Allah, You are the Most Merciful, the Provider, the One whose hand holds every affair…” and finds, by the time he reaches his need, that the need has shrunk and the heart has grown. The second has not asked less; he has asked from a higher place.

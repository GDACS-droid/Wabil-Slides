The First Kind · His Names

Praising Him by His Names and Attributes

The first kind has two faces. The first is the servant composing praise of Allah by His attributes — and this, says Ibn al-Qayyim, is what the great adhkār are made of:
سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ، وَسُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ المُلْكُ وَلَهُ الحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
“Glory be to Allah; praise be to Allah; there is no god but Allah; Allah is most great; glory and praise be to Allah; there is no god but Allah alone, no partner has He — His is the dominion and His the praise, and He is over all things powerful.”
Wording of the adhkār as gathered in the matn

The more a phrase gathers the praise and broadens it, the better it is: “Subḥāna llāhi ʿadada khalqih” outranks bare “Subḥāna llāh,” for it weighs against the count of His creation. This is the second face — measuring praise by His own boundlessness.

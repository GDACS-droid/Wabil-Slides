The Muqaddima

Is Allah Not Enough for His Servant?

أَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِكَافٍ عَبْدَهُ
“Is Allah not sufficient for His servant?”
a-laysa llāhu bi-kāfin ʿabdah
Sūrat al-Zumar 39:36

His exact measure

Whoever is a servant to Allah in both states — upholding His right in what he hates and in what he loves — is the one this āyah embraces. Then Ibn al-Qayyim draws the rule out with a precision worth memorizing: الكِفَايَةُ التَّامَّةُ مَعَ العُبُودِيَّةِ التَّامَّةِ، وَالنَّاقِصَةُ مَعَ النَّاقِصَةِ — “complete sufficiency comes with complete servanthood; deficient sufficiency, with deficient servanthood.” The promise of Allah's kifāya is not withdrawn from anyone, but you draw on it to the exact measure that you are His servant. فَمَنْ وَجَدَ خَيْرًا فَلْيَحْمَدِ اللهَ، وَمَنْ وَجَدَ غَيْرَ ذَلِكَ فَلَا يَلُومَنَّ إِلَّا نَفْسَهُ.

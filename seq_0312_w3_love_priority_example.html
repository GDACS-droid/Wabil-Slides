Istiqāmat al-Qalb

Honoured or Disgraced at the Test

“How easy in word, how hard in deed.” The collision Ibn al-Qayyim describes rarely announces itself as a grand crisis. It arrives as a small, ordinary fork where one love quietly elbows the other aside — and the heart shows which it truly seats first.
In practice

The fajr adhān sounds, and the bed is warm. In that thin moment the two loves stand side by side: the love of comfort and the love of Allah. No one is watching; nothing dramatic hangs on it; and that is exactly why it tests the real order of the heart. So too the friend whose approval you crave more than you fear displeasing your Lord; the customer you would not lose by telling the truth; the family expectation you would sooner break a sunna than disappoint. At each of these the heart answers honestly the question it cannot answer in the abstract: which love rules here? Ibn al-Qayyim's warning lands precisely on these forks — prefer the lesser love, and you will be made to taste hardship in the very thing you chose.

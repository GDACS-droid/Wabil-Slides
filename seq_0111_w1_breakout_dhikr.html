Group breakout
Before Ibn al-Qayyim defines it for us — what do we mean by dhikr, and by a living heart?
  • Is dhikr only words on the tongue, or something of the heart too? Can you have one without the other?
  • What would it feel like to have a heart that is alive versus one that is heedless? Name the difference from your own week.
  • If dhikr is “rain on the heart,” what is the “good earth” it needs in order to take root?
Ten minutes in your group. Choose one answer to bring back to the room — we will test it against the text as we go.

Fasting

Not Mere Hunger

مَنْ لَمْ يَدَعْ قَوْلَ الزُّورِ والعَمَلَ بِهِ، فَلَيْسَ لِلَّهِ حَاجَةٌ فِي أَنْ يَدَعَ طَعَامَهُ وشَرَابَهُ
“Whoever does not give up false speech and acting upon it — Allah has no need that he give up his food and drink.”
Recorded by al-Bukhārī (#5706) from Abū Hurayra رضي الله عنه

“This is the fasting that was prescribed,” says Ibn al-Qayyim, “not mere abstention from food and drink.” As food and drink cut the fast and spoil it, so do sins cut its reward and ruin its fruit, leaving its keeper like one who never fasted — as in the حَدِيث: “Many a faster gets nothing from his fast but hunger and thirst.”

A corollary

So the test of a fast is not at إِفْطَار but at the moments between: the argument you do not enter, the gossip you do not pass on, the screen you do not open, the worker you do not snap at. A Ramaḍān of perfect hunger and unguarded tongue is, in the words of the حَدِيث, a fast Allah “has no need” of.

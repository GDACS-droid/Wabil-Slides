“The servant has of his prayer only what he was conscious of in it.” Of your last five prayers, honestly, how much of each were you truly present before Him: a half, a third, a tenth? And the harder question Ibn al-Qayyim leaves us with: where is your taʿẓīm of the command thinnest — in guarding its time, inspecting its pillars, or keeping its soul alive — and when a right of it was lost, did your heart even flinch?

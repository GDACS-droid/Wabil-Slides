The Author

Sixteen Years with His Shaykh

  • He studied with many scholars of Damascus — ḥadīth, fiqh, uṣūl, and the laws of inheritance (the last from his own father).
  • The teacher who shaped him most was Ibn Taymiyya, whom he accompanied for some sixteen years, from the shaykh's return from Egypt in 712H until his death in prison in 728H.
  • He shared his teacher's trials, and was himself imprisoned in the citadel of Damascus alongside him.

Reading with the taḥqīq

A caution we will need tonight: when this letter says قَالَ شَيْخُ الإِسْلَام, the editor shows it sometimes means not Ibn Taymiyya but al-Harawī al-Anṣārī, author of Manāzil al-Sāʾirīn. A later copyist guessed wrong and “corrected” the text to Ibn Taymiyya — so we read with the taḥqīq, not from assumption.

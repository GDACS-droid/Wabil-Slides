Carry It Out the Door

What to Carry This Week

  • Dhikr is the easiest of acts and the most rewarded — and the substitute the poor were given to overtake the rich. Do not be among those who, when the covering lifts, regret leaving the cheapest treasure unspent.
  • Make the bāqiyāt ṣāliḥāt — سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَالحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ — a daily wird: each phrase plants a tree in your Garden and lays a stone against the Fire.
  • Keep لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّه for the heavy task and the dreaded door — and بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّه at the threshold of your home.
  • When the heart hardens or finds no rest, return to بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ القُلُوب — and treat it like a prescription, not a one-time calm: missed doses bring the relapse the poet warned of.
  • Remember to ripen love: “the servant keeps remembering until he loves Him” — and love, once settled, leaves no room for anything but ikhlāṣ.

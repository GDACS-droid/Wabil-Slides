The Balance of Reliance

Take the Means — Then Surrender to Allah

احْرِصْ عَلَى مَا يَنْفَعُكَ، وَاسْتَعِنْ بِاللَّهِ وَلَا تَعْجِزْ، وَإِنْ أَصَابَكَ شَيْءٌ فَلَا تَقُلْ: لَوْ أَنِّي فَعَلْتُ كَانَ كَذَا وَكَذَا، وَلَكِنْ قُلْ: قَدَرُ اللَّهِ وَمَا شَاءَ فَعَلَ، فَإِنَّ لَوْ تَفْتَحُ عَمَلَ الشَّيْطَانِ
“Be keen on what benefits you, seek Allah's help, and do not be weak. If anything befalls you, do not say: had I done such-and-such it would have been so — but say: Allah decreed, and what He willed He did. For ‘if’ opens the work of the shayṭān.”
Recorded by Muslim, from Abū Hurayra

Here is Ibn al-Qayyim's balanced definition of reliance, held in one hadith. The Prophet ﷺ commands the servant to take the means he cannot do without (“be keen, do not be weak”) — and, when the decree overtakes him, to surrender to it without the corrosive “if.” Tawakkul is neither abandoning the means nor leaning on them; it is doing one's full part, then resting the outcome with Allah.

Muḥbiṭāt al-Aʿmāl

What Cancels a Deed

Ibn al-Qayyim warns: وَمُحْبِطَاتُ الأَعْمَالِ وَمُفْسِدَاتُهَا أَكْثَرُ مِنْ أَنْ تُحْصَرَ — the things that void and corrupt deeds are too many to count. “The matter is not in the doing of the deed; the matter is in guarding the deed from what spoils and voids it.”
  • Riyāʾ — showing off: even a subtle trace of it voids the deed, and its doors are beyond counting.
  • A deed not bound to the Sunna in its form is likewise void.
  • Reminding Allah of your good deed in your heart (المَنّ), or reminding people of your charity and kindness, corrupts it.
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُبْطِلُوا صَدَقَاتِكُم بِالْمَنِّ وَالْأَذَىٰ
“O you who believe, do not nullify your charities by reminders of your generosity and by injury.”
Sūrat al-Baqara 2:264

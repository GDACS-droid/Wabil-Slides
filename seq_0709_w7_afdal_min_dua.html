Faṣl 2 · Dhikr Above Duʿāʾ

Why Remembrance Outranks Supplication

الذِّكْرُ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الدُّعَاءِ؛ لِأَنَّ الذِّكْرَ ثَنَاءٌ عَلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ بِجَمِيلِ أَوْصَافِهِ وَآلَائِهِ وَأَسْمَائِهِ، وَالدُّعَاءُ سُؤَالُ العَبْدِ حَاجَتَهُ، فَأَيْنَ هَذَا مِنْ هَذَا؟
“Dhikr is more excellent than supplication — because dhikr is praise of Allah by His beautiful attributes, His favours, and His names; while supplication is the servant asking for his need. How far is the one from the other!”
Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Wābil al-Ṣayyib

This is the answer to the opening hadith. To praise Allah is to be occupied with Him; to ask is to be occupied with one's own need. The one absorbed in His praise is given “the best of what the askers are given” — for he has reached the higher of the two stations without ever having stated a request.

The Descent of sakīna upon the Rememberer

السَّكِينَة
al-sakīna
the settling stillness Allah lets down upon the believing heart
الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَطْمَئِنُّ قُلُوبُهُم بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ ۗ أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ
“Those who believe and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah — surely in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest.”
Sūrat al-Raʿd 13:28

Everything in this week's list — the cure of the heart, the gathering of the scattered, the safety, the light — converges here. The fruit of constant dhikr is a heart that has come to rest: تَطْمَئِنُّ القُلُوب. Ibn al-Qayyim taught earlier that sakīna descends upon the assemblies of remembrance; this is where the whole book has been carrying us.

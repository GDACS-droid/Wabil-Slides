How the Heart Travels

Between Two Wings

Witnessing the favour

  • مُشَاهَدَةُ المِنَّة — seeing that every good in you is Allah's gift.
  • This gives rise to love, praise, and gratitude to the Bestower of blessings.

Inspecting the self's faults

  • مُطَالَعَةُ عَيْبِ النَّفْسِ وَالعَمَل — seeing your own faults and the flaws in your deeds.
  • This gives rise to lowliness, brokenness, and constant repentance — to see yourself as bankrupt.

A bird needs both wings

The knower travels to Allah between these two. Lose one and he is like a bird that has lost a wing — it cannot fly. Ibn al-Qayyim cites the words of the shaykh of Islām (Abū Ismāʿīl al-Harawī al-Anṣārī): “The knower travels to Allah between witnessing the favour and inspecting the fault of the self and the deed.”

Capstone practicum
Now build it. From only what the book has given us tonight, each person writes a personal daily وِرْد on one page — small enough to actually keep. A worked example: Fajr → سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ ×100 + sayyid al-istighfār; evening after ʿAṣr → the same two, turned to the evening; bedtime → submit, recite Āyat al-Kursī, sleep on the right; after each سَلَام → اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ.
  • A morning set and an evening set — choose 3–4 phrases each from Set 1 that you will say after Ṣubḥ and after ʿAṣr. Be honest about how much you will truly keep, not how much looks impressive.
  • A صَلَاة-linked set — pick at least one from Set 3 that ties to a prayer you already pray (answering the أَذَان, or اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَى ذِكْرِكَ after the سَلَام).
  • One situational دُعَاء — choose a single comprehensive supplication for when it is hard: distress, grief, رِزْق, or the firmness of the heart. Name when you will reach for it, before you need it.
Twelve minutes. Write it down; do not leave it in your head. Read your وِرْد aloud to one partner and have them check it is small enough to last a year, not a week.

Orientation

Where we're going

  • Move into Ibn al-Qayyim's fuṣūl nāfiʿa on dhikr and lay out its five kinds — His names and attributes, His command and prohibition, His blessings, His own speech (the Qurʾān), and calling upon Him.
  • Learn the two faces of remembering His names: composing praise (the great adhkār) and reporting His perfections as He and His Messenger ﷺ described them — and weigh dhikr by the One remembered.
  • Settle why dhikr outranks mere asking (الذِّكْرُ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الدُّعَاء), then work through the ādāb al-duʿāʾ one by one — each with its reason and its practice. This is the heart of the week.
  • Place recitation of the Qurʾān above dhikr in the abstract — then learn the fiqh of when the lesser act is the better act in its place.
  • Cross into tawakkul: surrender to the decree only after exhausting the lawful means — and let the comprehensive duʿās and tawba turn the heart home to Allah.

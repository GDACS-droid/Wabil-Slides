Orientation

Where we're going

  • Move into Ibn al-Qayyim's fuṣūl nāfiʿa on dhikr: its three kinds — remembering His names and attributes, His command and prohibition, and His blessings.
  • Learn why dhikr outranks mere asking (الذِّكْرُ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الدُّعَاء) — praise of Allah by His beautiful attributes is above stating a need.
  • Sit with the ādāb al-duʿāʾ — how beginning with His praise and ṣalāt on the Prophet ﷺ makes a supplication answered. This is the practical heart of the week.
  • Place recitation of the Qurʾān above dhikr in itself — yet learn the fiqh of when the lesser act is the better act.
  • Cross into tawakkul: surrender to the decree after exhausting the lawful means — and let tawba turn the heart back to Allah.

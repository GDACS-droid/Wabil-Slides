Orientation

Where we're going

  • Move into Ibn al-Qayyim's فُصُول نَافِعَة on ذِكْر and lay out its five kinds — His names and attributes, His command and prohibition, His blessings, His own speech (the Qurʾān), and calling upon Him.
  • Learn the two faces of remembering His names: composing praise (the great أَذْكَار) and reporting His perfections as He and His Messenger ﷺ described them. Weigh ذِكْر by the One remembered.
  • Settle why ذِكْر outranks mere asking (الذِّكْرُ أَفْضَلُ مِنَ الدُّعَاء), then work through the آدَاب الدُّعَاء one by one, each with its reason and its practice. This is the heart of the week.
  • Place recitation of the Qurʾān above ذِكْر in the abstract, then learn the فِقْه of when the lesser act is the better act in its place.
  • Cross into تَوَكُّل: surrender to the decree only after exhausting the lawful means, and let the comprehensive أَدْعِيَة and تَوْبَة turn the heart home to Allah.

Jawāmiʿ al-Duʿāʾ · The Best to Ask

After Certainty, Nothing Is Better Than ʿĀfiya

تَسْأَلُ اللَّهَ العَفْوَ وَالعَافِيَةَ، فَإِذَا أُعْطِيتَ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ أَفْلَحْتَ
“Ask Allah for pardon and well-being (al-ʿafw wa-l-ʿāfiya); for if you are given that, you have succeeded.”
From the ḥadīth of Anas — recorded by al-Tirmidhī

Abū Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him) reports the Prophet ﷺ saying: “No one is given, after certainty (بَعْدَ اليَقِينِ), anything better than well-being.” And ʿĀʾisha was taught: if you reach Laylat al-Qadr, say “اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ العَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي” (al-Tirmidhī, ṣaḥīḥ).

A corollary

Ibn al-Qayyim records the warning beneath this. A man prayed, “O Allah, I ask You for patience,” and the Prophet ﷺ said, “You have asked Allah for trial — ask Him for well-being.” The lesson is not to chase hardship in order to prove ourselves, but to ask the Lord, who knows our weakness better than we do, to spare us the test and grant us ʿāfiya. After a heart certain of Allah, this is the best thing a tongue can ask for.

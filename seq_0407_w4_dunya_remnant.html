Ḥiqārat al-Dunyā

Only a Sliver of the Day Remains

إِنَّهُ لَمْ يَبْقَ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا فِيمَا مَضَى إِلَّا كَمَا بَقِيَ مِنْ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا فِيمَا مَضَى مِنْهُ
“There remains of the dunyā, against all that has passed, only as much as remains of this day of yours against what has already gone of it.”
Recorded by al-Tirmidhī (#1921) and Aḥmad; al-Tirmidhī: ḥasan ṣaḥīḥ

The Prophet ﷺ said this as the sun sat on the mountaintops, near sunset. Ibn al-Qayyim tells the wise man who counsels himself to weigh exactly what he has gained from this thin remnant of all the dunyā — lest he find he is in a delusion and a tangle of dreams, having sold everlasting happiness and abiding bliss for a paltry share that is worth nothing. Had he sought Allah and the Last Abode instead, He would have given him that paltry share too — pleasant and complete.

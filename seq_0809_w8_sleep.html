Faṣl 2 · Lying Down

What the Prophet ﷺ Said at the Bed

اللَّهُمَّ أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ، وَوَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ، وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ، رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ، لَا مَلْجَأَ وَلَا مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلَّا إِلَيْكَ، آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلْتَ، وَبِنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ
“O Allah, I submit my soul to You, I turn my face to You, I entrust my affair to You, in hope of You and fear of You; there is no refuge and no escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You sent down, and in Your Prophet whom You sent.”
al-Barāʾ b. ʿĀzib — al-Bukhārī 247 & Muslim 2710

The Prophet ﷺ taught al-Barāʾ to make wuḍūʾ, lie on the right side, and say this — “and let it be the last thing you say: if you die that night, you die upon the fiṭra.” To it Ibn al-Qayyim joins the simplest of all, بِاسْمِكَ اللَّهُمَّ أَمُوتُ وَأَحْيَا (al-Bukhārī 6324, from Ḥudhayfa), and the gratitude Anas was given (Muslim 2715) — sleep entered in trust and thanks, not in distraction.

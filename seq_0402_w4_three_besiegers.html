The Struggle

Besieged from Within

Ibn al-Qayyim says the slave was tested with appetite (الشَّهْوَة), anger, and heedlessness — and with an enemy, Iblīs, who never tires of him.

How the enemy gets in

Shayṭān enters through the doors that are already in your own self and nature — he comes carrying what you love, so the nafs leans toward him. Then the three agree against the slave: shayṭān, the nafs al-ammāra, and hawā become three commanding masters, and the limbs are only a tool that obeys — going wherever it is ordered, aiming wherever it is pointed.

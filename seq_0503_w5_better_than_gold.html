Faḍl al-Dhikr

The Best and Purest of Your Deeds

أَلَا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِخَيْرِ أَعْمَالِكُمْ وَأَزْكَاهَا عِنْدَ مَلِيكِكُمْ، وَأَرْفَعِهَا فِي دَرَجَاتِكُمْ، وَخَيْرٍ لَكُمْ مِنْ إِنْفَاقِ الذَّهَبِ وَالْفِضَّةِ، وَمِنْ أَنْ تَلْقَوْا عَدُوَّكُمْ فَتَضْرِبُوا أَعْنَاقَهُمْ وَيَضْرِبُوا أَعْنَاقَكُمْ؟ قَالُوا: بَلَى يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، قَالَ: ذِكْرُ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ
“Shall I tell you of the best of your deeds, the purest with your Sovereign, the highest in your ranks — better for you than spending gold and silver, and better than meeting your enemy and striking their necks?” They said: “Yes, Messenger of Allah.” He said: “The remembrance of Allah.”
From Muʿādh ﷺ; recorded by al-Tirmidhī, Ibn Mājah, al-Ḥākim — reported through several routes; scholars differed over rafʿ and waqf

Why this matters

Here the Prophet ﷺ sets dhikr above charity and above the open battlefield. This is the seed of the famous faṣl al-khiṭāb we turn to next — how a single act of the tongue and heart can outrank the sword.

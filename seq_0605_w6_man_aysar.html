“Nothing Was Easier for Us Than Dhikr”

إِذَا انْكَشَفَ الغِطَاءُ لِلنَّاسِ يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ عَنْ ثَوَابِ أَعْمَالِهِمْ لَمْ يَرَوْا عَمَلًا أَفْضَلَ ثَوَابًا مِنَ الذِّكْرِ، فَيَتَحَسَّرُ عِنْدَ ذَلِكَ أَقْوَامٌ فَيَقُولُونَ: مَا كَانَ شَيْءٌ أَيْسَرَ عَلَيْنَا مِنَ الذِّكْرِ
“When the covering is lifted from people on the Day of Rising, over the reward of their deeds, they will see no deed greater in reward than dhikr. At that, a people will be filled with regret and say: nothing was easier for us than remembrance.”
Saying of ʿUmar mawlā Ghufra, cited by Ibn al-Qayyim (the editor notes he could not trace it with an isnād)

The regret is not over a difficulty missed, but over the easiest thing of all left undone. Ibn al-Qayyim sets this beside the image of the racers: the dust hides the swift until it settles — and then the rememberers are seen to have carried off the prize.
